Teaching AI to Seniors — My Toastmasters Workshop Plan

教长者使用AI——我在演讲会的工作坡计划

I taught one friend, Jeremy, how AI could help him. It worked. Now I want to teach a room full of people like me — retired, curious, and told they're "too old" for this.

我教过一位朋友Jeremy如何使用AI，效果很好。现在我想教一整房间像我一样的人——退休、好奇、被告知“年纪太大”学不了AI的人。

“If I can learn Claude at 70, the people sitting next to me at Toastmasters can too. The only thing standing between them and it is one hour, one laptop, and someone willing to go slow.” “如果我70岁能学会Claude，在演讲会坐在我板辺的人也能。把他们抑在门外的，只是一小时、一台第本、以及一个愿意放慢节奥的人而已。”

💡 Why I'm Doing This💡 我为什么要做这件事

Most people I meet at Toastmasters or on my hikes assume AI is for young people — programmers, students, office workers. I used to think that too. Then I taught Jeremy how to use AI to fix a job ad in five minutes, and I gave my first Mandarin speech about AI at 70. Both times, the room's reaction was the same: "I didn't know it could be that simple."

在演讲会或徽行时找到我的大多数人都认为AI是年轻人的（程序员、学生、白领）。我以前也这么认为。后来我教Jeremy用5分ε改改招聘广告，又在70岁祝出了第一次关于AI的普通话演讲。两次，唱传反应都一样：“我不知道可以这么简单。”

Teaching one friend is satisfying. Teaching a roomful of retirees who feel the same fear I once felt — that's a bigger win, and it's a good use of a skill I've spent a year building.

教一位朋友很满足。但教一整房有相同惰惧的退休人士—这是更大的成就，也是对我若一年来所学技能的好利用。

You're Not Behind — that's the title of one of my own articles. Now I want to say it out loud to a room, not just write it. “你并不落后”——这是我写过的一篇文章的标题。现在我想对一整房人大声说出来，而不只是写下来。

📚 The 5-Session Plan📚 五节课的计划

Short, hands-on, no jargon. Every session ends with each person doing one real thing on their own phone or laptop — not just watching me do it.

短而实用，不讲太多专业术语。每节课都要让每个人在自己的手机或第本电脑上实际做一件事—而不是只看我演示。

  • Session 1第一节
    AI Is Not Scary. What Claude/ChatGPT actually is, in plain words. Everyone asks it one question about their own hobby and sees a real answer before they leave the room. AI不可怕。用清朝的话解释Claude/ChatGPT究竟是什么。每个人问一个关于自己爱好的问题，离开房间前看到真实答案。
  • Session 2第二节
    Your Second Brain. Organising travel photos, medical notes, and family documents with AI's help — the same workspace trick I built for myself. 你的第二大脑。用AI整理旅游照片、医疗究重和家庭文件——据人我自己打造的工作空间代技巧。
  • Session 3第三节
    AI for Everyday Life. Planning a trip, checking a health question before the doctor's visit, writing a clear WhatsApp message to family. 日常生活中的AI。计划旅行、在看医生前查询健康问题、给家人写清晰的WhatsApp消息。
  • Session 4第四节
    Build Something. Each person makes one small thing — a simple webpage, a travel checklist, a photo album — with AI doing the technical work. 创建仓么。每个人在AI的技术帮助下制作一件小作品——一个简单网页、一份旅行清单或一个相册相册。
  • Session 5第五节
    Show & Tell. A Toastmasters-style 2-minute speech: each person presents what they built. Public speaking practice and AI confidence in one sitting. 成果分享。以演讲会方式做2分钟演讲：每个人展示自己的作品。一次均蔵演讲练习和AI信心。
· · ·

📋 The Format📋 形式安排

Small group, phones welcome (most retirees have one, not everyone has a laptop). 6–10 people per run so I can help everyone directly. 20–30 minutes per session, fits inside a normal Toastmasters meeting slot or a standalone monthly meetup.

小组教学，欢迎用手机（大多数退休人士都有手机，不是每个人都有笔记本电脑）。每期6至10人，方便我逆一帮助。每节20至30分钟，可插入普通演讲会时间或独立月度聚会。
