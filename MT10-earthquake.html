Japan Seismic Situation Overview日本地震现状概述
Japan sits on the Pacific "Ring of Fire" and experiences around 1,500 earthquakes annually. The JMA (Japan Meteorological Agency) monitors all seismic activity 24/7. Both Tokyo and Hokkaido/Niseko have active fault systems — but Japan's infrastructure and emergency response is world-class. 日本位于太平洋"火圈"上，每年约发生1,500次地震。日本气象厅（JMA）全天候监测所有地震活动。东京和北海道/二世谷均有活动断层，但日本的基础设施和紧急响应体系属世界一流水平。
MODERATE ALERT — Stay Informed中度警报 — 保持关注
Noto Peninsula — Ongoing能登半岛 — 持续活动
Noto Peninsula Aftershocks (Ishikawa)能登半岛余震（石川县）
Following the M7.6 earthquake on Jan 1, 2024, the Noto Peninsula continues to experience aftershocks. Ishikawa Prefecture is NOT on Paul's itinerary — Tokyo and Niseko/Hokkaido are at safe distance from this zone. 继2024年1月1日M7.6级地震后，能登半岛持续有余震。石川县不在Paul的行程范围内 — 东京和二世谷/北海道距该区域较远。

Tokyo Risk东京风险
Tokyo — Sagami Trough Fault Zone东京 — 相模海槽断层带
Tokyo sits near the Sagami Trough and the junction of three tectonic plates. Experts estimate a 70% probability of a major earthquake (M7+) in the Tokyo region within 30 years. Short-stay visitors face very low probability during a 5-day window. All major buildings are built to strict seismic codes. 东京位于相模海槽附近，处于三大板块交汇处。专家估计30年内东京发生7级以上强震的概率为70%。短期访客在5天内遭遇的概率极低。所有主要建筑均按严格抗震标准建造。
Long-term risk / Low short-visit risk长期风险 / 短期访问风险低
Niseko / Hokkaido二世谷 / 北海道
Niseko — Mt. Yotei & Hokkaido Faults二世谷 — 羊蹄山与北海道断层
Niseko is located near Mt. Yotei (dormant volcano — last eruption ~7,800 years ago). The 2018 Hokkaido Eastern Iburi M6.7 caused landslides in eastern Hokkaido. Niseko is in western Hokkaido and generally less affected. Low volcanic and seismic risk for your dates. 二世谷位于羊蹄山（休眠火山，上次喷发约7,800年前）附近。2018年北海道东部胆振M6.7级地震造成东部北海道山体滑坡。二世谷位于北海道西部，受影响较小。你出行期间火山和地震风险较低。
LOW RISK for your dates你出行期间 — 低风险
Tsunami Awareness海啸意识
Coastal Awareness — Hokkaido海岸线意识 — 北海道
Niseko is inland (~30km from coast) — no direct tsunami risk. If near the coast, always follow JMA tsunami alerts immediately. Japan's sirens and J-Alert system give early warning. 二世谷位于内陆（距海岸约30公里）— 无直接海啸风险。若在海岸附近，请立即遵从JMA海啸警报。日本的警报和J-Alert系统可提前预警。
Inland — Safe from tsunamis内陆 — 海啸安全

During an Earthquake地震发生时
▼
1
DROP趴下 — Get on your hands and knees immediately立即双手双膝着地
2
COVER掩护 — Cover head and neck under a sturdy table or against an interior wall在坚固桌子下或内墙旁保护头颈部
3
HOLD ON抓紧 — Stay in place until shaking stops. Do NOT run outside保持不动直到震动停止。切勿跑向室外
4
Away from windows远离窗户 — Glass shatters. Stay away from all windows and external walls玻璃会碎裂。远离所有窗户和外墙
5
In bed在床上 — Stay there. Cover your head with a pillow留在床上，用枕头保护头部
⛔ Do NOT: Use elevators · Stand in doorways · Run outside during shaking · Light matches (gas leaks) ⛔ 禁止：使用电梯 · 站在门框里 · 震动时跑向室外 · 点火（防止燃气泄漏）

Immediately After地震结束后
▼
1
Check for injuries检查伤情 — Check yourself and Joanne first before moving先检查自己和Joanne的伤情再移动
2
Smell for gas闻气体 — If you smell gas, open windows, leave immediately, do not use switches如闻到燃气，开窗后立即离开，不要触动开关
3
Grab Go-Bag拿紧急包 — Passport, cards, phone, medication, water. Keep them together in one bag护照、卡、手机、药物、水。放在同一个袋子里
4
Open doors first先开门 — Doors can jam after quake. Open exit doors before doing anything else地震后门可能会卡住。在做任何事之前先打开出口门
5
Evacuation point疏散地点 — Ask your hotel where the designated earthquake assembly point is询问酒店指定的地震集合点在哪里
💡 Expect aftershocks for hours to days after a major quake. Stay calm — Japanese buildings are designed for this. 💡 预计主震后数小时至数天内会有余震。保持冷静 — 日本建筑就是为此设计的。
🏨
In Your Hotel Room在酒店房间
▼
  • Know your floor's fire exit了解你所在楼层的紧急出口 — Check on check-in入住时确认
  • Do NOT use lifts不要使用电梯 — Always use stairs after a quake地震后始终走楼梯
  • Lower floors = faster exit低楼层 = 更快逃出 — If possible request floors 3-7如可能，请求3-7楼的房间
  • Ask the front desk for their earthquake procedure on arrival到达时向前台询问他们的地震应对程序
✅ Japan hotels are legally required to have earthquake procedures and regular drills. Staff are trained to assist guests. ✅ 日本酒店依法必须制定地震应对程序并定期演练。员工经过专业培训，能够协助客人。
