STM01: From Failure to Success: My AI Leadership Project in Practice

Dear friends, fellow evaluators, fellow Toastmasters:

Two years ago, I set myself a goal.

A very simple goal — to use AI to help me plan my travels.

The result?

I failed.

Not because I didn't try hard enough. But because I had no real plan. I was just exploring AI, not leading a project. That was the root cause of my failure.

One month ago, I started over.

This time, I wasn't just "trying it out" — I set up a clear 30-day project: to use AI to completely transform the way I work.

My strategy was unusual. I work for one hour, then rest for four hours. Work one hour, rest four hours.

Sounds lazy, right?

But that's leadership. Because a smart leader knows how to allocate resources. AI is my most powerful resource — I let it do the work, not me.

I faced my first challenge.

During the project, I decided to do something ambitious — connect AI directly to my IBKR brokerage account, so AI could analyse my portfolio in real time.

I started installing the IBKR API. First attempt — failed. Second attempt — failed again. Once, twice, three times… I failed ten times.

After the tenth failure, I told myself: forget it, give up. This task is too hard.

I wanted to give up, but AI asked me not to give up.

I kept asking AI, kept adjusting, and tried one more time.

Finally — I succeeded.

At that moment, I thought of the most important leadership lesson: not every challenge is solved on the first, second, or third try. A good leader, at the moment when they most want to give up, chooses to try one more time.

Project Results — Five Breakthroughs

First, travel planning. I used AI to plan a self-drive trip through Northern and Southern Xinjiang. AI told me: book the hotels in Kanas early. I did. And the result? Tourists who didn't have AI guidance arrived only to find — no rooms available. That's the power of data-driven decisions.

Second, I used AI to write a travel ebook. Before AI, this dream was simply impossible.

Third, I uploaded all my stocks and options into AI and had it analyse my portfolio — like having a personal financial advisor available 24/7.

Fourth, AI helped me organise my articles, making my writing clearer and more logical.

Fifth, work that used to take four hours, AI can now finish in minutes.

Five breakthroughs. One month. That's the difference between a planned project and random exploration.

The Leadership Multiplier Effect

A friend of mine who works in software development told me: before, it took 10 people four months to finish one project. Now, just 5 people can complete 3 projects in 3 months.

That's the leadership multiplier effect that AI creates.

Say we have the same IQ, but I spend $20 a month on Claude AI — I can build websites, plan trips, analyse data. Today, this 70-year-old adventurer can do all of this, because I learned to lead this tool, not be led by it.

But I also see the danger.

If too many people let AI replace their thinking, we'll see young people losing cognitive ability — just like how 80% of drivers, after relying on GPS, can no longer drive without it and easily get lost.

A good leader never hands over all their judgment to a tool.

From failure to success, I did just one thing.

Two years ago, I failed because I wasn't leading the project.

One month ago, I succeeded because I made one decision — I lead AI, not the other way around.

Today, even this speech was prepared with AI's help. But the person standing here is me. The one telling this story is me. Every decision made is mine.

My final words: Use AI to help you, but let yourself lead the AI.

Thank you.
