  • Put side (195/190): at $0 both puts are deep ITM (delta ≈ 1, ~zero time value left), so the spread is already worth its full $5.00 width (−$500) even with 35 DTE remaining — no cushion from time left on the clock.认沽腿（195/190）：股价为$0时两腿均深度价内（delta≈1，几乎无时间价值），价差已达到其最大宽度$5.00（−$500）——即使还剩35天到期，也没有时间价值缓冲。
  • Call side (230/235): at $0 both calls are worthless — nothing owed to close that side.认购腿（230/235）：股价为$0时两腿均一文不值——平仓这一边无需支付。

❓ Q: At what % should I close a condor to minimize loss?问：铁鹰应在差幅跌到多少%时平仓以减少亏损？▸

  • An Iron Condor's max loss is already hard-capped by the long (bought) strikes — the loss can never exceed wing width minus credit received, no matter how far the underlying moves. So closing early can't reduce the loss below that cap; the real decisions are locking in profit before it erodes, and avoiding early-assignment risk on the put side (real consequences: assignment, margin), which closing early does address.铁鹰的最大亏损本来就被买入（长）行权价硬性封顶——无论标的走多远，亏损都不会超过翼宽减去已收权利金。所以提早平仓无法把亏损降到该上限之下；真正要考虑的是在缓冲被侵蚀前先锁定盈利，以及规避认沽一侧的提前被行权风险（有实际后果：被行权、保证金问题）——这两点提早平仓确实能解决。
  • Use the same traffic-light bands as the Put Variance column above (and the Cut-Loss tab): 🟢 8%+ comfortable · 🟡 3–8% getting close · 🔴 <3% danger zone. Trigger point: close or roll once Put Variance drops toward 3%, or definitely once share price actually touches the short put strike (B) — cushion of 0%.沿用上方「认沽差幅」栏与「止损」页签相同的三色区间：🟢 8%以上 安全 · 🟡 3–8% 渐渐接近 · 🔴 低于3% 危险区。触发点：认沽差幅跌向3%时即应平仓或展期，若股价已实际触及卖出认沽行权价（B）——缓冲归零——则必须平仓。

❓ Q: Why is 11% cushion considered Safe but 4% is considered Tight (not safe)?问：为什么11%的缓冲算「安全」，而4%却算「偏紧」（不安全）？▸

  • The 5% line isn't arbitrary math — it's a rule-of-thumb buffer for "how much room is left before a single bad trading day matters."5%这条界线并非随意订的算式——它是一个经验法则缓冲值，衡量「在一个波动剧烈的交易日发生之前，还剩多少空间」。
  • 11% cushion = the stock would need to drop over a tenth of its value just to reach your short put strike. That's well beyond a normal day's move — even a rough day (2–3% down) leaves you nowhere near the strike. Plenty of room to react before it becomes urgent.11%缓冲 = 股价需要下跌超过十分之一才会碰到你卖出的认沽行权价。这远超一般单日波动幅度——即使是较差的一天（下跌2–3%），也离行权价还很远。有充足时间可以应对，不急迫。
  • 4% cushion = the stock is already close enough that one unusually volatile day — a bad macro headline, a semiconductor selloff, an earnings scare in a related name — could realistically erase that gap and put price right at (or through) your strike. It's not "unsafe" in the sense that something's already wrong; it's a heads-up that there's no longer much margin for error, so it's worth checking daily instead of glancing occasionally.4%缓冲 = 股价已经足够接近，只要出现一个异常剧烈波动的交易日——例如宏观利空消息、半导体板块抛售、相关个股财报惊吓——就可能真的把这个差距抹平，让股价直接触及（或跌破）你的行权价。这并不代表现在已经出问题；而是提醒你容错空间已经不多，值得每天留意，而不是偶尔瞄一眼就好。
  • Where the 5% cutoff itself comes from: it roughly matches what a single elevated-volatility day looks like for stocks like NVDA/QQQ/SPY (a few percent), so under 5% means "a single bad day could close the gap," and over 5% means "it would take more than one bad day." This is also consistent with how this was first flagged back on 17 Jul, when NVDA and QQQ sat at ~4% and ~2.25% variance and got called out as the ones to watch.5%这个门槛本身的由来：它大致对应NVDA/QQQ/SPY这类股票单日剧烈波动的典型幅度（约几个百分点）——因此低于5%代表「一个坏日子就可能补平差距」，高于5%则代表「需要不止一个坏日子」。这也与7月17日首次标记此现象的情况一致，当时NVDA和QQQ的差幅分别约为4%和2.25%，被列为需要留意的对象。

❓ Q: My Max Loss is bigger than my Max Return — is that ok?问：我的最大亏损比最大盈利还大——这样正常吗？▸

  • Depends which price the numbers came from. A broker order ticket often defaults to showing worst-case pricing (filling at the Ask instead of the middle of the bid/ask). If that shows a negative Max Return, the order is genuinely broken — you'd lose money even if the stock lands perfectly between your strikes at expiry. Don't submit at that price; use a Limit order near the mid instead.要看这数字是用哪个价格算出来的。券商下单界面常会默认显示最差情况的价格（以卖价Ask成交，而非买卖价中间价）。若因此显示负数的最大盈利，代表这笔单确实有问题——即使到期时股价完美落在两个行权价之间，你仍会亏钱。此时不要以该价格下单，应改用接近中间价的限价单（Limit）。
  • But once priced correctly (near mid), Max Loss > Max Return is completely normal for every Iron Condor — including all of Paul's positions, e.g. SPY: Max Loss $647 vs Max Return $353 (1.8×); XLE at target price: Max Loss ~$135 vs Max Return ~$65 (2.1×).但只要价格算对（接近中间价），最大亏损大于最大盈利对任何铁鹰期权来说都完全正常——包括Paul目前所有持仓，例如SPY：最大亏损$647 vs 最大盈利$353（1.8倍）；XLE以目标价成交：最大亏损约$135 vs 最大盈利约$65（2.1倍）。
  • Why: an Iron Condor sells premium/insurance — you collect a small, capped credit up front (your max win), and if price breaks through a strike you owe the wing width minus that credit (your max loss). The edge isn't win-size vs loss-size, it's probability: strikes are picked with enough cushion (e.g. XLE's ~8.8%/6.4%) that the stock is expected to stay between them most of the time, so you collect the smaller win far more often than you take the bigger loss.原因：铁鹰期权本质是卖出权利金/保险——你先收取一笔小额、有上限的权利金（最大盈利），若股价突破某一侧行权价，你就要赔付翼宽减去已收权利金的差额（最大亏损）。这个策略的优势不在于「赢的金额比输的金额大」，而在于概率：行权价的选择留有足够缓冲（例如XLE约8.8%/6.4%），使股价多数时间预期会停留在两者之间，因此你多数时候赚到较小的盈利，只有少数时候才会承受较大的亏损。
  • Rule of thumb: a healthy credit is roughly 30–50% of the wing width (e.g. $65 credit on a $200-wide XLE spread = 32.5%). 🧮 Q: What does the "Net Δ" number mean?问：「净Delta」这个数字是什么意思？▸

  • Net Δ measures how much your position's value moves when the stock price moves $1. NVDA: +0.04 means if NVDA rises $1, the position gains about $0.04 (roughly $4 per $100 stock move, for 1 contract).净Delta衡量股价每变动$1，你的持仓价值会跟着变动多少。NVDA：+0.04代表NVDA若上涨$1，持仓约赚$0.04（1张合约下，股价每涨$100约赚$4）。
  • It's close to zero on purpose — an Iron Condor is built to be "delta-neutral": you don't need the stock to go up or down, you just need it to stay between your two short strikes. Near-zero = balanced, working as designed.数字接近零是刻意设计的——铁鹰策略追求「delta中性」：不需要股价上涨或下跌，只需要它停留在两个卖出行使价之间即可。接近零代表持仓平衡，运作正常。
  • If it drifts toward ±0.30–0.50, the stock has moved close to one of your short strikes — that side is starting to act like owning (or shorting) the stock outright, and it's worth a closer look at the Cushion/Variance numbers on the Positions tab.若数字往±0.30–0.50靠近，代表股价已接近某一侧的卖出行使价——该侧开始表现得像直接持有（或做空）股票，此时该多留意「持仓」页签上的缓冲/差幅数字。
