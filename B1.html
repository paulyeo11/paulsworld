📚 Book1: The Power of Now

by Eckhart Tolle
A Guide to Spiritual Enlightenment
📖 Book: The Power of Now
✍️ Author: Eckhart Tolle
📅 First Published: 1997
📚 Genre: Spirituality, Self-Help, Personal Growth

Why I Picked Up This Book

At 70, retired and with more time to reflect, I found myself thinking a lot about the past — old regrets, missed chances — and worrying about the future. A friend recommended The Power of Now, saying it had changed the way he looked at life. I was curious, so I gave it a try. I'm glad I did.

What the Book Is About

Eckhart Tolle's main message is simple but powerful: life only happens in the present moment. The past is just a memory. The future is just a thought. The only place where life is actually being lived is right here, right now.

He explains that most of our suffering comes from our minds — constantly replaying the past or imagining the future. By learning to be fully present, we can find a deep sense of peace that doesn't depend on circumstances.

"Realize deeply that the present moment is all you ever have."
— Eckhart Tolle

3 Big Lessons I Took Away

1. Stop identifying with your thinking mind.

Tolle says we are not our thoughts. The voice in our head that worries, judges, and complains is not who we really are. Once I started observing my thoughts instead of being swept away by them, I felt lighter.

2. Pain comes from resisting what is.

When something happens that I don't like — bad weather on a travel day, a delayed flight, an argument — my suffering doesn't come from the event itself. It comes from my resistance to it. Accepting "this is how it is right now" doesn't mean giving up. It means stopping the inner war.

3. The present moment is the only place real life happens.

I've travelled to over 38 countries, but sometimes I realized I was so busy taking photos or planning the next stop that I missed actually being there. Now I try to pause — feel the air, hear the sounds, just be in the place. Travel has become richer.

How This Book Changed Me

  • I worry less about things I cannot control.
  • I enjoy small daily moments more — a good cup of coffee, a chat with Joanne, a sunset walk.
  • I sleep better because my mind isn't racing.
  • I approach learning AI and new technology with curiosity, not anxiety.
  • I feel grateful for retirement, instead of restless about "what's next."

Who Should Read This Book?

Anyone who feels stressed, anxious, or stuck in their thoughts. Especially helpful for people in retirement or going through a major life transition. It's not a light read — some parts feel repetitive or very spiritual — but the core message is worth the effort.

My Honest Take

This is not a quick-fix book. You won't read it once and become enlightened. But if you read it slowly, take notes, and try to practice what it teaches, it can quietly change how you experience daily life. For me, it was a turning point.
