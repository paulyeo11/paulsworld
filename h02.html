H2: My Cholesterol Journey — Numbers Don't Lie

Tracking, Managing & Winning the Cholesterol Battle

Why I Started Tracking

At 70, my doctor told me my LDL was creeping up. Instead of just taking medication without understanding it, I decided to track everything — food, exercise, readings — and really understand what was happening in my body.

This article is the honest story of that journey: the numbers, the changes I made, and what I learned.

What Is Cholesterol?

Cholesterol travels in the blood in tiny packages called lipoproteins. There are two main types:

LDL (Bad Cholesterol) Builds up in artery walls. Below 2.6 mmol/L is ideal for most adults.
HDL (Good Cholesterol) Carries cholesterol away from arteries. Above 1.0 mmol/L for men is healthy.
Triglycerides A type of fat in the blood. Below 1.7 mmol/L is normal.

What Changed My Numbers

I didn't just rely on medication. I made real lifestyle changes that moved the needle significantly.

Diet
Cut out coconut milk, reduced red meat, added oats and nuts daily
Exercise
30 minutes brisk walking 5 times a week, added swimming twice a week
Supplements
Fish oil (Omega-3), plant sterols, and red yeast rice under doctor supervision
Stress
Reduced stress through morning devotion, afternoon walks, and less screen time

My Advice to You

If you are over 60, please get your cholesterol checked at least once a year. The earlier you catch it, the easier it is to manage. Don't be afraid of the numbers — they are just information that helps you take action.

Work with your doctor, make small sustainable changes, and track your progress. It works — my numbers prove it.
