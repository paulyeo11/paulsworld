The BKE Right Turn
My Solo Hike to the Reservoir向右转
我独自徒步至水库

By Paul Yeo  ·  22 June 2026  ·  Singapore 杨汉忠  ·  2026年6月22日  ·  新加坡

Every hike teaches you something new. Last time on the BKE, I went left — and found the secret trail to Upper Peirce Reservoir. This time, I turned right. A different direction. A different reservoir. The same peace. 每次徒步都让我有新收获。上次在BKE，我向左走——找到了通往上皮尔斯水库的秘密小径。这次，我向右转。不同的方向，不同的水库，同样的宁静。

Why Turn Right Today?为何今天向右转？

On 22 June 2026, I laced up my shoes at the usual hour and headed out to the open grassy field near my home — the same starting point as always. The morning was bright, clouds building slowly to the east, just enough warmth to make you feel alive without making you sweat before you even start. 2026年6月22日，我在惯常的时间穿好鞋，走向家附近那片开阔的草地——一如既往的起点。清晨阳光明媚，东边云朵缓缓聚集，温度恰到好处，让人神清气爽，却又不至于出发前就已大汗淋漓。

I have walked the BKE left-turn route many times now — through Zhenghua Park, through the Belukar Trail, out onto the expressway shoulder, and east into the forest toward Upper Peirce Reservoir. I know every white tree marker along that path. I know where the fallen logs are, where the mud collects after rain, where the snail eggs hang above the waterline. 我已经多次走过BKE向左的路线——穿过正华公园，穿过贝鲁卡小径，走上高速公路路肩，向东进入森林，前往上皮尔斯水库。那条路上的每个白色树标我都了熟于心。我知道倒下的大树在哪里，雨后哪里积水，水面上方哪里挂着蜗牛卵。

Today I wanted something different. At the BKE, I turned right. 今天我想换个口味。到了BKE，我向右转。

The Starting Field出发草地

The open field at the start of every hike is one of my favourite places. It looks like nothing — just grass, a concrete path, some trees. But I have stood here so many mornings now that it feels like the doorway into a different world. On one side, the HDB blocks, the roads, the ordinary life of Singapore. On the other side, the trail, the forest, and whatever the morning has in store. 每次徒步开始时的那片开阔草地，是我最喜欢的地方之一。看起来平淡无奇——只是草地、一条水泥小路、几棵树。但我已经在这里驻足了无数个清晨，它仿佛成了通往另一个世界的门口。一边是组屋、道路和新加坡的日常生活，另一边是小径、森林，还有这个清晨将要带来的一切。

"Every hike begins here — in the open, before the forest swallows you whole. I always take a moment to breathe. Then I go.""每次徒步都从这里开始——在开阔地带，在森林将你完全吞噬之前。我总会停下来深呼吸片刻，然后出发。"

The Right Turn向右转

The path through Zhenghua Park is familiar. Past the old mossy signboard, through the Belukar Trail, the trees closing in on both sides, the city noise fading behind you. Then the gate, the road, and the BKE shoulder — that narrow strip between the expressway and the jungle that almost nobody walks on. 穿越正华公园的路我早已熟悉。经过那块长满苔藓的旧指示牌，穿过贝鲁卡小径，两侧树木收拢，城市的喧嚣渐渐消散在身后。然后是铁门、道路和BKE路肩——那条几乎无人行走的、夹在高速公路和丛林之间的狭窄地带。

In the past, I always turned left — north along the BKE, toward the forest gap that leads to Upper Peirce. Today: right. South. A new direction with a new destination waiting at the end of it. 以前，我总是向左转——沿BKE向北，朝着那个通往上皮尔斯的森林缺口前进。今天：向右。向南。一个新方向，终点等待着我去探索。

What I found on the right side of the BKE was quieter, greener, and in some ways even more beautiful. The trail opened into forest, the forest opened into water, and the water opened into the kind of silence you can only find when you are genuinely alone in nature. BKE右侧的世界更加静谧、更加翠绿，在某些方面甚至更加美丽。小径通向森林，森林通向水面，水面通向只有在大自然中真正独处时才能感受到的那种寂静。

While Everyone Else Is in the Jam当所有人都堵在路上

Near the BKE verge I stopped and looked out at the road. Bumper to bumper. The Monday morning queue, barely moving — everyone rushing to offices, meetings, email inboxes. I watched it for a minute or two from my side of the fence. Then I turned back into the forest, and the sound of traffic faded behind me in seconds. 在BKE路肩附近，我停下来望向公路。车头紧贴车尾，周一早高峰，车流几乎一动不动——每个人都赶着去办公室、开会、处理邮件。我在围栏这边看了一两分钟，然后转身走回森林，几秒钟内，车声便消失在身后。

"They were heading to work. I was heading to the reservoir. I know which one I prefer.""他们赶去上班。我赶去水库。我知道我更喜欢哪一个。"

The Entry Point — I Turn Right入口——我向右转

The entry point on the right side of the BKE is different from the one I use on the left. Here, a mossy concrete pipe marker sits half-buried in dense vegetation, a metal railing fence running beside it. There is no path, no sign. Just a gap in the overgrowth — if you know it is there. I ducked under a branch, squeezed through, and I was in. The jungle closed behind me immediately. BKE右侧的入口与左侧不同。这里，一个长满苔藓的混凝土管道标记半埋在茂密的植被中，旁边是一道金属栏杆围栏。没有小路，没有标识，只有一个植被间的缺口——如果你知道它在那里。我低头钻过一根树枝，挤身而入，丛林随即在我身后合拢。

My World. My Paradise.我的世界。我的天堂。

The moment I stepped through, everything changed. Dense canopy overhead. Shafts of morning light breaking through the leaves. The dark rich smell of jungle soil. I could still faintly see the green of the monitoring station fence through the trees behind me — but it was already far away, already belonging to another world. This is my world. No roads, no cars, no meetings. Just trees, birds, and the sound of my own footsteps on soft earth. 就在踏入的那一刻，一切都变了。头顶是密密的树冠，晨光穿透叶隙洒落。丛林土壤散发着浓郁的气息。透过身后的树木，我仍隐约能看到气象站绿色围栏的影子——但它已经很遥远，已经属于另一个世界。这是我的世界。没有道路，没有汽车，没有会议。只有树木、鸟鸣，以及自己脚踩软土的声音。

The Tree That Made Me Stop — Why?那棵让我驻足的树——为什么？

I stopped walking when I saw this tree. Its trunk was covered in dozens of small bumpy growths — clustered along the bark like little fists. I stood there and asked out loud: why? This is called cauliflory — some tropical trees grow their fruits directly on the trunk and main branches, not on the outer twigs. The reason is elegant: large fruits need large animals to disperse their seeds — bats, hornbills, civets — animals that cannot land on thin branches. So the tree brings the fruit to where they can reach it. A million years of evolution, right here on this bark. I looked at it a long time. 当我看到这棵树时，我停下了脚步。树干上布满了数十个小小的凸起——沿着树皮簇生，像一只只小拳头。我站在那里，脱口而出：为什么？这被称为茎花现象（Cauliflory）——某些热带树木直接在主干和大枝上结果，而非在细小的外枝。原因很妙：大果实需要大动物来传播种子——蝙蝠、犀鸟、果子狸——这些动物无法停在细枝上。于是树木把果实长在它们够得到的地方。数百万年的进化，就写在这片树皮上。我凝视了很久。

Every Day, Something New每天，都有新发现

Further along the trail I came across a small wooden bridge — just a few planks laid across a muddy crossing, palm fronds pressing in on both sides. I have never seen it before. I walk this forest often. And yet today, a bridge. Maybe it was always here on a path I never took. Maybe someone laid those planks last week. Either way: every single time I come into this forest, I find something I have not found before. A bridge. A tree. A butterfly on a leaf. A pool of still water caught in a root. 在小径的更深处，我发现了一座小木桥——几块木板铺在泥泞的穿越处，两侧棕榈叶向内伸展。我从未见过它。我常常走进这片森林，但今天——是一座桥。也许它一直在这里，只是我从未走过这条路。也许有人上周才把那几块板子铺上。无论如何：每次走进这片森林，我都会发现一些以前没有发现过的东西。一座桥、一棵树、一只落在叶片上的蝴蝶、一汪积在树根里的静水。

"Every day this forest shows me something I have not seen. That is why I keep coming back.""每天，这片森林都向我展示一些我从未见过的东西。这就是我一次又一次回来的原因。"

What Retirement Looks Like退休生活的模样

I am 70 years old, retired at 67, and this is what my mornings look like. Not a beach chair and a drink. Not a cruise ship. Not bingo at a community centre. This — a pair of long pants, a trail that almost nobody takes, a reservoir at the end of it, and enough time to sit and watch the water move. 我今年70岁，67岁退休，这就是我清晨的样子。不是沙滩椅和饮料，不是游轮，不是社区中心的宾果游戏。而是这个——一条长裤，一条几乎无人走过的小径，尽头的一座水库，以及足够的时间静坐观水。

Every morning, I put on my long pants and long t-shirt — my hiking uniform — and I go. Rain or shine, weekday or weekend. This is what I chose instead of staying comfortable. Comfortable is fine. But this is better. 每天清晨，我穿上长裤和长袖衬衫——我的徒步制服——然后出发。无论晴雨，无论工作日还是周末。这是我选择的，而不是安逸地待着。安逸固然不错，但这更好。

"Same BKE. Same starting field. Different turn. Different morning. That is enough — that is the whole point.""同一条BKE，同一片草地，不同的转向，不同的清晨。这已足够——这就是意义所在。"

The Monitoring Station气象监测站

At one point along the trail, I stepped into a small clearing and almost walked into it — a weather monitoring station, completely hidden from any road or path. Green metal cages, sensors, solar panels. The forest looked wild all around it, but the city had already been here. Singapore is always measuring something — the temperature, the humidity, the rainfall. Even out here, in the middle of the nature reserve, the data never stops flowing. 在小径的某处，我走进一片小空地，差点撞上它——一座气象监测站，完全隐藏在任何道路或小径之外。绿色金属笼、传感器、太阳能板。四周的森林看起来野性十足，但城市早已来过这里。新加坡总是在测量着什么——温度、湿度、降雨量。即使在自然保护区的深处，数据也从未停止流动。

I stood there for a moment and looked at it. Instruments in the middle of a jungle. A reminder that nature and civilisation in Singapore are not opposites — they live right next to each other, always have. 我在那里驻足片刻，凝视着它。丛林中央的仪器。提醒我，新加坡的自然与文明并非对立——它们一直比邻而居。

A Memory from Indonesia印尼的记忆

On the trail today, I looked down and saw them: delicate pink-and-white flowers, fallen from the trees overhead, scattered across the path like little stars. Feathery petals, soft pink tips. Perfectly still. The moment I saw them, I was somewhere else entirely. 在今天的小径上，我低头一看，发现了它们：娇嫩的粉白色花朵从头顶的树上飘落，散落在路上，如同一颗颗小星星。羽毛般的花瓣，柔软的粉色花尖，静静地躺在那里。就在那一刻，我仿佛置身于另一个地方。

I was back in Indonesia — on a forest trail, years ago — the same fallen flowers, the same heat, the same jungle quiet. The tropics speak the same language everywhere you go. Singapore and Indonesia are separated by a strait of water, but in the forest, you cannot tell the difference. The same trees. The same birds. The same flowers on the same kind of path. 我仿佛回到了印度尼西亚——多年前在一条森林小径上——同样的落花，同样的炎热，同样的丛林寂静。热带地区到处说着同一种语言。新加坡和印度尼西亚被一条海峡隔开，但在森林里，你分辨不出区别。同样的树木，同样的鸟鸣，同样的花朵散落在同样的小径上。

That is one of the things I love most about hiking: a single image can unlock a door to another country, another decade, another version of yourself. 这正是我最热爱徒步的原因之一：一幅小小的画面，就能打开通往另一个国家、另一个年代、另一个自我的大门。
