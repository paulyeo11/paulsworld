Most people open Claude, type a quick question, get a so-so answer, and decide AI is "overrated." I understand — that was me on day one too. But the people who get extraordinary results aren't smarter. They simply understand that Claude works in levels, and they keep climbing.

Let me take you up those levels, one step at a time.

Level 1

💬 Claude Chat — The Art of Asking Well💬 Claude对话——善问的艺术

Level 1 is the everyday chat box — and most people never get past it because they treat Claude like Google. Here is the most important lesson I ever learned:

"Claude is not a search engine. It is a thinking partner. It does not know your situation unless you tell it."

A search engine wants a few keywords. Claude wants the opposite — it wants context. The more of your world you hand over, the better its answer. The video puts this into a simple formula I now use without thinking:

Context → Task → Format
1

🗺️ Context

Tell Claude the background. Who you are, what you're working on, what you've already tried. "I'm a 70-year-old retiree, new to coding, building a personal website." That one line changes everything about the reply you get.

2

🎯 Task

Say exactly what you want done. Not "help with my website" but "rewrite this paragraph so it sounds warmer and simpler." Be specific. Give an example of what good looks like if you can.

3

📐 Format

Tell it how you want the answer — a bullet list, a short email, a table, three options to choose from. When you name the shape, you get something you can actually use straight away.

💡 The Habit That Changed Everything

Don't expect the perfect answer on the first try. Iterate. Read the reply, then say "more casual," "shorter," "now add a real example." A conversation beats a single question every time.

🌏 How I live this

When I write my travel stories, I never just say "write about Japan." I tell Claude the season, the little ramen shop in Sapporo, how the snow felt, the name of the friend I travelled with — then ask for a warm first-person piece. The context is what makes the story mine instead of generic.

Level 2

⚙️ Power Features — Beyond the Chat Box⚙️ 强大功能——超越对话框

This is where most people quietly fall behind, simply because nobody showed them these existed. There are three I lean on constantly.

A

📁 Projects

A dedicated space with its own memory for one ongoing job — a book you're writing, exam prep, a business plan. You load in your notes once, and every chat inside that Project already knows your world. No repeating yourself.

B

🧩 Artifacts

Claude doesn't just describe things — it builds them right there in the chat. A working calculator, a webpage mock-up, a polished document you can edit live. You watch the thing appear in front of you.

C

🧠 Extended Thinking

For harder problems, you can let Claude reason more deeply before answering. It takes a little longer and uses more effort, but the answers are noticeably more careful and accurate. I switch it on for anything to do with money or logic.

📊 How I live this

I keep separate Projects for my website, my Bible reading notes, and my trading research — each one remembers its own context so I'm never starting cold. And the first version of one of my financial dashboards was an Artifact Claude built for me in the chat, right before my eyes. I didn't write a line of it by hand.

· · ·
Level 3

🚀 Claude Code & Cowork — Claude Does Real Work🚀 Claude Code与Cowork——让Claude做真正的工作

This is the top floor, and honestly the part I'm proudest of reaching. Here Claude stops being something you only talk to and becomes something that does.

①

💻 Claude Code

Claude works directly with your real files and tools. It can build a whole website, create dashboards, organise folders, and run actual tasks on your computer — not just suggest them. This is the leap from "advice" to "done."

②

🔌 Claude Cowork & Connectors

On the paid plans you can connect Claude to your other apps so it can fetch, update and automate work across them. The chat becomes a hub that reaches out into everything else you use.

🏗️ How I live this

My website, paulsworld.vercel.app, was built with Claude Code. So were my stock-portfolio and brokerage dashboards. I describe what I want, Claude writes and edits the real files, and it goes live. I am a 70-year-old retiree who never studied programming — and I now ship working software. That still amazes me a little every time.

· · ·

✨ The Real Secret: Build a Mini Operating System✨ 真正的秘密：构建一个迷你操作系统

Here is the punchline of the whole video — the thing the top 1% understand that almost everyone misses.

"The best users don't just write better prompts. They build a little operating system inside Claude — a system builder, a workflow engine, and a thinking partner, all in one."

In other words, they stop using Claude as a vending machine for answers and start using it as a place where their work lives. One connected home where their projects, their tools, and their thinking all sit together and feed each other.

When I read that, my jaw dropped a little — because that is exactly what I had accidentally built, without ever hearing the phrase.

🌟 My "paulsworld"

I created a single workspace I call paulsworld — one folder that all my AI tools share. My website, my dashboards, my travel stories, my notes — they all live in one connected home. I even gave myself an AI "team": a small set of specialist assistants I orchestrate for different jobs. That is my mini operating system. It is the video's secret, built by a retiree who simply refused to stop tinkering.

🪜 If You're Starting Today, Here's the Climb

  1. Master Context → Task → Format. Use it for one week on every question. Watch your answers transform.
  2. Stop treating Claude like Google. Give it your situation. It can't read your mind — but it's brilliant once you let it in.
  3. Open one Project. Pick something ongoing in your life and give it a home with memory.
  4. Ask for an Artifact. "Build me a simple budget calculator." Watch it appear. That moment hooks people.
  5. Try Claude Code on one real task. A small webpage, tidying a folder. Let Claude actually do it.
  6. Build your own little world. Keep your work in one connected place. That's the 1% move — and it's open to anyone patient enough to climb.

If I Can Do This at 70, So Can You

I am not a programmer. I am not young. I am a retired man who got curious and kept climbing — one level at a time. The gap between the 99% and the 1% is not talent or age. It is simply knowing the levels exist, and being willing to take the next small step.

So open Claude today. Give it your context. Build your own little world. And one day soon you'll watch a video about the "top 1%" and quietly smile, the way I did — because you'll already be there.
