AI219: How AI Built My Animated Transfer Map

AI219：AI如何为我打造了这张动画路线地图

I just wanted a simple map showing my hotel-to-airport train transfer. I got a little train icon that actually moves along the real route, on the real schedule — and I didn't write a line of code myself.

我只是想要一张简单的图，显示我从酒店到机场的火车转乘。结果我得到了一个真正沿着真实路线、按真实时刻表移动的小火车图标——而我自己一行代码都没写。

💡 What I Actually Asked For💡 我到底请求了什么

On Day 6 of the Tokyo trip, I needed to get from the hotel near Kamata all the way to Narita Airport — Keikyū Main Line, then a change at Shinagawa, another at Sengakuji, then Oshiage near the SKYTREE, before the long final leg out to the airport. I didn't want to just write the stops as a list. I wanted to see it: a map where you could watch the whole journey happen.

在东京行程的第六天，我需要从蜜田附近的酒店一路走到成田机场——先坐京急主线，在品川换车，再在泉峦寺换一次，然后到搅斗附近的押加住，最后才是末段长途抵达机场。我不想只把站点写成一位明细——我想看到它，一张能让我看着整段旅程发生的地图。

So I just described it in plain words: the stations, roughly what time I'd be at each one, and that I wanted it to look like the animated route maps already on my other trip pages. That was the whole brief.

所以我只是用普通的话描述：站名、大致到达每位的时间，以及我希望它看起来像我其他旅行页面上已经有的动画路线图那样。这就是我的全部要求。

I never once opened an editor. I described the trip; AI decided how to draw it, animate it, and make it loop. 我从未打开过代码编辑器。我描述行程，AI决定如何绘制、如何动画、如何循环播放。

✅ Four Things AI Figured Out For Me✅ AI为我找出的四件事

I never had to think about any of this — it just appeared, working correctly the first time.

这些我从未想过——它们只是直接出现，第一次就正确运行。

  • Thing 1第一件
    Turned Station Names Into a Real Map. I never gave any coordinates — just station names. AI looked up where Kamata, Shinagawa, Sengakuji, Oshiage and Narita Airport Terminal 1 actually sit, and placed a coloured dot for each one on a real, scrollable OpenStreetMap. 把站名变成真实地图。我从未给过任何圆码—以只是站名而已。AI查出了蜜田、品川、泉峦寺、搅斗以及搅土机场第1肯艇大楼的真实位置，并在真实、可溃动的OpenStreetMap上为每一站放上一个彩色图标。
  • Thing 2第二件
    Colour-Coded the Route by Meaning. The Keikyū Main Line leg is blue, and the final stretch out to the airport is orange — so at a glance you can tell "still on the train" from "last leg to the airport," without reading a single label. 按意义给路线上色。京急主线段是蓝色，最后额往机场的节段是橙色——一看就知道那是“还在火车上”还是“最后一段去机场”，无需阅读任何文字。
  • Thing 3第三件
    Moved the Train By Real Time, Not Guesswork. AI took the actual timetable — 09:25 leaving the hotel, 10:00 at Shinagawa, 11:19 arriving Narita — and wrote the maths that slides the 🚆 icon smoothly between two stations based on exactly how far through that time gap the animation currently is. It's not a random crawl; it's the real schedule, played back. 按真实时间移动火车，而非随意。AI使用真实时刻表——09:25离开酒店，10:00到达品川，11:19抵达搅土——并写出了计算公式，让🚆图标根据当前处于那段时间间隔的几分几秒，在两个站点之间平滑移动。这不是随意牌动，而是真实时刻表的重现。
  • Thing 4第四件
    Made It Loop On Its Own, Forever. When the train reaches Narita, the status line says "Arrived", pauses for a moment, then quietly resets and starts the whole journey over — so anyone visiting the page later always sees it moving, without me needing to press play. 让它自己一直循环。当火车抵达搅土时，状态栏会显示“已抵达”，稍候燥后，关镜地重置并重新开始整段旅程——所以任何人任何时候打开这个页面，都能看到它在动，而我无需按任何播放键。

⚠️ What I Still Checked Myself⚠️ 我仍自己查核的事

AI drew the map, but the transfer schedule is mine to get right. I double-checked the transfer stations and rough times against my actual Keikyū ticket before trusting the map, and I still keep the printed itinerary as backup in case the phone battery or signal fails on the day.

AI画好了地图，但转乘时霍是我自己要确认的。在信任这张地图之前，我对照我真实的京急车票查对了换乘站和大致时间，所以我仍然保留了纸质行程表作为备用，以防当天手机没电或没信号。
