📈 The Problem With 22 Positions📈 持有22个仓位的问题

When you hold just two or three stocks, life is simple. You check the price, you either smile or wince, and you carry on. But 22 positions is a different animal entirely.

You have different sectors — tech, banking, healthcare, real estate. Different markets — US, Singapore, Hong Kong. Different currencies moving against each other. Earnings dates scattered across the calendar. And every day, something is moving for a reason you need to understand.

⚙️ My Daily AI + Tiger Workflow⚙️ 我的每日AI + Tiger工作流程

Here is exactly how I work every morning. It takes about 20 minutes and gives me complete clarity for the day.

  1. Open Tiger and screenshot my full portfolio. I tap Holdings, scroll through all 22 positions, and take a clear screenshot showing each stock, quantity, current price, and my P&L percentage.
  2. Paste the data into Claude. I describe what I see — or I type the key numbers directly into the chat. “Here are my 22 positions as of this morning. Here are the ones up or down more than 3%.”
  3. Ask Claude to explain the movers first. Which stocks jumped or fell? Why? Is it company news, sector news, or just general market mood? Claude gives me a plain-English briefing in seconds.
  4. Ask for a portfolio health check. Am I too concentrated in one sector? Is any single position now too large a % of my total? Should I trim anything?
  5. Ask one strategic question. Maybe I read a headline that worried me. Maybe I’m curious whether to add to a winning position. I bring that question to Claude and we think through it together.
  6. Make my decision and log it. If I’m going to act — buy, sell, or hold — I decide now. Then I go back to Tiger and execute. No guessing. No panic. Just a clear head.

💡 The Key Insight

I do not let AI make the final call. I use it to think more clearly. The decision stays with me — but I arrive at it with far more information than I ever had before.

💬 What I Actually Ask Claude💬 我实际上问Claude什么

Here are real examples of the prompts I use. These are not fancy. They are plain, honest questions from a retiree who just wants to understand his money.

📈 For daily movers
“My Tiger portfolio is showing these positions down more than 3% today: [list]. Can you explain in simple terms what might be driving each one? Is this sector-wide or company-specific?”
Claude gives me a clear reason for each mover — earnings miss, interest rate news, sector rotation — without me having to hunt across ten news sites.
🧮 For concentration risk
“Here are my 22 positions and their current values. Am I too concentrated in tech? Which sectors am I missing? If one sector dropped 20%, how badly would my portfolio be hurt?”
In two minutes I get a sector breakdown, a concentration risk reading, and a frank answer about my vulnerability — better than most brokerage reports I’ve seen.
💰 For earnings season
“I hold [stock name] and they report earnings next week. What are analysts expecting? What should I watch for in the results? What would make me want to trim or add?”
I go into earnings week with a prepared checklist instead of watching a price move and reacting emotionally.
🚫 For exit decisions
“I bought [stock] at $X and it’s now at $Y, a [Z]% gain. My original reason for buying was [reason]. Is that reason still valid today? Should I take profit, hold, or add?”
Claude helps me think through it systematically — not emotionally. It reminds me of my original thesis and challenges me to check if it still holds.

🤔 The Question That Saves Me Most Often

“Pretend you are a cautious financial adviser reviewing my portfolio. What three things would you flag as risks that I might be ignoring?” — This one question has helped me catch blind spots I would never have seen on my own.

🌟 What Actually Changed🌟 真正改变了什么

I want to be honest here. AI did not make me a genius investor overnight. I still have positions that go the wrong way. The market still surprises me. But three things genuinely changed.

✅ Change 1: No more panic
When a stock drops 5%, my old instinct was to panic-sell or freeze. Now I open Claude, describe what happened, and get a calm, evidence-based read on whether this is noise or something real. Nine times out of ten, it’s noise. That knowledge alone is worth everything.
✅ Change 2: I understand what I own
Embarrassingly, I used to hold stocks I could not fully explain. Now I ask Claude to describe every company I own in three plain sentences — what they do, how they make money, what could hurt them. No jargon. Just clarity. I only hold what I understand.
✅ Change 3: I have a thinking partner at 3am
Markets move while Singapore sleeps. Sometimes I wake up at 3am, check US markets, see something alarming, and my mind starts spinning. Now I open Claude on my phone, explain what I see, and get a measured, grounded perspective. My sleep has genuinely improved. That sounds small. It is not small.

🍃 One More Thing

I also asked Claude to help me build a simple watchlist brief — a template I fill in monthly to review each of my 22 positions against its original buy thesis. Keeping that discipline has stopped me holding losers too long out of stubbornness, and stopped me selling winners too early out of nerves.

🕒 If You Use Tiger (or Any Trading App) — Try This

You do not need 22 positions to benefit from this approach. Even if you hold five stocks, you can use AI to think more clearly about them. Here is where I would start:

  1. Take a screenshot of your full holdings. Describe them to Claude — name, sector, how long you’ve held it, and your reason for buying.
  2. Ask one honest question: “Am I holding any position I can’t clearly explain?” If yes, start there.
  3. Set up a monthly review. Once a month, go through each holding and ask: “Has my original reason for owning this changed?” Let Claude challenge your thinking.
  4. Before any new buy, tell Claude why. “I want to buy [X] because [reason]. What am I missing?” The pushback is almost always useful.

🔄 Options Trading on IBKR — My New Frontier🔄 在IBKR做期权交易——我的新领域

Three years ago, shortly after I retired, I started trading options on Interactive Brokers (IBKR). It was a deliberate decision — I wanted my money working harder, and options offered that possibility. But I will be honest: options are a different beast entirely compared to buying and holding stocks.

With stocks, you buy, you wait, you sell. Options have expiry dates, strike prices, premiums, Greeks — a whole language of their own. In the beginning it felt overwhelming. But that is exactly where AI changed the game for me.

📈 How AI Helps Me With Options
“I’m looking at this call option on [stock]: strike $X, expiry [date], premium $Y. The stock is currently at $Z. Explain the risk and reward in plain terms. What does the market need to do for me to profit? What do I lose if it doesn’t?”
Claude translates the numbers into a plain scenario: “If the stock rises above $X + premium by expiry, you profit. If not, your maximum loss is the premium paid.” Simple. Clear. No jargon.

One thing AI has helped me with especially is entry timing. Options are sensitive — enter too early or at the wrong price and the trade works against you even when you are right on direction. I now ask Claude to help me confirm my entry: is the premium reasonable? Is the risk/reward ratio in my favour? AI gives me a second opinion before I pull the trigger, which has stopped me from chasing bad entries.

⚖️ Risk/Reward Discipline

Before every options trade I ask Claude one question: “What is my maximum loss, and is my potential reward worth that risk?” If the answer does not satisfy me, I do not enter. That single discipline has saved me from several costly mistakes.

“Options trading is a bit complicated — unlike trading stocks. But with AI beside me, I understand what I’m getting into before I commit a single dollar.”

🍷 Tiger Brokerage — My Hong Kong Strategy🍷 老虎证券——我的香港策略

Within my Tiger portfolio, I have a focused strategy for Hong Kong stocks. Rather than spreading thin across dozens of HK names, I concentrate on two giants: Alibaba and BYD.

Alibaba is a recovering tech titan — beaten down but fundamentally strong, with massive cash flow and a dominant position in Chinese e-commerce and cloud. BYD is the world’s leading electric vehicle maker, growing fast and backed by strong domestic and export demand. Both are names I understand, follow closely, and believe in for the long term.

Alongside these two, I also hold a selection of HK dividend stocks. The goal is straightforward: earn a yield that beats what the bank pays on savings. When a quality company pays me 4–6% annually in dividends just for holding, that is money working harder than it ever would sitting idle in a deposit account.

💵 Why Dividends Beat the Bank

Singapore savings accounts pay around 2–3%. A good HK dividend stock often pays double that — and on top of any capital gain. AI helps me screen which dividend payers have sustainable payouts versus ones that look attractive but may cut their dividend at any time.

🏭 OCBC Unit Trusts — The Steady $50,000

Not everything has to be active. I have invested S$50,000 through my OCBC bank account into unit trusts — professionally managed funds that spread the money across a broad basket of assets.

This is my “set and monitor” allocation. I am not trading in and out daily. The fund managers do the heavy lifting. My role is to choose the right funds at the start, review performance periodically, and make sure the allocation still suits my goals.

Even here, AI helps. I use Claude to review fund factsheets, understand the underlying holdings, compare expense ratios, and check whether a fund’s performance is genuinely good or just riding a market wave. What used to take hours of reading I can now work through in a focused 15-minute AI conversation.

📈 My Three-Layer Approach

Between IBKR options, Tiger stocks & HK dividends, and OCBC unit trusts, I have built a three-layer investment approach: active, focused, and steady. Each layer serves a different purpose — and AI helps me stay on top of all three without it becoming a full-time job.

A Co-Pilot, Not a Crystal Ball

I want to be clear: AI cannot predict the market. Nobody can. What it can do is help you think better, panic less, and understand more — and in investing, that edge compounds over time just as surely as interest does.

I am 70 years old, self-taught, with no finance degree. But every morning I sit down with my Tiger app and my AI partner and I review 22 positions with calm and clarity. That is a privilege I never imagined having. And it costs me nothing but the discipline to ask good questions.
