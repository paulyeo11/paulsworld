H1: My Colonoscopy Journey: Better Late Than Never

Ng Teng Fong General Hospital  ·  22 May 2026黄廷方综合医院  ·  2026年5月22日

Ten Years of Procrastination — Ends Today

For the past ten years, I knew I should go for a colonoscopy. The doctors had reminded me, the health articles had nudged me, and deep down I was aware that it was something I simply needed to do — especially after turning 60. But life was busy, work was demanding, and somehow, there was always a reason to push it off.

Then I retired. Suddenly, I had no more excuses. With all the free time in the world and no schedule to hide behind, I finally made the appointment at Ng Teng Fong General Hospital. Today, 22 May 2026, was the day.

The 3-Day Preparation: A Test of Willpower

Nobody warns you just how much effort goes into preparing for a colonoscopy. The procedure itself is over in an hour — the prep work takes three whole days.

3 Days
Before
Restricted diet begins. I could eat fruits, vegetables, milk and dairy products. Fish and meat were allowed — but not the skin. All high-fibre and hard-to-digest foods were out.
Day Before
12 PM
Took 2 laxative pills to start clearing the bowel.
Day Before
6 PM
Another 2 laxative pills. The process was well and truly underway!
7 PM
Onwards
Every hour, I had to drink one litre of water mixed with one sachet of bowel-cleansing powder. This continued for several hours — the goal was to completely clear the colon so the scope could get a clean view.
💛 My Wife Joanne — the Real Hero I must give a very special thank you to my wife Joanne. She spent three hours peeling and preparing pear and apple, blending them into a fruit drink for me to mix with the cleansing powder. Because of her love and effort, the otherwise unpleasant drink became much more bearable. Thank you, Joanne — you made this so much easier for me. ❤️

Procedure Day: 22 May 2026

I arrived at Ng Teng Fong General Hospital at 7:30 AM, a little earlier than my scheduled time of 8:30 AM. Registration was smooth, and the nurses were warm and efficient. I was given a hospital gown to change into and directed to the endoscopy prep area.

Before the procedure, a nurse inserted a cannula into my right hand — a small receptacle that would allow the doctor to administer sedation medication directly into my vein, putting me into a comfortable, drowsy sleep for the scope.

⚠️ A Small Accident — Handled with Grace Just before the procedure was about to begin, Dr. Edwin Yang accidentally cut his finger whilst opening a medical instrument package. The cut on his last finger was quite deep and required attention before he could proceed. He apologised to me sincerely for the delay. I told him not to worry — accidents happen, and I was just glad he was okay. It was a small reminder that doctors are human too, and we should always treat our healthcare professionals with kindness.

Once everything was sorted, the sedation was administered. The procedure began at 8:57 AM. The scope was advanced through the entire colon to the terminal ileum. I was mostly asleep — though I did briefly stir in the middle of the procedure before drifting off again. By all accounts, I tolerated the procedure well, with no blood loss and no adverse events.

The Results: Good News, Praise the Lord!

After the procedure, Dr. Edwin briefed me on what was found during the colonoscopy. I was relieved and grateful to hear the results.

📋 Post-Procedure Findings

  • Diverticulosis of the Colon — Mild diverticulosis with a few small, scattered diverticula found in the caecum and mid sigmoid colon. No inflammation. Not serious.
  • Colon Polyp (4mm) — One small, benign-appearing polyp found in the mid transverse colon. It was successfully removed during the procedure via cold snare polypectomy. No further surgery needed.
  • Bowel Prep: Good — Boston Bowel Preparation Score of 7. The cleansing was effective, giving the doctor a clear view throughout.
  • No Follow-Up Required — The doctor confirmed there is no recommended follow-up procedure needed at this stage.

The polyp was tiny — just 4mm — and was removed completely on the spot. Catching it early means there is nothing more to worry about. This is exactly why colonoscopies matter. If left undetected, polyps can grow and, over many years, potentially become cancerous. Finding and removing it today was the best possible outcome.

I was discharged with a 3-day hospitalisation leave certificate, post-care instructions, and an appointment with the Colorectal Surgery team at NUHS to review the biopsy results by 12 June 2026.

A Message to Everyone Aged 60 and Above

If you are 60 years old or older and have not yet had a colonoscopy, I urge you — please go and do it. Yes, the three-day preparation is inconvenient. Yes, the drink is not pleasant. Yes, it costs around S$1,500. But compared to the peace of mind you gain, and compared to what can happen if a problem is left undetected, it is absolutely worth it.

I waited ten years. Don't be like me. Early detection is everything.
