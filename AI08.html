How to Learn So Fast
It's Almost Unfair

A YouTube video changed the way I think about learning — at 70, it's never too late to upgrade your brain

🎬 The Video That Changed How I Learn
Watch this — it will change the way you think about learning forever.

🧠 Why Most People Learn the Wrong Way

The video opens with a powerful truth: intelligence is no longer the real advantage in the age of AI. Any skill you have today can become outdated tomorrow. The only real edge is how fast you can learn and adapt.

The speaker grew up poor in Mumbai, struggled as a student, yet went on to graduate from MIT and become a CEO. His secret was not intelligence — it was learning how to learn faster than everyone around him.

Most people fail at learning because they try to cram information into the wrong part of the brain. Your prefrontal cortex — the CEO of your brain — burns enormous energy. It is not built for parallel processing. When you overload it with too much information at once, nothing sticks.

💡 Key Insight

Your brain burns up to 20% of your body's total fuel. Cramming overloads the prefrontal cortex — the most energy-hungry part. That's why traditional studying feels exhausting and produces poor results.

🔑 The 3C Protocol: The Secret System

The heart of the video is the 3C Protocol — a three-step learning system backed by cognitive science. It works with your brain's natural design, not against it.

Step 1

🗜️ Compress

Don't try to memorise everything. Instead, compress information into patterns and frameworks your brain can handle. Think of how chess grandmasters don't memorise every move — they recognise patterns. Simplify complex ideas into their core essence first.

Step 2

📚 Compile

Use active recall instead of passive re-reading. Make your brain work to generate the answer — this is called the Generation Effect. The harder you work to retrieve information, the deeper it embeds into long-term memory. Test yourself, don't just review.

Step 3

🔒 Consolidate

Rest is not laziness — it is when your brain actually locks in what you learned. Sleep and breaks allow memory consolidation. Spacing your learning over time (spaced repetition) beats cramming every single time. Rest is part of learning.

💭 What This Means to Me at 70

When I watched this video, I felt both humbled and excited. Humbled because I realised I had been learning the wrong way my whole life — reading and re-reading, trying to remember everything at once. Excited because it is never too late to change.

As a 70-year-old learning AI, coding, options trading, and travel writing all at the same time, the 3C Protocol makes perfect sense to me. I don't need to memorise every command or every concept. I need to compress the key ideas, compile them through practice, and consolidate them with rest.

"The only real edge in the age of AI is not how smart you are — it's how fast you can learn."

This is especially true for seniors like me. Our brains may process information a little differently than a 25-year-old's. But the 3C Protocol is not about speed alone — it is about efficiency. Working smarter, not harder. And that is something wisdom and experience actually help with.

🛠️ How I Am Applying This to My AI Learning

Here is how I am using the 3C Protocol in my own life right now:

  1. Compress: When learning a new AI tool, I write one sentence that captures the core idea. Not pages of notes — just one sentence.
  2. Compile: I immediately try to use what I just learned. I build something, write something, or teach it to someone. Practice beats passive reading.
  3. Consolidate: I no longer feel guilty about taking breaks or sleeping. I now know rest is when the learning actually happens.
  4. Space it out: I revisit what I learned after 1 day, then 3 days, then a week. This spaced repetition locks it in permanently.
  5. Embrace struggle: When something is hard to understand, I don't give up. The struggle itself deepens the learning.

It's Never Too Late to Learn Faster

Whether you are 25 or 70, the way you learn matters more than how much you study. The 3C Protocol — Compress, Compile, Consolidate — is a gift from science to anyone willing to try. Watch the video, apply the system, and watch how fast you grow.
