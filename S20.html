The Loop and the Reservoir

A solo morning hike through Singapore’s Chestnut Nature Park — two hours, 8,500 steps, and a reservoir view that stopped me in my tracks.独自清晨徒步穿越新加坡栗自然公园——两小时、8500步，以及一处让我不由得停下脚步的水库美景。

The Walk Out the Door踏出家门

Five Minutes from Home, a World Away离家五分钟，却如另一个世界

16 June 2026  ·  8:00 AM  ·  Dairy Farm Residences → Chestnut Nature Park

I stepped out of Dairy Farm Residences at eight o'clock in the morning. The air was still cool, the light still gentle, and the day had not yet made up its mind about what it wanted to be. Five minutes of walking, and I was standing at the entrance to Chestnut Nature Park, looking at the trail map bolted to a wooden board. You Are Here — a little red marker, right in the middle of all that green.早上八点，我从 Dairy Farm Residences 走出来。空气依然清凉，阳光依然柔和，这一天还未决定它想成为什么样子。走了五分钟，我便站在了栗自然公园的入口处，望着钉在木板上的步道地图。您在这里——一个小小的红色标记，就在满眼绿色的正中央。

I have lived close to this park for a while now, and I had never done the Southern Hiking Loop. That is the thing about having a park practically on your doorstep: you tell yourself you will get to it, and then you keep getting to other things instead. Today, I finally got to it. Today I said yes.我在这附近住了一段时间，却从未走过南部登山环形道。这就是家门口有座公园的"问题"：你总告诉自己会去的，然后总有别的事情把你绊住。今天，我终于去了。今天，我说了"好"。

"Eight o'clock in the morning, five minutes from my front door, and a trail I had never walked before. Retirement has taught me that the best adventures are often hiding closest to home.""清晨八点，距我家门口五分钟路程，一条从未走过的小径。退休让我明白：最好的冒险，往往就藏在离家最近的地方。"

I was alone. No company, no earphones, no agenda except to walk the loop and see what it showed me. That is enough of a plan. At seventy, I have learned not to overcomplicate things that are already good.我是一个人。没有同伴，没有耳机，没有任何计划，只是走完这条环形道，看它会带给我什么。这就够了。七十岁的我，已经学会不去把本已美好的事情复杂化。

Into the Southern Loop进入南部环形道

A Jungle Trail That Earns Its Keep一条名副其实的丛林小道

Chestnut Nature Park  ·  Southern Hiking Loop  ·  Singapore

The Southern Hiking Loop at Chestnut Nature Park is not a stroll. It winds, it climbs, it dips, it turns back on itself through jungle that feels properly wild — not manicured, not tamed, not designed for Instagram. Real jungle, with roots across the path and branches overhead and the kind of damp, rich smell that tells you the earth is alive and working hard.栗自然公园的南部登山环形道可不是随便散散步的地方。它蜿蜒、起伏、跌宕，穿越一片真正原始的丛林——没有修剪，没有驯化，也不是为了拍照摆拍而设计的。真实的丛林，横亘路面的树根，头顶交错的枝桠，还有那股潮湿浓郁的气息，告诉你这片土地充满生机，正在努力运转。

The trail is well-marked but not crowded. On a Monday morning at eight, I had long stretches entirely to myself. Just me and the birds — and there were many birds, doing the things birds do in a Singapore forest at that hour: calling, answering, moving through the canopy in ones and twos, completely unbothered by the retired man walking below them.步道标识清晰，但并不拥挤。周一早上八点，有大段路程完全只有我一个人。只有我和鸟儿——鸟儿很多，做着它们在那个时刻应该做的事：鸣叫、应和、在树冠间三三两两地穿梭飞行，对于在它们下面行走的这个退休老人浑然不觉。

I took it at my own pace. That is the privilege of going solo — no one to slow down for, no one to keep up with. Just the trail and me. I paused when I wanted to. I looked up when something moved in the canopy. I stood still when the forest went quiet for a moment, just to hear what quiet sounds like when it is full of living things.我按自己的节奏走。这就是独自前行的特权——不必为任何人放慢脚步，也不必追赶任何人。只有小径和我。想停就停。有什么在树冠间移动，就抬头看。当森林突然安静下来，我便静静伫立，只为听一听，被生命填满的寂静究竟是什么声音。

The Southern Loop trail — earth path winding through thick jungle, cool and quiet at eight in the morning.南部环形道步道——泥土小径蜿蜒穿越茂密丛林，清晨八点，清凉而寂静。

The loop is marked in yellow on the trail map — a winding circuit through the southern section of the park. As I walked it, I kept thinking about how Singapore has managed to hold on to pockets of nature like this, right inside the city, right next to people's homes. We are lucky. We do not always act like it, but we are.环形道在步道地图上以黄色标注——一条穿越公园南部的蜿蜒回路。走着走着，我一直在想，新加坡是如何做到在城市内部、在人们家门口，保留下这样一片片自然净土的。我们很幸运。我们不总是有这样的意识，但我们确实很幸运。

The Reservoir水库

The View That Stopped Me Cold让我停下脚步的风景

Somewhere along the loop  ·  The moment that made the whole morning

And then the trees parted, and there it was.树木豁然开朗，它就在那里。

I had been walking through dense jungle for a while — head down, watching the path, counting roots and noting the way the trail turned. And then the trail curved one final time and opened onto the reservoir, and I stopped walking entirely.我在茂密的丛林中走了一段时间——低着头，盯着路，数着树根，留意着步道的转向。然后，步道最后一次转弯，豁然开朗，水库就在眼前，我停住了脚步，整个人定在那里。

The water stretched out ahead of me, wide and calm and very, very still. The trees on the far bank were reflected in it so perfectly that for a moment the whole scene seemed doubled — the real world above, and its mirror image below, both equally clear, both equally green. The morning sky, still pale at that hour, lay on top of all of it like a second ceiling. No wind. No sound except birds.水面宽阔地展开在我面前，平静，静谧，极其，极其的静。对岸的树木倒映在水中，如此完美，那一瞬间整个景象仿佛翻了倍——上方是真实的世界，下方是它的镜像，同样清晰，同样翠绿。那个时刻天色尚浅，晨空淡白，像第二层天花板覆盖在一切之上。没有风。除了鸟鸣，没有任何声音。

The reservoir — perfectly still in the morning light, the tree line reflected like a mirror on the water.水库——晨光中平静如镜，对岸树线倒映在水面上，宛如镜中。

I stood there for a long time. Not because I was tired — I was not tired — but because the view demanded it. There are moments in nature that ask you to stop and pay attention, and this was one of them. The water, the trees, the light, the silence. It was the kind of beautiful that you feel in your chest rather than just see with your eyes.我在那里站了很久。不是因为累——我并不累——而是因为眼前的风景要求我这样做。大自然有些时刻会要求你停下来，认真凝视，这就是其中之一。水、树、光、静。那是一种你用胸腔去感受、而不仅仅用眼睛去看的美。

I have travelled to a lot of places in my life. I have stood at the edges of famous lakes and famous rivers and famous coastlines. And this reservoir, tucked into a park five minutes from my house in Singapore, was as beautiful as any of them. That surprised me. It also made me very happy.我这一生去过很多地方。站在著名湖泊、著名河流、著名海岸线的边上。而这座水库，就藏在新加坡我家五分钟路程的公园里，却与它们同样美丽。这让我感到惊讶，也让我非常快乐。

"The water was perfectly still, reflecting the trees on the far bank like a mirror. I stood there for a long time, not because I needed to rest, but because some views ask you to stop. This was one of them.""水面静如明镜，将对岸的树木倒映其中。我在那里站了很久，不是因为需要休息，而是因为有些风景会要求你停下来。这就是其中之一。"

Morning light on the water — the kind of scene you do not expect to find five minutes from home.晨光照在水面上——这样的景色，你不会想到就在离家五分钟的地方。

Two Hours Well Spent两小时，不虚此行

8,500 Steps, and Feeling Strong8500步，精力充沛

End of the loop  ·  Back at the trailhead  ·  10:00 AM

I completed the full Southern Loop in two hours. When I reached the trailhead again and checked my step count, the number read 8,500 steps. That is not a bad morning's work for any age. At seventy, it feels like a statement.我用了两个小时走完了整条南部环形道。回到步道起点，查看步数，显示 8500步。对任何年龄来说，这都不是一个糟糕的上午成绩。在七十岁，这感觉像是一种宣言。

I did not feel beaten up by it. I felt the opposite — that particular kind of energy that comes from having worked your body properly in the open air. The muscles in my legs were warm and used. My breathing was steady. Everything felt right. This is what I mean when I say I feel very fit now: not young again — I know I am not young — but genuinely, measurably well. Moving well. Thinking clearly. Present in my body in a way that sitting still never gives me.我没有感到疲惫不堪，恰恰相反——是那种在户外好好活动了身体之后特有的能量感。腿部肌肉温热而充实。呼吸平稳。一切都感觉对了。这就是我所说的"现在状态很好"的含义：不是重新变年轻——我知道我不年轻——而是真实地、可感知地健康。行动自如，思维清晰，以一种静坐永远给不了我的方式，与自己的身体同在。

I retired at sixty-seven. I am seventy now. Three years into retirement, and I am doing two-hour solo jungle hikes before the morning is half done. I find this extraordinary, and I find it completely normal, in equal measure. That is the gift of doing this regularly. You stop seeing it as remarkable and start seeing it as simply what Tuesday morning looks like. Or Monday. Or whatever day it is when you step out the door and say: yes, let us go.我六十七岁退休。现在七十岁。退休三年，我在上午还没过一半的时候就完成了两小时的独自丛林徒步。我觉得这既非凡，又完全平常，二者并存。这就是坚持做一件事的馈赠。你不再觉得它了不起，而是开始把它当作周二早晨理所当然的样子。或者周一。或者无论哪一天，当你走出家门，说：好，走吧。

"8,500 steps. Two hours in the jungle. A reservoir I will not forget in a hurry. Alone with the birds and the trees and the sound of water. At seventy, that is not a small thing. That is everything.""8500步。两小时丛林行走。一座久久难忘的水库。独自与鸟儿、树木和水声为伴。在七十岁，这不是一件小事。这就是一切。"

🌿 What I Know About Chestnut Nature Park (Southern Loop)我对栗自然公园（南部环形道）的了解

  1. Go early. Eight o'clock is ideal — cool air, soft light, almost no one on the trail. The park fills up as the morning goes on.早去。八点最理想——空气清凉，光线柔和，步道上几乎没有人。随着上午时间推移，公园会越来越热闹。
  2. The loop is well-marked. The trail map at the entrance is accurate. Yellow markings on the Southern Loop. You will not get lost.标识清晰。入口处的步道地图准确可靠。南部环形道以黄色标注。你不会迷路的。
  3. Don't rush the reservoir. When the water appears, stop. Sit if there is somewhere to sit. Look at it properly. It is the best part of the whole trail.在水库前别着急。当水面出现时，停下来。如果有地方坐，就坐下来。好好看看它。这是整条步道最精彩的部分。
  4. Bring water. Two hours in a Singapore jungle, even in the morning, requires hydration. There are no vending machines inside the park.带水。在新加坡的丛林里走两个小时，即使是在早上，也需要补水。公园内没有自动售货机。
  5. Wear proper shoes. The trail has roots, mud patches, and uneven ground. Trail shoes or good running shoes, not sandals.穿合适的鞋。步道上有树根、泥泞地段和不平整的地面。穿越野鞋或专业跑鞋，不要穿凉鞋。
  6. Solo is fine. Tell someone where you are going (I sent Joanne a quick text) and take your phone. But going alone is peaceful and completely safe on this trail.独自前行没问题。告诉某人你要去哪里（我给 Joanne 发了条短信），带上手机。但独自走这条步道是宁静的，也完全安全。

What It Means这意味着什么

Retired at 67, Still Exploring at 7067岁退休，70岁仍在探索

A thought at the end of a very good morning

I walked into a park I had never properly explored, alone, at eight in the morning, at the age of seventy. I walked for two hours through real jungle. I stood beside a reservoir that was so beautiful it made me go quiet. I came home feeling better than when I left.七十岁的我，独自在清晨八点走进一座从未好好探索过的公园。在真实的丛林中走了两个小时。站在一座美得令我沉默的水库旁。回到家时，比出发时感觉更好。

This is what retirement looks like when you refuse to let it become small. Not every day is a grand adventure — some days are errands and doctors and all the ordinary furniture of life. But the days when I lace up my shoes and step out into the green — those days belong entirely to me, and they are among the best days I have had.这就是当你拒绝让退休变得渺小时，它的样子。不是每一天都是宏大的冒险——有些日子是跑腿办事、看医生，以及生活中所有平凡的日常。但那些我系上鞋带、迈入绿意的日子——那些日子完全属于我，是我拥有过的最美好的日子之一。

If you have never walked the Chestnut Nature Park Southern Loop, go. Go soon. Go alone if you can — the silence is part of what makes it special. And when you reach the reservoir, stop for a while. Let the view do what it needs to do.如果你从未走过栗自然公园的南部环形道，去吧。快去。如果可以，独自去——那份宁静，正是它特别之处的一部分。当你到达水库时，停留一会儿。让那片风景做它该做的事。

You will not regret it.你不会后悔的。
