H5: My Blood Pressure Journey — From High to Healthy

How I Brought My Blood Pressure Under Control Naturally

The Wake-Up Call

At my routine health screening in early 2025, my blood pressure reading was 158/95. The doctor said: "Paul, this is Stage 2 hypertension. We need to address this."

That was my wake-up call. I didn't want to rely solely on medication for the rest of my life. I decided to take action through lifestyle changes first, with medication as a safety net.

Understanding Blood Pressure

Normal Below 120/80 mmHg — the goal everyone should aim for.
Elevated 120–129 / below 80 — time to make lifestyle changes.
Stage 1 Hypertension 130–139 / 80–89 — medication may be considered.
Stage 2 Hypertension 140+ / 90+ — requires immediate medical attention and lifestyle changes.

My Readings Progress

📊 Blood Pressure Readings

  • Jan 2025 — 158/95 (Stage 2 — Wake-Up Call)
  • Apr 2025 — 148/90 (Improving with lifestyle changes)
  • Jul 2025 — 138/86 (Stage 1 — Medication reduced)
  • Oct 2025 — 131/83 (Nearly Normal)
  • Jan 2026 — 126/80 (Normal Range — Medication stopped)

What I Changed

Salt
Dramatically reduced salt — no more adding soy sauce, less hawker food
Walking
45 minutes brisk walking daily — most powerful change I made
Weight
Lost 4kg — even small weight loss significantly reduces blood pressure
Alcohol
Reduced alcohol to special occasions only
Stress
Daily morning devotion, afternoon walks, reduced commitments

My Message to You

High blood pressure is called the "silent killer" because it often has no obvious symptoms. Many people walk around with dangerous readings and don't know it. Please get checked.

And if your numbers are high — don't panic. Lifestyle changes really do work. My numbers went from Stage 2 to Normal in one year, primarily through walking, less salt, and stress reduction. Your body has amazing power to heal if you give it the right conditions.

💊 Note I worked closely with my doctor throughout this journey. Please do not stop medication without medical guidance. My case was managed carefully by my physician.
