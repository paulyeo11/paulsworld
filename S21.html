City Walk: From Chinatown to the Bay

A self-guided walking route through Singapore, starting at Chinatown — temples, hawker food, old shophouses, and a finish at the Merlion with Marina Bay in front of you.从牛车水出发的新加坡自导步行路线——庙宇、小販美食、老店屋，以及在滨海湾前鱼尾狮处的圆满收尾。

Start: Chinatown MRT ~4 km 1.5–2 hrs 6 Stops
The Route

Old Singapore on Foot徒步走遍老新加坡

This walk starts right at Chinatown MRT Station and threads through some of the oldest streets in Singapore — a Hindu temple, a Hokkien temple, a hawker centre, a hill of restored shophouses — before ending at the Merlion with the Marina Bay skyline laid out in front of you. Easy pace, mostly flat, plenty of places to stop for food along the way.

Point-by-point breakdown (~4km, 1.5–2 hrs):
📍 START — Chinatown MRT Station (牛车水地铁站)
Exit toward Pagoda Street. The street is lined with souvenir shops and old shophouses — a good place to get oriented.

↓ ~400m / 5 min walk
🛕 1. Sri Mariamman Temple (马里安曼兴都庙)
Singapore's oldest Hindu temple, built in 1827 — a colourful gopuram tower right in the middle of Chinatown. ~15 min visit.

⛩️ 2. Thian Hock Keng Temple (天福宫)
Singapore's oldest Hokkien temple (1839), built without nails. Quiet courtyard, intricate roof carvings. ~15–20 min.

🚶 Tips for This Walk

  1. Go in the late afternoon. Start around 4–5 PM so you finish at Merlion Park as the skyline lights come on.
  2. Wear light, breathable clothes. Singapore heat and humidity make a 4km walk warmer than it sounds — bring water.
  3. Eat at Maxwell Food Centre. Come a little hungry — it's the best food stop on the route.
  4. Shortcut option: if tired after Ann Siang Hill, take a short taxi/Grab to Merlion Park instead of walking the last two legs.
  5. Bring your phone for the views — Merlion Park facing Marina Bay Sands is one of the best photo spots in Singapore.
