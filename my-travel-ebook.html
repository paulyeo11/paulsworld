The Hard Climbs Made Me Stronger艰难的攀登让我更坚强

Three Years Alone with a Backpack — A Travel Memoir独自背包三年——旅行回忆录

by Paul Yeo杨汉忠 著
Retired at 67 · 9 countries · on foot · alone67岁退休 · 9个国家 · 徒步 · 独行
The Beginning开篇

A Man, a Backpack, and No One Waiting一个男人、一个背包，以及没有人在等待

Three years on the road  ·  Roughly age 67 to 70三年在路上  ·  大约67至70岁

When I retired at sixty-seven, I did not buy a rocking chair. I bought a backpack. Over the last three years or so I have travelled to many corners of the world — mostly on my own — sleeping in hostel bunks, eating whatever the locals eat, and walking until my legs begged me to stop. No tour guide holding a little flag. No luxury suite. Just me, a pack on my back, and a map I usually managed to misread.当我67岁退休时，我没有买摇椅。我买了一个背包。在过去三年左右的时间里，我走遍了世界的许多角落——大多是独自一人——睡在青旅的铺位上，吃当地人吃的食物，走到双腿求饶才停下。没有举着小旗的导游。没有豪华套房。只有我、背上的包，和一张我通常搞错方向的地图。

People ask me if I get lonely, travelling solo at my age. The honest answer is: rarely. A hostel is the friendliest place on earth. You arrive a stranger and by breakfast you're swapping stories with a twenty-two-year-old from Germany and a retired teacher from Argentina, all of you bent over the same bad map. Solo travel never meant alone. It meant free — free to go where I liked, stay as long as I pleased, and turn down any road that looked interesting.人们问我，独自旅行会不会孤独。诚实的答案是：很少。青旅是世上最友好的地方。你以陌生人的身份抵达，到早餐时就在和一个22岁的德国年轻人以及一位来自阿根廷的退休教师交换故事，大家都在看同一张糟糕的地图。独自旅行从不意味着孤独，而是意味着自由——随心所欲地去往任何地方，想待多久就待多久，随时可以转入任何看起来有趣的道路。

But I won't pretend it's been easy. The hiking, especially, has been hard — genuinely hard. My knees know it. My lungs know it. And that, I've come to believe, is the whole point.但我不会假装一切都很轻松。徒步尤其艰难——真的很艰难。我的膝盖知道，我的肺知道。而这，我渐渐相信，正是其意义所在。

”I don't climb because it's easy. I climb because the hard ones are the only ones that change you. Every difficult trail has handed something back to me — a little more strength than I had at the bottom.””我攀登不是因为它容易。我攀登是因为艰难的那些才是唯一能改变你的。每一条难走的山路都给了我一些回报——比出发时多一点点的力量。”

Chapter One · The Highest Climb第一章 · 最高的攀登

Nepal — The Long Walk to 4,130 Metres尼泊尔——漫长跋涉4130米

Annapurna Base Camp, the Himalayas安纳普尔纳大本营，喜马拉雅山

If you want to know what tested me most, it was the trek to Annapurna Base Camp in Nepal — 4,130 metres up into the Himalayas. Day after day of climbing, the air thinning until every breath felt rationed, my heart pounding on steps that a younger man would have skipped up without a thought. There were mornings I sat on a cold rock wondering, quietly, what on earth a man my age was doing up here.如果你问什么最考验我，那就是在尼泊尔徒步至安纳普尔纳大本营——海拔4130米深入喜马拉雅山。日复一日地攀登，空气愈来愈薄，每一口呼吸都像是配给的，心脏在年轻人会轻松跳过的台阶上怦怦直跳。有些早晨，我坐在冰冷的岩石上，静静地想：一个我这般年纪的人究竟来这里干什么。

And then I reached it. The painted sign — Annapurna Base Camp — prayer flags snapping in the wind, the great white peaks ringed all around me like a council of giants. I stood there with tears in my eyes, partly from the cold and mostly from the joy. I had not been carried up. I had walked every single step. At sixty-something, with my own two legs.然后我到达了。那块写着"安纳普尔纳大本营"的牌子，经幡在风中猎猎作响，巍峨的雪峰环绕四周，如同巨人的议会。我站在那里，热泪盈眶——一半是因为寒冷，更多是因为喜悦。我不是被人抬上来的。我是用双腿走上来的，每一步都是自己走的。六十几岁，靠自己的两条腿。

Annapurna Base Camp, Nepal — 4,130 metres. Prayer flags, thin air, and the proudest summit of my life. Namaste.尼泊尔安纳普尔纳大本营——海拔4130米。经幡、稀薄的空气，以及我此生最自豪的山顶。南无。

That climb gave me something no easy holiday ever could. It taught me that the body, even an older body, will rise to whatever you ask of it — if you ask kindly, and keep putting one foot in front of the other.那次攀登给了我任何轻松假期都无法给予的东西。它告诉我，身体——即便是年老的身体——只要你温和地要求它，并不断把一只脚迈到另一只脚前面，就能达到你所要求的一切。

Chapter Two · The End of the World第二章 · 世界尽头

Patagonia — Arms Wide Above a Turquoise Lake巴塔哥尼亚——双臂张开，俯瞰翠绿色湖泊

Torres del Paine, southern Chile托雷斯德尔潘内，智利南部

From the roof of Asia I went to the wild bottom of the Americas — Patagonia. The wind down there does not blow; it shoves. The trails are long, the weather changes its mind every twenty minutes, and the famous towers of Torres del Paine hide behind cloud just to test your patience. I hiked for hours, soaked and aching, half-convinced I'd see nothing.从亚洲屋脊，我来到美洲的荒野尽头——巴塔哥尼亚。那里的风不是在吹，而是在推。山路漫长，天气每二十分钟换一次主意，托雷斯德尔潘内著名的岩塔躲在云后考验你的耐心。我在雨中艰难跋涉数小时，湿透了，腰酸背痛，几乎以为什么也看不到了。

Then I scrambled up onto a boulder above a glacial lake the colour of melted turquoise, and the clouds parted, and there they were — the jagged peaks, lit silver. I threw both arms up to the sky. No words. Just a seventy-year-old man on a rock at the end of the world, grinning like a boy, the cold lake glittering below.然后我爬上一块巨石，俯瞰一片融化的翡翠色冰川湖，云散开了，它们出现了——锯齿状的山峰，被银光笼罩。我把双臂高举向天空。没有言语。只有一个七十岁的男人站在世界尽头的岩石上，像孩子一样咧嘴笑着，冰冷的湖水在下方闪光。

Patagonia, Chile — arms wide above a glacial lake, the towers of Torres del Paine behind. Earned, every soaking step of it.智利巴塔哥尼亚——双臂张开，俯瞰冰川湖，托雷斯德尔潘内的山峰在身后。每一步淋湿的跋涉，都是值得的。

Annapurna and Patagonia are the two climbs I think of most. They were the hardest. They are also, not by accident, the ones that left me strongest — in the legs, yes, but mostly somewhere deeper.安纳普尔纳和巴塔哥尼亚是我最常想起的两次攀登。它们是最艰难的。也绝非偶然，它们同样是让我变得最强壮的——腿上是的，但更多是在某个更深的地方。

Chapter Three · Silence & Sand & Stone第三章 · 静默、沙漠与石头

From Antarctic Ice to Egyptian Sand从南极冰原到埃及沙漠

Antarctica · Egypt · Mongolia南极洲 · 埃及 · 蒙古

Not every place asked me to climb. Some simply asked me to stand still and feel small. Antarctica did that. In my red jacket among the glaciers, the water so still it became a mirror, I understood what true silence sounds like — the white silence at the very bottom of the world. Nothing moved. Nothing needed to. I have never felt so far from everything, and so completely at peace.不是每一个地方都要求我攀登。有些地方只是让我静立，感受自己的渺小。南极洲就是如此。穿着红色夹克，置身冰川之间，水面宁静如镜，我才明白真正的寂静是什么声音——世界最底端的白色寂静。什么都没有移动。什么都不需要。我从未感觉如此远离一切，又如此彻底地平静。

Antarctica — a red jacket in a white silence. The stillest place I have ever stood.南极洲——白色寂静中的红色夹克。我站过的最静止的地方。

Then the opposite extreme: Egypt, where I stood with my arms outstretched before the Pyramids of Giza, four and a half thousand years of stone in front of me, and the heat coming up off the sand in waves. To touch something so old reminds you how short a single life is — and how worth filling.然后是截然相反的极端：埃及。我张开双臂，站在吉萨金字塔前，四千五百年的石头在我眼前，热浪从沙地上滚滚涌来。触摸如此古老的事物，提醒你一段生命有多短暂——以及多值得去填满。

The Pyramids of Giza, Egypt — arms open before four and a half thousand years of stone.埃及吉萨金字塔——张开双臂，面对四千五百年的石头。

And in Mongolia, I climbed a dune. It does not sound like much until you try it — sand swallowing every step, slipping you backward, the sun overhead. I reached the crest, threw up my arms, and laughed at the sheer ridiculous joy of an old man conquering a hill of sand. The desert, it turns out, keeps the same lesson as the mountain: keep going, and you'll get there.在蒙古，我爬上了一座沙丘。听起来不像什么大事，直到你亲自尝试——沙子吞没每一步，把你向后滑，头顶烈日炎炎。我到达了顶峰，高举双臂，为一个老人征服一座沙山的荒谬喜悦而大笑。沙漠，原来，和山一样保有同一个道理：不断前进，你终将到达。

Mongolia — triumphant on a desert dune.蒙古——在沙漠沙丘上凯旋。
Chapter Four · Roofs, Summits & Strangers第四章 · 屋顶、山顶与陌生人

Tibet, the Philippines, and a Village in Africa西藏、菲律宾，以及非洲的一个村庄

The roof of the world, and the warmth of strangers世界屋脊，以及陌生人的温暖

In Tibet I stood high on the “roof of the world,” mountains in every direction, the thin air making even a smile feel like effort. Up there, closer to the sky than most people ever get, I felt the same quiet I'd found in Antarctica — the bigness of the world putting me gently in my place.在西藏，我高高站在”世界屋脊”上，群山在四面延伸，稀薄的空气让即使是微笑也感觉费力。在那里，比大多数人一生所到的更靠近天空，我感受到了与南极洲同样的宁静——世界的辽阔轻轻地将我安放于我的位置。

Tibet — high on the roof of the world, mountains in every direction.西藏——站上世界屋脊的高处，群山环绕四面。

In the Philippines, after a hard climb through mist, I sat on a rock atop a mountain summit with the clouds rolling past below my boots. Soaked, tired, happy. That's the recipe, it seems — the harder the way up, the sweeter the rock at the top.在菲律宾，历经在云雾中艰难攀登后，我坐在山顶的岩石上，云雾在靴子下方飘过。湿透了，疲惫，快乐。这似乎是那个配方——上山的路越艰难，顶端那块岩石就越甜蜜。

The Philippines — resting on a misty summit after the long climb up.菲律宾——长途攀登后，在云雾缭绕的山顶小憩。

But the moment that stays warmest in my heart wasn't a summit at all. It was a rural village in Africa, sitting on the red-dirt road, sharing the local food the people offered me with open hands. They had little, and they gave freely. Travel had taken me to the highest, coldest, most remote corners of the planet — and yet it was a simple meal among kind strangers that I keep coming back to. Mountains make you strong. People make you grateful.但留在我心中最温暖的那一刻，根本不是山顶。而是非洲的一个乡村，坐在红土路上，和人们分享他们敞开双手递给我的当地食物。他们拥有不多，却慷慨地给予。旅行将我带到了地球上最高、最寒冷、最偏远的角落——然而，让我不断回想的，是在善良陌生人之间共享的一顿简单饭食。山让你变得坚强，人让你学会感恩。

A village in Africa — sharing local food on a red-dirt road, among the kindest people I've met.非洲的一个村庄——在红土路上共享当地食物，与我遇见过的最善良的人们。
Chapter Five · Still Learning第五章 · 仍在学习

Harbin — An Old Man on Skis哈尔滨——一个老人在滑雪

Near Harbin, China中国哈尔滨附近

And just so you don't think it's all reverent silence and mountain tears — near Harbin, in China's frozen northeast, I went skiing. At seventy. Carving down a snowy slope, cold air biting my face, very much hoping my travel insurance was up to date. I am not a graceful skier. But I am a determined one. And there is no rule that says a man must stop learning new ways to fall over just because his hair has gone grey.不要以为这一切都是虔诚的寂静和山顶的泪水——在中国冰冷东北地区哈尔滨附近，我去滑了雪。七十岁。在雪坡上转弯，冷风扑面，非常希望自己的旅行保险仍在有效期内。我不是一个优雅的滑雪者，但我是一个有韧劲的滑雪者。没有任何规定说，一个人的头发变灰了，就必须停止学习新的摔倒方式。

Near Harbin, China — skiing at seventy. Still learning, still falling, still getting back up.中国哈尔滨附近——七十岁滑雪。仍在学习，仍在跌倒，仍在爬起来。
Chapter Six · A New Kind of Climb第六章 · 一种新的攀登

The Day I Wrestled a Robot — and Won我与机器人搏斗的那一天——并且赢了

Singapore · June 2026 · 5:46am to 7:23am新加坡 · 2026年6月 · 凌晨5:46至7:23

Not every hard climb involves a mountain. Some involve a computer, a blinking cursor, and a morning that starts before six.并非每一次艰难的攀登都涉及山。有些只需要一台电脑、一个闪烁的光标，以及一个六点前就开始的早晨。

When I am not travelling, I keep my mind alive by learning. Over the past month I went deep into the world of AI — not just using it, but truly understanding it. I learned how Claude Code works, the difference between Claude Code (a coding tool you run in a terminal) and Claude the chat assistant, and how to make use of these tools to solve my own real problems: building this website, pulling investment data from the Tiger Brokers and IBKR APIs, and writing the stories you are reading right now.不旅行的时候，我用学习保持头脑活跃。过去一个月，我深入探索了AI的世界——不只是使用它，而是真正理解它。我学习了Claude Code的工作原理，了解了Claude Code（一个在终端运行的编程工具）和Claude聊天助手之间的区别，以及如何利用这些工具解决我自己的实际问题：建立这个网站、从Tiger Brokers和IBKR API获取投资数据，以及写下你现在正在阅读的这些故事。

None of it was easy. Setting up APIs, troubleshooting PATH conflicts, accidentally exposing API keys in screenshots — I made every possible mistake. Four API keys deleted. Seven failed login attempts. A Bun-versus-npm conflict that took the better part of an hour to crack. At 7:05am I told the AI: “I give up.” It told me: “Don’t give up. You’re very close.” Eighteen minutes later, everything worked.这一切都不容易。设置API、排查PATH冲突、不小心在截图中暴露API密钥——我犯了每一个可能的错误。四个API密钥被删除。七次登录尝试失败。一个Bun与npm的冲突花了将近一个小时才解决。早上7:05我告诉AI：”我放弃了。”它告诉我：”不要放弃。你已经很接近了。”十八分钟后，一切正常工作。

”The most valuable thing I learned is not from a book. It is from doing, failing, and doing it again until it works. Trial and error — that is the real teacher.””我学到的最宝贵的东西不是来自书本。而是来自实践、失败，再实践直到成功。反复试验——那才是真正的老师。”

The comparison to a mountain climb is not accidental. The feeling at 7:23am, when Claude Code finally opened cleanly with no errors — no Bun, no API key, no expired login codes — was the same feeling I had at Annapurna Base Camp. Hard-won. Earned. A little ridiculous that it took this long. And completely unforgettable.与山地攀登的比较并非偶然。早上7:23，当Claude Code终于干净地打开，没有任何错误——没有Bun，没有API密钥，没有过期的登录码——那种感觉与我在安纳普尔纳大本营时一模一样。来之不易，是自己挣来的。花了这么久，有点荒谬。完全难以忘怀。

Travel and learning are, at their core, the same thing: you arrive somewhere unfamiliar, you make mistakes, you ask for help, and eventually you find your way. The mountain does not care how old you are. Neither does the computer. Both reward the same quality: the refusal to quit.旅行与学习，在本质上是同一件事：你来到一个陌生的地方，犯错，寻求帮助，最终找到方向。山不在乎你多大了。电脑也不在乎。两者都奖励同一种品质：拒绝放弃的执念。

Epilogue · What It Gave Me尾声 · 它给了我什么

The Hard Way Up Was the Whole Gift艰难的上坡路，本身就是全部的礼物

A reflection, at seventy七十岁时的一段反思

Three years. Nine corners of the world. A great many hostel bunks and more sore mornings than I can count. People my age are supposed to slow down, and perhaps one day I will. But not yet — not while there are still trails that frighten me a little, and summits I'm not sure my legs can reach.三年。世界的九个角落。数不清的青旅铺位和酸痛的清晨。我这般年纪的人本应放慢脚步，也许有一天我会。但还不是现在——还没到那些仍让我略感恐惧的山路、还没到那些不确定双腿能否到达的山顶都走完的时候。

Because here is the truth I carried home from all of it: the hard climbs are the ones that made me stronger. Not just in the body — though my body is tougher now than it was at sixty-five — but in the spirit. Every difficult trail taught me that I could do more than I believed. Every solo flight taught me I needn't be afraid. Every stranger in a hostel kitchen taught me the world is far kinder than the news would have you think.因为这是我从那一切中带回家的真相：艰难的攀登才是让我更坚强的那些。不只是身体上——尽管我的身体现在比六十五岁时更强健——而是在精神上。每一条难走的山路都告诉我，我能做到比自己以为更多的事。每一次独自出发都告诉我，不必害怕。每一个青旅厨房里的陌生人都告诉我，这个世界比新闻让你以为的要善良得多。

”Age is no barrier. It is only a number you carry up the mountain with you. And when you reach the top — out of breath, knees aching, heart full — you set it down for a moment and you are simply a person, alive, alone, and free.””年龄不是障碍。它只是你携带上山的一个数字。当你到达顶峰——气喘吁吁，膝盖酸痛，心中充盈——你暂时放下它，你只是一个人，活着，独自，自由。”

What Three Years Alone Taught Me独自三年教会我的事

  1. The hard climbs change you; the easy ones don't. Comfort is pleasant, but strength only comes from the steep way up.艰难的攀登改变你；轻松的不会。舒适是愉快的，但力量只来自陡峭的上坡路。
  2. Solo never means alone. A backpack and an open heart will fill your days with strangers who become friends by morning.独行从不意味着孤独。一个背包和一颗开放的心会让你的日子充满陌生人，他们到早上就成了朋友。
  3. The body rises to what you ask of it — at sixty-seven, at seventy, at whatever age you happen to be reading this. Ask kindly, and keep walking.身体会回应你的要求——六十七岁、七十岁、无论你读到这段话时是什么年纪。温和地要求，继续前行。
  4. Gratitude weighs nothing and is the best thing to carry. The mountains gave me strength; the people gave me the reason.感恩不重，却是最值得携带之物。山给了我力量；人给了我理由。
  5. Learning never stops. Whether it’s a Himalayan trail or a blinking cursor at 5:46am — trial and error is the greatest teacher of all.学习永无止境。无论是喜马拉雅山的小径还是凌晨5:46闪烁的光标——反复试验是最伟大的老师。

So if you're wondering whether it's too late to go — to climb the hard hill, to fly alone, to sleep in the cheap bunk and wake to a new country — let an old man with a worn-out backpack tell you: it isn't. It never was. The mountain is still there. Lace up your boots. I'll see you on the trail. 🎒⛰️所以，如果你在想是否已经太晚去走那段路——去攀登那座难山、独自飞行、睡在廉价铺位上醒来面对新的国度——让一个背包已经磨破的老人告诉你：还没有。从来没有。那座山还在那里。系好靴带。我们山上见。🎒⛰️

The End全文完
