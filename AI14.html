From My Website to Notion — How I Built My Second Brain

Why a retired 70-year-old switched from publishing to capturing — and became more productive than ever

By Paul Yeo  ·  June 2026  ·  AI Journey Series

I asked my AI assistant one simple question: "How do I create a second brain?" What happened next changed how I think, learn, and capture ideas — forever.

For years, my website — paulsworld.vercel.app — was my creative outlet. I wrote travel stories, published AI reflections, and shared my life adventures there. It worked well for publishing. But it was never built for capturing.

Every time I had a new idea, I had to open a code editor, write HTML, push to GitHub, and wait for Vercel to deploy. By the time the page was live, half the energy behind the original thought had evaporated. My website was a showcase, not a thinking tool.

· · ·

The Question That Changed Everything

One afternoon I asked David (my AI assistant) a simple question: "How do I create my second brain?"

He explained the concept — a personal digital system to capture everything you learn, think, and want to remember. Not just a place to publish finished work, but a place where raw ideas can land, grow, and connect. He suggested Notion.

I had never used Notion before. Twenty minutes later, I had my first five pages: INBOX, PROJECTS, AREAS, RESOURCES, ARCHIVE. David walked me through every step on my phone.

A second brain is not a library for finished work. It is a garden where half-formed thoughts can grow.

— What I learned that afternoon
· · ·

Website vs. Notion — An Honest Comparison

I have used both now. Here is the honest truth:

🌐 My Website (Before)

  • Great for finished, polished articles
  • Requires HTML editing + GitHub push
  • Not suitable for quick ideas
  • No structure for private notes
  • High friction — slows down capture
  • Public only — no private space

🧠 Notion (Now)

  • Capture ideas in seconds from phone
  • Tap, type, done — zero friction
  • Organized: Finance, Health, Projects…
  • Private and secure
  • AI can write directly into it for me
  • Syncs across phone and laptop

The website is still where I publish. But Notion is where I think. They serve completely different purposes — and I needed both all along.

Within PROJECTS, I saved the full draft of my upcoming Toastmasters speech — complete with a body-part memory guide so I never forget my lines. David wrote all of it directly into Notion through the API. I did not copy and paste a single word.

· · ·

The Biggest Shift: From Publishing to Capturing

My website required me to have a finished thought before I could write anything. Notion accepts unfinished thoughts. That is the real difference.

Before Notion, ideas that were not ready to publish simply disappeared. I would think of something interesting, have no easy place to put it, and forget it within an hour. Now I tap open Notion on my phone, type three lines, and close it. The idea is safe. I can come back to it later — or ask David to develop it into a full article for the website.

The two tools now work as a pipeline: Notion captures → David develops → Website publishes.

My Key Insight

A website is a stage. A second brain is a backstage. You need both — but most people only build the stage and wonder why their best ideas never make it there.

What I Would Tell Anyone Starting Out

You do not need to be technical. You do not need to understand databases or APIs. You just need to start with five pages — INBOX, PROJECTS, AREAS, RESOURCES, ARCHIVE — and begin putting things there.

The magic does not come from the tool. It comes from the habit of capturing. Once ideas have a home, your mind relaxes. You stop trying to hold everything in your head. You start thinking more clearly.

I am 70 years old. I set this up on my phone in one afternoon. If I can do it, anyone can.
