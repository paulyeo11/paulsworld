The Night I Was Back at My Old Job 那晚我又回到了 旧工作

A dream about late nights, old pressure, and a customer who liked to gamble his money away 一场关于加班、旧日压力，和一位喜欢把钱拿去赌的客户的梦

By Paul Yeo  ·  Singapore  ·  July 2026 杨汉忠 著  ·  新加坡  ·  2026年7月

✅ Link copied!链接已复制！

Last night I had a dream I haven't had in a long time. In it, I was still working for my old boss — the one from before I retired. I was out working late, and I could feel the old pressure sitting on my chest again, the same way it used to when I was employed. It surprised me how real it still felt, even after all these years of being free of it. 昨晚我做了一个很久没做过的梦。梦里，我还在替我退休前的老老板打工。我在外面加班到很晚，胸口又压上了那种熟悉的压力，跟我还在上班时一模一样。让我惊讶的是，即使已经自由了这么多年，那种感觉依然那么真实。

Back Out Working Late又是加班在外的夜晚

In the dream I wasn't in an office — I was outside, still on the job, still on the clock, well past the hour I should have gone home. There was no clear crisis, just that low, constant hum of pressure that comes from knowing people are depending on you to get something right. I remember thinking, even inside the dream, "haven't I already left all this behind?" But the feeling didn't care that I was retired. It just showed up again, exactly as it used to be. 梦里我不是在办公室，而是在外头，还在工作，还没到能回家的时候，早就过了该下班的时间。没有什么明确的危机，只是那种低沉、持续的压力——知道有人在依靠你把事情做对的那种感觉。我记得自己在梦里都在想："我不是早就把这一切放下了吗？"可那种感觉不管我是不是退休了，它就是那样又冒出来，跟以前一模一样。

💬 The Customer and His Problem那位客户与他的问题

At some point I was talking with a customer. I asked him how he had solved his problem — genuinely curious, the way I always used to be. That was the part of the job I never minded, even under pressure: sitting with someone, listening to what had gone wrong for them, and trying to help them work it out. In the dream I was doing exactly that again — trying to help this customer, and others, solve whatever it was they were stuck on. 梦里的某一刻，我在跟一位客户交谈。我问他是怎么解决自己的问题的——是真心好奇，就跟我一直以来的习惯一样。这是这份工作里我从来不介意的部分，即使压力再大也一样：坐下来听一个人说他遇到了什么麻烦，然后试着帮他想办法。梦里我又在做同样的事——试着帮这位客户，还有其他人，解决他们卡住的问题。

And then, mixed in among the customers I was trying to help, was this detail my mind held onto: some of them liked to gamble their money away. Not all of them had a problem I could actually fix — some of them, the real problem wasn't the thing they were asking me about at all. It was something underneath, a habit that kept undoing whatever progress we made together. 而在我试着帮助的这些客户当中，我的脑海里还留住了这样一个细节：有些人喜欢把钱拿去赌。并不是每个人的问题都是我真能解决的——有些人，真正的问题其实根本不是他们表面上问我的那件事，而是底下藏着的一个习惯，不断把我们一起取得的进展给拆掉。

— Paul Yeo, a dream on the night of 5 July 2026杨汉忠，2026年7月5日夜里的一个梦

Why This Dream, Now为什么是这个梦，为什么是现在

I retired at 67, and it's been good years since — travel, hiking, learning AI, no boss, no clock to be out past. So it caught me off guard to wake up still carrying that old, specific pressure in my chest, like my body had simply filed it away instead of letting it go. I think some things you carry for nine years at a company don't leave just because you've handed in your resignation. They surface again, quietly, in a dream, on an ordinary night. 我67岁退休，之后的这几年过得很好——旅行、爬山、学AI，没有老板，也没有非得在外头待到几点的规定。所以醒来时发现自己胸口还带着那种旧有的、具体的压力，就像身体只是把它归了档，而不是真的放下了，这让我有点措手不及。我想，有些在一家公司待了九年积累下来的东西，不会因为你递了辞职信就真的离开。它们会安静地再次浮现，在一个梦里，在一个再普通不过的夜晚。

But the part that stayed with me most wasn't the pressure — it was that I was still trying to help. Even in a dream, asking a customer how he'd solved his own problem, still curious, still wanting to be useful to someone stuck. That impulse to help people work through what's troubling them clearly outlasted the job itself. It's still in me now, whether I'm talking to a real customer or just writing these stories for whoever reads them. 但让我印象最深的，不是那份压力——而是我仍然在试着帮忙。就算是在梦里，问一位客户他是怎么解决自己问题的，依然带着好奇，依然想对一个陷入困境的人有点用处。这种想帮别人理清麻烦的冲动，显然比这份工作本身活得更久。它现在依然在我身上，不管我是在跟一位真实的客户交谈，还是只是在写这些故事给读到它们的人看。

What I'm Taking From This Dream这个梦给我的启发

  • ①The body remembers pressure long after the job ends. Retirement removed the boss and the clock, but old feelings can still visit uninvited — and that's normal, not a step backwards.身体记得压力，即使工作早已结束。退休拿走了老板和打卡钟，但旧有的感受还是会不请自来——这很正常，不是退步。
  • ②Wanting to help people solve their problems was never really "the job." It was a part of me. That's why it's still showing up, years later, in my dreams and in my life.想帮别人解决问题，从来就不只是"工作"。那是我的一部分。这就是为什么这么多年后，它还会出现在我的梦里，也出现在我的生活里。
  • ③Some people fix the wrong problem. Chasing a quick win — like gambling — instead of the real issue underneath is a trap I saw in customers back then, and it's a reminder I still keep for myself today.有些人解决的是错误的问题。追逐一个快速的输赢——比如赌钱——而不是面对底下真正的问题，这是我当年在客户身上看到的陷阱，如今也依然是我提醒自己的一课。
  • ④I'm grateful I don't have to live that late-night pressure anymore. The dream reminded me, more than anything, how much better my life is now — and how much I don't miss being out working late for someone else.我很庆幸自己不用再过那种加班到深夜、压力缠身的生活了。这个梦提醒我最多的，其实是我现在的生活好了多少——也让我明白，我一点都不怀念那种为别人加班到深夜的日子。

Some dreams are just the mind tidying up old boxes it forgot to unpack. This one reminded me who I still am underneath all the years — someone who likes helping people find their way through a problem — and who I'm glad I no longer have to be, at least not the late nights and the pressure. 有些梦，不过是大脑在整理它忘了拆开的旧箱子。这一个提醒了我，这么多年过去，我骨子里仍然是谁——一个喜欢帮别人找到解决问题办法的人——也让我庆幸，至少那些加班到深夜、压力缠身的日子，我已经不必再过了。
