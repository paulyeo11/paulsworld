h12: Why Do I Feel Scared Hearing a High-Speed Train?h12：为什么听到高速列车驶过我会感到害怕？

A Sudden Startle Response — and What I Learned About It一次突然的惊吓反应——我从中学到了什么

What Happened发生了什么

I was standing near the tracks when I heard a high-speed train coming — that fast, rising rumble getting louder every second — and suddenly I felt a wave of fear. My heart started beating faster, my body tensed up, and for a moment I wasn't sure why something so ordinary made me feel that way.我站在铁轨附近，听到一列高速列车驶来——那种越来越响的隆隆声——突然间我感到一阵恐惧。我的心跳加快，身体紧绷，一时间我不明白为什么这么平常的事会让我有这种感觉。

At 70, I've heard trains all my life. So why did this one feel different? I looked into it, and it turns out this is a very normal reaction — and there are real reasons behind it.我今年70岁，一辈子都听过火车的声音。那为什么这一次感觉不一样？我查了一下，发现这其实是一种很正常的反应——背后是有原因的。

Why This Happens为什么会这样

The Startle Reflex惊吓反射A sudden loud, rising noise triggers an automatic "fight or flight" response in the brain — it happens before you even consciously register what the sound is. This is instinct, not weakness.突然的、逐渐增强的响声会触发大脑自动的"战或逃"反应——在你意识到那是什么声音之前，这个反应就已经发生了。这是本能，不是软弱。
Sound That Seems to Approach听起来正在逼近的声音A fast-moving train's pitch and volume rise quickly (the Doppler effect) — the brain reads a rapidly rising sound as "getting closer, fast," which is exactly the kind of signal our nervous system is wired to treat as danger.高速列车的音调和音量会迅速升高（多普勒效应）——大脑会把快速增强的声音理解为"正在快速逼近"，而这正是我们神经系统天生会当作危险信号的那种声音。
Age-Related Hearing Sensitivity与年龄相关的听觉敏感As we age, some people become more sensitive to loud or sudden sounds (a form of hyperacusis), even without any hearing loss. The ears and brain can process sudden noise more intensely than before.随着年龄增长，有些人对突然或响亮的声音会变得更敏感（一种称为"听觉过敏"的现象），即使听力本身并没有下降。耳朵和大脑对突发噪音的处理会比以前更强烈。
General Anxiety Amplifies It整体焦虑会放大反应If you're already a little tired, stressed, or your blood pressure has been running high, your body is primed to react more strongly to sudden stimuli. The train didn't cause the anxiety — it just triggered it.如果你本来就有点疲惫、压力大，或者血压偏高，身体就会更容易对突发刺激产生强烈反应。列车本身不是焦虑的原因——它只是触发了原本就存在的反应。

What I'm Doing About It我正在采取的做法

🌿 Simple Steps That Help有帮助的简单做法

  • Slow breathing the moment I notice the fear — in for 4 seconds, hold for 4, out for 6一察觉到害怕就放慢呼吸——吸气4秒，屏气4秒，呼气6秒
  • Remind myself out loud: "This is just a train. I am safe."出声提醒自己："这只是一列火车，我很安全。"
  • Stand a comfortable distance from the platform edge if the sound feels overwhelming如果声音让我感到不适，就站得离站台边缘远一点
  • Avoid extra caffeine before travel — it makes the startle response sharper出行前避免多喝咖啡——它会让惊吓反应更强烈
  • Notice and accept the reaction rather than fight it — it passes within seconds接受这个反应而不是去对抗它——它几秒钟内就会过去

When to See a Doctor什么情况该看医生

⚠️ Don't Ignore These不要忽视这些情况

  • The fear comes with chest pain, breathlessness, or a racing heart that doesn't settle恐惧伴随胸痛、呼吸困难，或心跳持续加快无法平复
  • Dizziness, spinning, or loss of balance along with the noise听到声音时伴随头晕、眩晕或失去平衡
  • Ringing in the ears (tinnitus) that is new or getting worse耳鸣是新出现的，或者越来越严重
  • The fear is happening with many ordinary sounds, not just trains, and is affecting daily life这种恐惧不只针对火车，而是对很多日常声音都会出现，并影响到日常生活

If any of these apply, it's worth mentioning to a doctor — it could be worth a hearing check, a blood pressure check, or simply talking it through.如果出现以上任何情况，建议告诉医生——可以做个听力检查、量个血压，或者找人聊一聊。

"A racing heart at a passing train is the body being alert — not broken. Breathe, and let it pass.""火车驶过时心跳加快，是身体在警觉——不是出了问题。深呼吸，让它过去。"
