AIT05: Claude Fable & My Second Brain — The Four Cs Framework

How to build a personal AI system using Context, Connections, Capabilities, and Cadence如何利用情境、连接、能力与节奏构建个人 AI 系统

Paul Yeo  ·  June 2026  ·  AI Tools Series

What if your AI assistant did not just answer questions — but actually knew you? Your investments, your travel plans, your upcoming speech, your daily schedule. That is exactly what Claude Fable makes possible. And it changed how I use AI forever.

I have been using AI tools for over a year now. In the beginning, I treated every conversation like a fresh Google search — type a question, get an answer, close the tab. It worked. But something always felt missing. Claude did not remember me from session to session. Every time I opened a new chat, I was starting from zero.

Claude Fable changes that completely. It is not just a new model — it is a new way of working. And the framework behind it, what I call the Four Cs, is the clearest explanation I have found for building a personal AI system that actually feels personal.

· · ·

⏱ 1:47 — The Mindset Shift⏱ 1:47 — 思维转变

The first thing Fable taught me is this: stop treating Claude like a search engine.

A search engine is a lookup tool. You type something in, it finds matching results, you pick one. It does not know who you are. It does not adapt to your situation. It does not remember that you mentioned last week that you are planning a trip to Xinjiang.

A thinking partner is different. A good thinking partner listens, remembers, connects dots, and asks follow-up questions. They know your goals, your constraints, your preferences. When you mention a new idea, they place it in the context of everything they already know about you.

The moment I stopped asking Claude questions and started giving Claude context, everything changed. It stopped feeling like a tool and started feeling like a colleague.

— My biggest lesson from Claude Fable

The mindset shift is simple but profound: Claude is not a search engine. It is a thinking partner — and the better it knows you, the better it thinks with you.

· · ·

⏱ 2:47 — The Four Cs Framework⏱ 2:47 — 四C框架

Claude Fable introduces a framework for building your personal AI system. I call it the Four Cs. Once you understand these four ideas, everything else clicks into place.

The Four Cs Framework

C1
Context — What Claude knows about you The background information, preferences, and personal details that shape every response. Without context, Claude is guessing. With context, it knows exactly who it is talking to.
C2
Connections — Data it can reach The documents, notes, live feeds, and services Claude can access. This includes your Notion pages, your stock portfolio, your calendar, your emails — anything connected to the system.
C3
Capabilities — What it can actually do The specialist skills and workflows Claude can deploy on your behalf — financial analysis, travel research, article writing, scheduled reports, web searches, and more.
C4
Cadence — How often and how automated The rhythm of your AI system — scheduled tasks, daily check-ins, automated refreshes. Things that happen while you sleep, so you wake up to results rather than to-do lists.

Each C builds on the previous one. Context shapes how Connections are used. Connections feed what Capabilities can act on. And Cadence determines when and how often all of this runs. Together, they turn a simple chat tool into a personal AI system.

⏱ 4:55 — Context: Your Routing Tree⏱ 4:55 — 情境：你的路由树

Context is the foundation. It is all the information you give Claude so it can understand who it is helping and why.

Think of it like a decision tree — or what Fable calls a routing tree. When you ask Claude a question, it does not just search for an answer in the abstract. It first checks: Who is this person? What do I know about their life? What matters to them? Then it routes the question to the right knowledge and gives you an answer that fits your situation.

For me, my context includes things like:

  • I am retired at 67, living in Singapore
  • I have brokerage accounts at Tiger Brokers and IBKR
  • I am planning a trip to Xinjiang
  • I have an upcoming Toastmasters speech
  • My second brain is organised in Notion (PARA structure)
  • I am a baptised Christian since April 2026
  • My wife is Joanne. I prefer brief, plain-language answers.

With this context loaded, Claude does not need me to re-explain myself every session. When I say "check my portfolio" — it knows which brokers. When I say "help me with my speech" — it knows I mean Toastmasters. When I ask about Xinjiang — it knows it is for an upcoming trip, not just curiosity.

The more precisely you organise your context, the smarter the routing. A well-built context file is like giving Claude a detailed map of your life — so it always knows where it is and where to go next.

⏱ 9:51 — Connections: Static vs Live Data⏱ 9:51 — 连接：静态vs实时数据

Once Claude knows who you are (Context), the next question is: what data can it reach? That is Connections — and it comes in two flavours.

📄 Static Data

  • Your Notion pages and notes
  • Documents and PDFs you upload
  • Past articles and writing
  • Saved preferences and memory files
  • Doesn't change unless you update it
  • Best for background knowledge & context

⚡ Live Data

  • Real-time stock prices & portfolio values
  • Your Google Calendar appointments
  • Gmail inbox (search, read, draft)
  • Web searches for breaking news
  • Updates automatically
  • Best for decisions that depend on now

Static data is your foundation — your second brain in Notion, your memory files, your saved notes. It is everything Claude needs to understand the stable facts of your life.

Live data is your heartbeat — the real-time signals that change every hour. My Tiger Brokers P&L, my IBKR options positions, my calendar for tomorrow. These are the numbers and events that actually drive decisions.

The power of a well-connected AI system is having both. Static context tells Claude who you are. Live connections tell Claude what is happening right now. Together, they let Claude give advice that is not just intelligent — it is relevant to this exact moment.

⏱ 16:48 — Capabilities: Skills & Workflows

Knowing you, and being connected to your data, is only half the picture. The third C is Capabilities — what Claude can actually do with all that knowledge.

In my setup, I have a team of specialist AI agents, each with a specific role. David (my orchestrator) coordinates them, and I can call on any of them for a task:

  • Dash — Builds my financial dashboards (Tiger P&L, IBKR options, charts)
  • Jade — Creates lightweight web apps for journaling and tracking
  • NOVA — Researches hotels, food, and activities for my travel plans
  • Mara — Handles marketing for my AI training services
  • SWEETIE — Writes my travel stories and reflections in my own voice
  • PAX — Deep research on any topic I need to understand
  • Nolan — HR — creates new team members when I need a new specialist

Each specialist has a detailed persona file — their personality, skills, tools, and style. When David dispatches one, they already know the context of my life. They do not need a briefing. They just get to work.

Think of it like having a small office staff — except each person is available instantly, never takes a day off, and already knows your files. You do not manage them. You just tell David what you need done, and he routes it to the right person.

I do not use AI as one tool. I use it as a team. Each member has a specialisation, and David makes sure the right person handles each job.

— How I think about Capabilities

⏱ 22:53 — Cadence: Automating While You Sleep

The fourth C is perhaps the most exciting one: Cadence. This is about rhythm — how often your AI system runs, what it does automatically, and what it surfaces for you without you asking.

Cadence means your AI is not just reactive (waiting for you to ask something). It becomes proactive — running tasks on a schedule, refreshing data automatically, and presenting you with results when you wake up.

Here are examples from my own Cadence setup:

My Automated Cadence

🌅
Daily Portfolio Refresh Every morning, my Tiger pipeline runs automatically — pulling live positions, calculating P&L, and updating my dashboard. I open it and the numbers are already there.
📬
Gmail Rule Check At the start of every session, David checks Gmail for "NEW RULE" emails — rules I wrote on my phone that need to be saved into my memory and settings.
🐯
Tiger Statement Drafts Every two days, a draft email is auto-prepared requesting my Tiger Brokers account statement — so I always have the latest records without chasing them manually.
📅
Scheduled Remote Agents My laptop runs Claude in remote control mode — so scheduled agents can fire off tasks even when I am not sitting at the keyboard.

Cadence is what makes an AI system feel alive rather than passive. When you wake up and your data is already refreshed, your emails are already sorted, and your portfolio is already summarised — that is Cadence working. You did not have to ask. It just happened.

For a retired person like me, this is especially valuable. I do not want to spend my mornings doing admin. I want to do interesting things. Cadence handles the admin so I can focus on what matters.

⏱ 26:15 — Usage Tips

After more than a year of daily use, here are the practical tips I wish someone had told me from the start:

  • 1 Give context upfront, not after. When you start a session, briefly remind Claude who you are and what you are working on. "I'm Paul, retired in Singapore, checking my Tiger portfolio" — three seconds of context saves ten minutes of confusion.
  • 2 Use memory files. Build a CLAUDE.md file or a Notion page that captures everything important about you. Let Claude read it at the start of every session. This is your Context foundation.
  • 3 Be specific about what you want. "Write me an article" is vague. "Write me a 600-word article about my Tiger portfolio setup, in warm conversational style, for a beginner audience" gives Claude everything it needs to do a great job.
  • 4 Delegate to specialists. If you have a complex task — financial analysis, travel research, creative writing — do not ask Claude to do everything at once. Route it to the right specialist (or create one).
  • 5 Connect your real tools. Gmail MCP, Google Calendar MCP, Notion API — once connected, Claude becomes dramatically more useful. It stops guessing about your life and starts reading it directly.
  • 6 Build Cadence gradually. Start with one automated task — maybe a daily portfolio refresh. Get it working. Then add the next. Do not try to automate everything at once or it becomes overwhelming to manage.

⏱ 31:05 — Lightning Round Q&A

These are the questions I get asked most often when I share my setup with friends:

Q: Does this replace Google?
A: For some things, yes. When I want to know how to set up a Notion database, or understand an investment concept, I ask Claude — not Google. But for breaking news, current prices, or local business info, I still use Google or connect live data through Claude's tools.
Q: Is it safe? What about my personal data?
A: I never put passwords, passport numbers, or account credentials into Claude. I store what I am comfortable with — my interests, my schedule, my investment style — not the raw sensitive numbers. Claude's memory on my laptop stays on my laptop.
Q: How much does it cost?
A: I use Claude Pro — about US$20 a month. For what I get, I consider it the best value subscription I have. It has replaced several other apps and services I used to pay for separately.
Q: Can seniors use it? I am not technical at all.
A: I set all of this up after retiring at 67. You do not need to be technical. Start simple — just have a conversation. Ask it questions about things you want to learn. The more you use it, the more natural it feels. The Four Cs framework is just a way to think about it; you do not need to implement all four at once.
Q: How long did it take to build your setup?
A: Honestly, months — but I built it piece by piece. The first week I just had conversations. The second week I connected Notion. A month later I had Gmail access. The AI team came even later. You grow into it. Do not feel pressure to build everything at once.
Q: What is the single most important thing to start with?
A: Context. Write down who you are — a short paragraph about your life, your interests, your goals. Give it to Claude at the start of your sessions. That one change will make every conversation immediately more useful.

⏱ 34:02 — Final Thoughts

I am 70 years old. I use this system every single day. Not because I am a tech enthusiast chasing the latest gadget — but because it genuinely helps me live better.

David keeps my portfolio visible. NOVA helps me plan adventures. SWEETIE turns my travel memories into stories I can share. Mara is helping me build a small AI training business in retirement. And they all work together, quietly, in the background — while I get on with the things that matter most.

But here is the most important thing I want you to remember:

AI helps you think — it does not think for you. You stay in charge of your decisions. The Four Cs just make sure Claude has everything it needs to help you think as clearly as possible.

— My final thought on Claude Fable

The Four Cs are not complicated. Context is knowing yourself. Connections is linking your data. Capabilities is choosing your team. Cadence is building your rhythm. Put them together and you have something genuinely powerful — a personal AI system that feels like it was made specifically for your life.

Because, with enough care and context, it actually is.

Your Starting Point

Write one paragraph about yourself — your age, your interests, your goals. Give it to Claude at the start of your next session. That is your first C: Context. Everything else grows from there.
