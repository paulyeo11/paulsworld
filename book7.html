🌅

Morning Nourishment

Dan. 2:34-35

You were watching until a stone was cut out without hands, and it struck the image at its feet of iron and clay and crushed them. And the stone that struck the image became a great mountain and filled the whole earth.

Job 42:5-6

I had heard of You by the hearing of the ear, but now my eye has seen You; therefore I abhor myself, and I repent in dust and ashes.

This great image in Daniel 2 signifies the aggregate of human government throughout all of history — from Babylon at the head, down to the Roman Empire at the ten toes. Every human kingdom, every empire, every government is part of this one great image.

The destiny of this image is to be crushed by a stone cut out without hands. That stone is Christ. Through His crucifixion He was cut — put to death — and in His resurrection He was cut out to become both the cornerstone for building the church and the crushing stone to destroy all human government.

Christ did not come alone at His first coming, and He will not come alone at His second coming either. He will come with His newly married bride — the overcomers — to strike the image at its toes, crushing it from the bottom to the top. Then the stone will become a great mountain filling the whole earth. This is God's eternal kingdom.

📖 Today's Reading

When Job finally saw God face to face, he said: 'Now my eye has seen You.' This is the turning point. Job had known God by tradition and by knowledge — but now he saw Him personally. Seeing God equals gaining God.

To gain God is to receive God in His element, in His life, and in His nature. This not only makes us one with God — it even makes us a part of God. We see God that we may be constituted with Him.

All of God's redeemed, regenerated, sanctified, transformed, conformed and glorified people will one day see God's face (Rev. 22:4). Seeing God transforms us, because in seeing Him we receive His element into us. As we receive God, a new element comes in, and the old element goes out. This metabolic process is transformation — into the glorious image of Christ, the God-man.

Lord, open my eyes to see You — not just know about You. Let Your nature flow into me and transform me today.
