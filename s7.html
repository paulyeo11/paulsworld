S7: A Chapter Closes — And A Debt of Gratitude

My resignation, the unfinished paperwork, and 27 years with an exceptional boss
✍️ Paul Yeo Poh Sai
May 2026
Personal Reflection

It started with a simple message from my boss. He informed me that he was resigning from the company — and it was only then that I realised something I had overlooked for years: I had never officially resigned from the Penang office. My resignation from the company in March 2023 was not complete. There was still an administrative loose end quietly waiting to be tied up.

In many ways, this small oversight is a fitting metaphor for how my career ended — not with a grand farewell ceremony, but quietly, almost without anyone noticing. That is simply how it is when you have spent 27 years with your head down, doing the work, trusting the people around you, and feeling fortunate every single day.

The Resignation I Almost Forgot

I officially stepped down from my roles in March 2023, retiring after a long and fulfilling career. Life moved on — travelling, learning about artificial intelligence, and spending time with my wife Joanne. Retirement was everything I hoped it would be.

Then came the message. My boss — Vincent Tan — was resigning. And with it came the realisation that my own resignation from TDS Technology (Penang) Sdn Bhd had never been properly filed. The paperwork with the Suruhanjanya Syarikat Malaysia (SSM) was pending. After all these years, I still had one last professional duty to fulfill.

To Vincent Tan — Thank You, From the Bottom of My Heart

There are bosses you work for, and then there are leaders who shape the person you become. Vincent Tan is the latter. Over 27 years — nearly three decades — I reported directly to him. In that entire time, we never had a single argument. Not one. That alone speaks volumes about the kind of man he is.

"He was always the one who shouldered the pressure, so that we could focus on doing our best work. That is the mark of a true leader."

— Paul Yeo Poh Sai

Vincent is a deeply strategic thinker. When problems arose — and in business, they always do — he had the rare ability to see the full picture, to remain calm, and to find a path forward. Under his guidance, I felt empowered to grow, to push myself, and to work harder than I ever thought I could. He motivated me not through pressure, but through example.

I remember vividly, in the year before my retirement, there were moments when leadership faced significant business challenges and high expectations from the organisation. The pressure on Vincent during those times was immense. Yet he stood firm. He handled it all with quiet resolve, never faltering, never passing the burden down to us. He absorbed the storm so we could continue our work in calm waters.

What Made Working for Vincent Special

Two things stand out above all else. First, he trusted us. He never micromanaged. He gave us the space to work, to think, and to grow — and that freedom was a gift I treasure deeply. Second, the salary I received was fair and consistent, yet it was Vincent who carried the weight of every challenge, every crisis, every difficult decision. He gave generously while asking for little in return.

Freedom to Work, Space to Grow

One of the greatest joys of my working life was something many employees never experience: I was never micromanaged. Vincent trusted his team. He set the direction clearly, communicated his expectations, and then stepped back to let us do what we did best. That kind of trust is rare, and it is deeply motivating.

Because of his leadership style, I found myself wanting to do more — not because I had to, but because I genuinely wanted to contribute to something he was building. That is the true power of an empowering leader. They do not push you forward — they inspire you to move forward on your own.

I also think back with a smile to how fortunate I was. I received a consistent, fair salary throughout my career, yet it was Vincent who quietly shouldered the burdens — the politics, the corporate pressures, the difficult conversations with headquarters. He made it look easy. He never complained. And because of him, I was able to do my job with peace of mind for 27 years.

27 Years — And Not a Single Argument

People are often surprised when I tell them this. In 27 years of working together, Vincent and I never once had an argument or a disagreement that left a mark. That is not to say we always agreed on everything — but whatever differences arose were resolved with respect, with listening, and with professionalism. He created an environment where open communication was safe, and that made all the difference.

Since retiring, I have had more time to reflect on what those 27 years truly meant. The travels, the learning, the new adventures in retirement — they are all wonderful. But there is something I did not expect to feel so strongly: I miss working with Vincent. Not just the work itself, but the camaraderie, the shared challenges, the quiet satisfaction of knowing you are part of something larger than yourself.

🙏

Vincent Tan

Thank you for 27 extraordinary years of leadership, trust, and unwavering support. You shaped my career and, in many ways, the person I am today. It was an honour and a privilege to work alongside you. I wish you every success and happiness in the chapters ahead.

Vincent's Reply — Words That Touched My Heart

After sending Vincent my heartfelt WhatsApp message, I did not have to wait long. His reply came, and when I read it, I had to pause for a moment. His words meant more to me than I can express.

VT
Vincent Tan
My Boss  ·  27 Years

"Thank you, Paul. Indeed, it was a pleasure and privilege to work with you for so many years. We complement each other in our working style and I am happy to have a working partner like you. I still missed the job that you are doing when you left. Thank you for your co-operation with me all these years and like you said we work happily with each other and there was never any disagreement or argument. That shows our deep understanding and trust with each other. I wish you all the best in your retirement adventures and stay healthy and keep in touch."

👍 👏 ❤️

Reading his message, what struck me most was how he described us — not as boss and subordinate, but as "working partners." That single word means everything. It reflects exactly how he always made me feel: valued, equal, and trusted.

He said he still misses the work I did. That is perhaps the greatest compliment a colleague can ever give. To know that your contribution was felt, that your absence left a mark — it is deeply humbling. And deeply gratifying.

His final words — "stay healthy and keep in touch" — say it all. This is not goodbye. It is simply a new chapter for both of us, and the friendship we built over 27 years will continue beyond any office walls.

Closing the Final Chapter

And so, in May 2026, I finally put pen to paper — or rather, submitted the formal resignation letter for the Penang office. It is a small act of paperwork, but it carries great meaning. It is the true closing of a career that gave me so much.

To anyone reading this who is still in the workforce: if you are fortunate enough to work for someone like Vincent Tan — someone who trusts you, shields you, empowers you, and leads with quiet strength — do not take it for granted. Appreciate it every single day. Such leaders are rare, and the mark they leave on you lasts long after you have both moved on.

As for me, I step into the next phase of retirement with a full heart, a grateful spirit, and memories that will last a lifetime. Thank you, Vincent. Truly.
