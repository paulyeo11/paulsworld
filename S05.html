A Retirement Story · 2023 – 2026

S05: Four years in, and the adventure is only beginning.

What I've learned about life, travel, and staying curious after retirement.

Retired March 2023 4 Years of Living

Before I retired, people warned me. "Stop working and you'll die young," some said. Others insisted that after one or two years of travel, life would turn dull and the spark would fade. Four years in, I can tell you — both predictions were wrong.

I retired in March 2023, and from the very first month I made a promise to myself: I would not shrink. I would live fully, learn constantly, and chase every curiosity that called my name.

Family First, Always

The greatest gift of retirement has been time with my mother. Every visit, every quiet conversation, every shared meal — these are the moments I will hold forever. Money can be earned again. Time with the ones we love cannot.

With my wife Joanne, I've travelled to places that have stretched both our hearts and our boundaries. She prefers comfortable journeys, so we share those together — the Greek islands, beautiful coastlines, places where the pace is gentle.

For the rougher adventures — the mountain hikes, the harder terrain, the off-the-map countries — I travel solo. Both kinds of travel feed something different in me, and I have come to love them both.

Climbing to the Roof of the World

One of the most unforgettable journeys of these four years was trekking to Annapurna Base Camp at 4,130 metres. The cold, the thin air, the snow-lit peaks at dawn — I will carry that morning with me forever.

The trail through Nepal was a teacher. Swinging bridges over rushing rivers, mossy stone steps, villages tucked into impossible green valleys — every step reminded me that the body, even at seventy, can still go further than the mind expects.

"Some told me retirement would make me old. Instead, it gave me back the time to truly become myself."

Still Chasing Horizons

This past year alone, I walked the deserts of Egypt beneath the Pyramids of Giza and explored carved cave dwellings of Cappadocia in Turkey. I've stood in places I once thought impossible to reach.

I've sailed to Antarctica in a Zodiac boat through icy silence. I've stood on top of the Gobi Desert sand dunes, arms wide, with nothing but sky above me.

I recently completed a four-day course to fly a 7-kilogram drone — something I never imagined I'd do at seventy. Soon, I'm heading to Japan. My wife will continue on to Australia and New Zealand for a self-drive trip of her own. Later, I'll join two close friends for an eleven-day self-drive expedition across Northern Xinjiang.

Learning Keeps You Young

One of my quiet passions has been learning artificial intelligence. I've spent many hours teaching myself how to work with Claude AI, and I've used it to write about my last four years of travels — preserving each memory in words and photos so that, one day, I can revisit every step of every journey.

This, I believe, is the secret nobody told me: retirement is not the end of growth. It is the beginning of learning purely for the joy of it — with no exam, no deadline, no boss. Just curiosity.

Moments Along the Way

Four years of journeys, captured

To anyone stepping into retirement, or already there — please don't slow down. Travel. Learn. Fly drones. Hike mountains. Sit longer with your mother. Pick up a new language, a new skill, a new continent. The years ahead are not for waiting. They are for living.

— Still moving, still learning, still curious.
