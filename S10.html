31 October 2023 · Mount Ulap, Benguet, Philippines

Some adventures are planned to perfection. Others find you — and leave a mark long after the blisters have healed. My hike up Mount Ulap on 31 October 2023 was very much the latter. My good friend Jeremy and I set off into the darkness before dawn, blissfully unaware of just how much this mountain would test us — and teach us.有些冒险经过精心计划，万无一失。而另一些则不期而遇——在水泡痊愈很久之后，仍在心中留下印记。我在2023年10月31日攀登乌拉普山的经历，正是后者。我和好友Jeremy在黎明前的黑暗中出发，浑然不知这座山将如何考验我们，又将给予我们多少启示。

Mount Ulap, nestled in the highlands of Benguet in the Philippines, is no casual stroll. At 1,800 metres above sea level, with a trail stretching 22 kilometres return, it demands every ounce of preparation a hiker can muster. We had enthusiasm in abundance. What we were slightly short on? Proper footwear.乌拉普山坐落于菲律宾Benguet高地，绝非一条轻松的散步道。海拔1,800米，来回22公里的山路，需要登山者倾尽所有准备。我们热情十足，唯独欠缺一样东西——合适的登山鞋。

Before the Sun日出之前

The alarm rang well before first light. By 6am, we were already on the trail — headlamps barely needed as the Philippine sky began to bruise with the first hints of amber. The morning air was cool and clean, carrying the scent of pine trees that blanket the lower slopes. It was Halloween morning, and the mountain had its own kind of magic.闹钟在天亮前很久便响起。早上六点，我们已经踏上山道——头灯几乎用不上，菲律宾的天空已开始泛出第一抹琥珀色。清晨的空气凉爽清新，带着覆盖低坡的松树芬芳。那是万圣节的早晨，这座山有着它自己的魔力。

The trail begins deceptively gentle — winding through village paths and bamboo groves before the serious climbing begins. But Mount Ulap is known for one thing above all else: its relentless ridge walks. Exposed, grassy, endlessly rolling — beautiful beyond words, and utterly unforgiving to the underprepared.山路起初看似温和，蜿蜒穿过村庄小径和竹林，然后真正的攀登才开始。但乌拉普山最著名的，是那无尽的山脊行走。裸露、遍布青草、绵延起伏——美得无以言表，对准备不足的人却毫不留情。

The 9km Milestone9公里里程碑

Halfway through any long hike, there comes a moment of reckoning. For us, it came at the green-and-yellow diamond sign planted along a rocky trail: 9 KM. We stopped, grinned, and gave a thumbs-up — because at that point, turning back would have been just as far as pushing forward. There is something wonderfully clarifying about that realisation.任何长途徒步到了一半，都会有那么一个时刻让人猛然惊醒。对我们来说，那一刻发生在山路边一块绿黄相间的菱形路牌前：9公里。我们停下脚步，相视而笑，竖起大拇指——因为此时此刻，回头路和前路一样长。意识到这一点，反而有种奇妙的豁然开朗。

Standing on Top of the World站在世界之巅

The signature landmark of Mount Ulap is its dramatic overhanging rock ledge — a cantilever of stone that juts out dramatically over a vast drop, with nothing below but green valleys fading into blue haze. Standing on it feels simultaneously thrilling and slightly mad. We stood there, arms raised, shouting with the pure joy of having climbed all that way.乌拉普山的标志性景点，是那块令人叹为观止的悬空岩石——一块巨石凌空伸出，脚下是万丈深渊，远处是消融于蓝色薄雾中的绿色山谷。站在上面，既令人心跳加速，又带着一丝疯狂的快感。我们张开双臂站在那里，用呐喊释放一路攀爬所积攒的纯粹喜悦。

"At 1,800 metres, with the Philippines spread out below us, even a twisted knee could not dim what we felt in that moment.""在海拔1,800米处，脚下是整片菲律宾大地，即便膝盖扭伤，也丝毫无法削减我们那一刻的感受。"

Paul, 31 October 2023保罗，2023年10月31日

The Slip That Taught Me Everything那次滑倒教会了我一切

I must confess my first mistake openly: I wore Skechers. Not trail runners. Not hiking boots. Skechers — comfortable, flat-soled, and with all the grip of a bar of soap on wet rock. For most of the hike, I compensated with a bamboo walking stick picked up from the trail. But the mountain eventually had the last word.我必须坦白我最大的失误：我穿的是Skechers休闲鞋。不是越野跑鞋，不是登山靴，是Skechers——舒适、平底，在湿滑岩石上的抓地力约等于一块肥皂。大部分路程，我靠着在山道上捡到的竹杖撑了过去。但山最终还是说了最后一句话。

On a particularly rocky section of the descent, my right foot slid. Before I could catch myself, I went down, and my kneecap twisted on impact. It was painful — genuinely painful — but mercifully nothing was broken. I dusted myself off, adjusted my grip on the walking stick, and continued. That is the thing about being on a mountain: you have no choice but to keep going.在下山一段特别崎岖的乱石路上，我的右脚一滑。还没来得及稳住，整个人就摔了下去，膝盖骨在撞击中扭转。那种痛是真实的——实实在在的痛——但幸好没有骨折。我拍掉身上的泥土，重新握紧竹杖，继续前行。这就是身处山中的现实：你别无选择，只能继续走。

⚠️ The Lesson I Learned the Hard Way我用亲身经历换来的教训

  • Always wear proper anti-slip hiking boots — fashion shoes have no place on a mountain trail必须穿防滑登山靴——时尚休闲鞋在山路上毫无立足之地
  • Carry a walking stick — it saved me from worse falls and helped on every steep descent随身携带登山杖——它让我免于更严重的摔跌，在每一段陡坡下山时都助了大力
  • Start early — 6am is ideal for a 22km trail; heat and fading light are real challenges早出发——早上六点出发最为理想；炎热和日落都是真实的挑战
  • Carry more water than you think you need — the ridges are fully exposed to the sun带比你以为需要的更多的水——山脊完全暴露在阳光下，毫无遮挡
  • Respect the mountain — Mount Ulap is rated for beginners, but it is still 1,800m and 22km尊重大山——乌拉普山虽被评为初级路线，但毕竟仍是海拔1,800米、全程22公里的考验

The Views That Made It All Worth It让一切都值得的风景

Words struggle to capture what it is like to look out from a summit in the Cordillera. Layer after layer of green mountain, fading into grey-blue distance, clouds rolling through the valleys far below. Pine trees framing the horizon. The air thin and cool. Fellow hikers sitting quietly, umbrellas open for sun shade, just taking it all in.语言难以描述从科迪勒拉山顶远眺的感受。一层又一层的绿色山峦，渐渐消融于灰蓝色的远方，云雾在远处低谷中翻涌。松树勾勒出天际线，空气稀薄而清凉。同行的登山者静静坐着，撑开阳伞遮阳，就这样沉浸其中，什么都不必说。

After the Mountain下山之后

By the time we reached the trailhead — 11 hours after setting off, legs aching, knee throbbing — we were not thinking about the pain. We were thinking about the view from that rock ledge. About the morning mist in the pine forests. About the wild, exhilarating feeling of standing at 1,800 metres with your arms thrown wide open.当我们终于回到登山口——出发后整整11小时，双腿酸痛，膝盖隐隐作痛——我们想的不是疼痛，而是那块悬空岩石上的壮阔视野，是松林中弥漫的晨雾，是站在海拔1,800米处张开双臂时那种奔放、令人心醉的感觉。

The twisted knee healed within days. And the best part? This hike was just one chapter of a glorious month in the Philippines — beaches, sunrises, sunsets, and long evenings at the spa being thoroughly pampered. Adventure and relaxation in perfect balance.扭伤的膝盖几天之内便痊愈了。而最美好的是？这次徒步只是我在菲律宾那段美好月份的其中一章——海滩、日出、日落，还有在水疗中心被好好宠爱的漫长夜晚。冒险与休闲，恰到好处地交融。

"From that day forward, I have never worn anything but proper hiking boots on a trail. The mountain taught me well.""从那天起，我再也没有穿非登山靴上山。这座山把我教得很彻底。"

Paul — a lesson permanently learned保罗——一个永远铭记的教训

Mount Ulap is not the tallest mountain in the Philippines, nor the hardest. But it is undeniably one of the most beautiful — and for me, one of the most memorable. Not just for the views, but for what it asked of us, and what it gave back in return.乌拉普山不是菲律宾最高的山，也不是最难攀登的。但它无疑是最美丽的山之一——对我而言，也是最难忘的。不仅仅因为那些风景，更因为它对我们的考验，以及它所给予我们的一切。

If you ever find yourself in Benguet with a free day and a pair of proper hiking boots — go. Start at 6am. Carry your walking stick. And when you reach that overhanging rock, raise your arms and shout. You will have earned it.如果你有一天身在Benguet，手头有一天空闲，脚上穿着一双合适的登山靴——去吧。早上六点出发。带上你的登山杖。当你站上那块悬空岩石，张开双臂，大声呼喊。那一刻，你当之无愧。
