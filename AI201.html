My Paulsworld AI Workspace

How one simple folder on my notebook became the nerve centre for all my AI conversations我笔记本上的一个简单文件夹，如何成为我所有AI对话的神经中枢

✍️ Paul Yeo 📅 May 2026 ⏱ 4 min read
"I used to feel scattered. Every AI conversation started from zero — no context, no memory of who I am. Then I watched a video that changed everything. What if I gave the AI a home to come back to?"

🎬 The Video That Sparked It All🎬 点燃这一切的视频

It started with a YouTube video. I was browsing one evening when I came across a tutorial about setting up a personal AI workspace — the idea that you could create a dedicated folder on your computer that any AI tool could read and understand. It was like giving your AI a briefing document about you, before every conversation.

The concept immediately resonated with me. As a 70-year-old learning AI from scratch, I had been frustrated by one thing more than anything else: every time I opened Claude or another AI, I had to re-explain who I am, what I like, what projects I'm working on. It felt exhausting. This video pointed to a better way.

📁 Meet "Paulsworld"

I created a single folder on my notebook computer and named it Paulsworld. Simple, personal, and unmistakably mine. Inside it, I organised everything about my life and interests that I might want an AI to know.

📁 Paulsworld/
  ├── 📂 My Life/  ← Who I am, my background
  │   ├── about-paul.txt
  │   └── my-faith.txt
  ├── 📂 My Hobby/  ← What I love doing
  │   ├── travel-interests.txt
  │   ├── options-trading.txt
  │   └── ai-projects.txt
  └── ... more to come!

The My Life subfolder holds the story of who I am — my background, my faith journey, my family. The My Hobby subfolder captures what I love: travel, options trading on Interactive Brokers, building websites with AI, and exploring the latest AI tools.

⚡ Why This Works So Well⚡ 为什么这么有效

💡 The Key Insight

AI tools like Claude, Codex, and Manus don't have memory by default — but they can read files you give them. By keeping your personal context in a folder, you become the memory. You supply the context; the AI supplies the intelligence.

The beauty of this approach is that it works across every AI tool. It doesn't matter whether I'm talking to Claude on my phone, using Codex for code, or experimenting with Manus for agents — the Paulsworld folder is always there, ready to brief any AI instantly.

🤖 Claude 💻 Codex 🕹 Manus 🌐 ChatGPT

All of these AI tools can access and benefit from my Paulsworld folder. When I start a new conversation, I simply point the AI to the relevant file. Within seconds, it knows I'm a retired 70-year-old who loves travel, trades options, and is passionate about learning AI. No more repeating myself!

· · ·

🛠️ How to Build Your Own🛠️ 如何构建你自己的

You don't need to be a tech expert to do this. If I can do it at 70, you certainly can! Here's how I set it up:

  1. Create a new folder on your computer. Name it something personal — like YourName-world.
  2. Inside it, create a subfolder called My Life. Write a simple text file: who you are, where you live, your family.
  3. Create another subfolder called My Hobby. Write what you love doing, what projects you're working on.
  4. When you open any AI tool, paste or attach the relevant file — and the AI instantly knows your context.
  5. Keep updating your files as your life evolves. Your AI workspace grows with you.
"A folder is just a folder — until you fill it with your life. Then it becomes your AI's memory."

🙏 What It Means to Me🙏 这对我意味着什么

At 70, I am proof that it's never too late to embrace technology. The Paulsworld folder is more than just an organisational tool — it's a declaration. It says: I exist, I have a story, and I want AI to know it.

Every morning when I sit down at my notebook, I feel a quiet excitement. I'm not just using AI — I'm collaborating with it. My Paulsworld folder is the bridge between my human experience and the machine intelligence that helps me do more, create more, and learn more every day.

My faith, my travels, my adventures, my trading strategies, my website — all of it lives in that one little folder. And thanks to the inspiration from that YouTube video, it now talks to every AI I use.

Start Your Own World Today

If you're on your own AI journey — whether you're 25 or 75 — I encourage you to create your own workspace folder. Give your AI a home. Tell it who you are. You'll be amazed at how much better every conversation becomes when the AI already knows your story.
