Retire Early — The Best Gift You Can Give Yourself

How one man's courageous choice at 53 changed the way I look at life, time, and freedom.

By Paul Yeo  ·  2025

There are moments in life when you hear something that quietly shifts everything — not with a loud bang, but with the gentle, undeniable clarity of truth. That is exactly what happened when I came across the speech of Mr. Yusuf Hashim.

Mr. Yusuf Hashim is a remarkable man. He retired at 53 — not out of failure or necessity, but by choice, and at the absolute peak of his career. Most people in that position would push harder, climb higher, accumulate more. But he had the wisdom — and the courage — to stop, look around, and ask: Is this really the life I want to live?

We need to live a balanced life. Don't wait until your body breaks down to finally start enjoying it.

— Mr. Yusuf Hashim

Those words landed like a quiet thunderclap for me. How many people do we know — successful, wealthy, driven — who work tirelessly until their very last breath? Millionaire employers who have more money than they could ever spend, yet cannot stop. They confuse being busy with being alive. They mistake their title for their identity. And one day, without warning, time simply runs out.

Mr. Yusuf's message is not anti-ambition. It is pro-living. He is not saying don't work hard. He is saying: know why you are working, and know when you have done enough. Then have the boldness to choose life over the grind.

💡 This speech lit a fire in me. It reinforced my conviction that retirement is not something you delay until your knees give out or your doctor orders you to stop. It is something you plan for, look forward to, and — when the time is right — step into with joy.

Because of this inspiration, Joanne and I have made the most of our years together. We have done self-drive holidays across beautiful, challenging roads — just the two of us, maps in hand, wind in our faces, no tour guide telling us where to go or when to leave. There is a freedom in that which no itinerary can replicate.

And when I was younger — around 50 — I took things even further. I threw myself into paragliding in Indonesia. Imagine standing on the edge of a ridge, the valley laid out beneath you like a living painting, and then simply stepping off into the sky. It was exhilarating beyond words. I no longer paraglide today — the body has its own say as the years pass — but I carry every one of those flights in my memory, and I am grateful I did not wait.

That is the point. I did not wait.

Too many of us live in a holding pattern — waiting for the right savings number, the right time, the right moment. But the body you have today is the youngest it will ever be again. The legs that can carry you up a mountain trail, the lungs that can gulp in cold Xinjiang air, the eyes that can still be moved to tears by a sunrise over the Kanas Lake — these are gifts with an expiry date.

My message to you is simple: retire early if you can. Not to be idle — idle minds decay. Retire to live intentionally. Travel with your spouse. Learn something new every week. Explore technologies like AI that were unimaginable when you were young. Find adventures that remind you that you are still, wonderfully, alive.

Pamper yourself. Not with excess — with experiences. With presence. With the quiet pride of knowing that when the day comes and you look back, you will say: I lived fully. I chose wisely. I did not wait.
