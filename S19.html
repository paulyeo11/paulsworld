The Decision决定

Eight O'Clock in the Morning, and I Said Yes早上八点，我说了好

8 June 2026  ·  Jungle trail to the summit丛林小道通往山顶

The alarm went off at seven. By eight o'clock I was already at the trailhead, lacing up my shoes in the early morning cool, looking up at a wall of green so thick it swallowed the light. The jungle here is alive with sound — birds calling from somewhere deep in the canopy, insects already at work, the faint rustling of things you do not see. It is a place that makes you feel very small, and very glad to be there.闹钟在七点响起。到八点，我已经站在登山口了，在清晨的凉意中系好鞋带，仰头望着那堵厚密的绿色屏障——茂密得把光都吞了进去。这里的丛林充满生机——鸟儿在树冠深处某个地方鸣唱，虫子已经开始忙碌，还有那些看不见的东西发出的细微沙沙声。这是个让你感到自己渺小，却又庆幸自己在这里的地方。

I am seventy years old. I retired at sixty-seven, and one of the gifts retirement gave me was permission to say yes to things I used to put off. No more waiting for the right time. Today was the right time.我七十岁了。我在六十七岁退休，而退休给我的礼物之一，就是允许自己对那些曾经一拖再拖的事情说"好"。不再等待合适的时机。今天就是合适的时机。

"At seventy, you do not climb a summit to prove something. You climb it to remind yourself what you are still capable of.""七十岁登顶，不是为了证明什么。是为了提醒自己，你还能做到。"

The Climb攀登

Steep Steps, Metal Rails, and One Stubborn Old Man陡峭的台阶、金属扶手，和一个固执的老头

The trail up向上的小道  ·  Wooden steps into the canopy通向树冠的木制台阶

The trail started gently enough — a packed earth path winding between the roots of enormous trees. Then came the steps. Wooden boards bolted into the hillside, with metal handrails on each side, climbing steeply up through the green dark. Each flight levelled out, caught its breath, and then turned up again.小道起初还算平缓——一条踏实的泥土路蜿蜒穿行于参天大树的根系之间。然后台阶出现了。木板钉入山坡，两侧装有金属扶手，在幽暗的绿色中陡然向上延伸。每一段台阶都会短暂平缓，喘一口气，然后再度向上攀去。

I took it slowly. That is the whole secret, and I offer it freely: slow is not stopping. I paused when I needed to, took in the view through the trees, watched the light filter down through the leaves overhead, and then kept going. There is no shame in taking a breath at seventy. There is only shame in giving up — and I did not give up.我走得很慢。这就是全部的秘诀，我毫无保留地分享：慢不等于停下。需要停的时候我就停，透过树丛欣赏风景，仰望阳光穿过头顶树叶洒落的光影，然后继续前行。七十岁喘口气没什么好羞愧的。唯一值得羞愧的是放弃——而我没有放弃。

The trail up — steep steps cutting through dense jungle. Every step earned.向上的山道——陡峭的台阶切入茂密的丛林。每一步都是实打实赚来的。

Looking up the stairway from below, you cannot see where it ends. The steps just disappear into the green. That is either terrifying or motivating, depending on the kind of person you are. I decided, standing there at the bottom with my hands on the rails, that it was motivating.从下方仰望台阶，看不到尽头在哪里。台阶就那样消失在一片绿色之中。这究竟是令人恐惧还是令人振奋，取决于你是哪种人。我站在底部，双手握着扶手，决定：这让我振奋。

The jungle closed in on both sides. Ferns brushed my arms. The air smelled of damp earth and living things. Far above, somewhere I could not yet see, was the top. I kept climbing.丛林从两侧把我包围。蕨类植物拂过我的手臂。空气中弥漫着潮湿泥土和生命的气息。远在高处，某个我尚看不到的地方，就是山顶。我继续攀爬。

My Daily Trail我的日常山道

Dairy Farm Summit — 3,000 Steps Before Breakfast乳农园山顶——早餐前爬3000级台阶

Every morning每天早晨  ·  Near home家附近  ·  Dairy Farm Nature Park

This is not just a one-off adventure. I hike up to Dairy Farm Summit near my house every morning, and it is one of the best decisions I have made in retirement. The trail is 3,000 steps — not a stroll, not a warm-up. By the time I reach the top, I am properly sweating, my heart is going, and my legs have been given a real workout. That is the whole point. At seventy, you do not stay strong by accident. You train.这不只是一次偶然的冒险。我每天早晨都会徒步爬上家附近的Dairy Farm 山顶，这是我退休以来做过的最好决定之一。这条山道有3000级台阶——不是散步，也不是热身。等我到达山顶时，我已经大汗淋漓，心跳加速，双腿得到了真正的锻炼。这正是重点所在。七十岁还能保持强健，靠的不是运气，靠的是训练。

But the 3,000 steps are only part of it. What keeps me coming back, morning after morning, is everything else along the route. I pass beneath beautiful, enormous trees — old ones, deep-rooted, their canopies so high they seem to belong to a different world than the city below. I hear the birds singing from the moment I step onto the trail. Not recorded, not through earphones — real birds, close by, going about their morning as if I were just another creature passing through.但那3000级台阶只是其中一部分。让我日复一日地回来的，是沿途的一切。我穿行于美丽的参天大树之下——那些老树，根系深扎，树冠高耸，仿佛属于另一个与城市全然不同的世界。从踏上山道的那一刻起，我就能听到鸟儿歌唱。不是录音，不是耳机里的声音——是真实的鸟鸣，近在咫尺，它们自顾自地过着早晨，好像我只是路过的另一种生物。

The trail at Dairy Farm — tall trees, dappled morning light, and the climb waiting above.Dairy Farm 的山道——高耸的树木，斑驳的晨光，以及等待着你攀登的高处。

The smell of the forest is something you cannot replicate anywhere else. Damp earth, fallen leaves, living wood — the jungle breathes, and when you are standing in the middle of it in the early morning quiet, you breathe with it. It slows everything down in the best possible way.森林的气味是任何地方都无法复制的。潮湿的泥土、落叶、活着的木头——丛林在呼吸，当你在清晨的寂静中伫立其间，你也随之呼吸。它以最美好的方式，让一切慢下来。

Along the route — trees so old and tall they make the city feel very far away.沿途的风景——古老高大的树木，让城市的喧嚣感觉遥不可及。

And then there is something harder to explain, but I will try. Out here, away from the screens and the concrete and the WiFi and the traffic, I can feel a very different kind of energy. A strong magnetic field — that is the best way I can describe it. A stillness, an aliveness, a sense that the natural world is doing something that we mostly walk straight past. I do not walk past it. I stop, I breathe, I feel it. It is one of the real reasons I come.还有一种更难言说的东西，但我想试着描述。在这里，远离屏幕、混凝土、WiFi和车流，我能感受到一种截然不同的能量。一种强烈的磁场——这是我能想到的最贴切的描述。一种宁静，一种鲜活，一种自然界正在做着某件事而我们通常视而不见的感觉。我不会视而不见。我停下来，深呼吸，用心感受。这是我每天来这里的真正原因之一。

The trail opens up — light through the canopy, birdsong everywhere, and the forest alive around you.山道豁然开朗——光线穿透树冠，鸟鸣四起，四周的森林生机勃勃。

My goal each morning is simple: hike all the way up to the summit, or — on the mornings my legs argue back — at the very least clock 10,000 steps. Some days I make the top with breath to spare. Some days I settle for the count. Either way I have moved, I have sweated, and I have been outdoors among the trees. At seventy, that is a morning well spent.我每天早晨的目标很简单：一路爬到山顶，或者——在双腿开始抗议的早晨——至少走满一万步。有些天我精力充沛地到达山顶；有些天我就满足于步数。无论哪种方式，我都动起来了，都流了汗，都在树木之间待过了。七十岁能有这样的早晨，已是善待自己。

Near the summit — the climb behind me, the city below, and morning air that smells of real forest.接近山顶——攀登已成过去，城市在脚下，晨风里飘着真正森林的气息。

"3,000 steps. Trees that have been standing longer than I have been alive. Birds that do not know I am retired. A forest that does not care what I used to do. That is exactly what I need every morning.""三千级台阶。比我年岁更长的树木。不知道我已退休的鸟儿。不在乎我曾经做过什么的森林。这正是我每天早晨所需要的。"

The Summit山顶

When the Steps Finally Ended当台阶终于走到尽头

Top of the trail山道的顶点  ·  Worth every step每一步都值得

And then, quite suddenly, the steps ended. The trail levelled. The trees opened. I was at the top.然后，毫无预兆地，台阶结束了。山道变得平坦。树木开阔了。我到了山顶。

I stood there for a moment and just breathed. Not just because I needed to catch my breath — though I did — but because the view demanded it. The jungle rolled out below me in every direction, a great ocean of green under the morning sky. A breeze came up from somewhere and cooled my face. The birds were louder here, more confident at the top of their world.我在那里站了一会儿，静静地呼吸。不只是因为需要喘息——虽然确实如此——而是因为眼前的景色让你不得不停下来。丛林在我脚下向四面八方铺展，在晨空之下是一片广阔的绿色海洋。不知从哪里吹来一阵微风，拂凉了我的脸。这里的鸟鸣更为响亮，在属于它们的世界之巅，充满自信。

I thought about the people who told me, kindly and with good intentions, that I should take it easy at my age. I thought about every time I have heard some version of "you're too old for that now." And I stood at the top of a jungle summit at eight-something in the morning, at seventy years old, and smiled to myself.我想起那些好心好意劝我在这个年纪要放轻松的人。我想起每一次听到"你已经太老了"这类话的时候。而我，七十岁的我，在早上八点多，站在丛林山顶，对自己笑了。

"The steps just disappeared into the green. You could not see the top from the bottom. That is probably the best kind of challenge — the kind you have to commit to before you can see how it ends.""台阶就那样消失在绿色之中。从底部看不到山顶。这大概是最好的挑战——那种你必须先全力投入，才能看到结局的挑战。"

I took a long, slow look at everything around me. The haze over the distant hills. The canopy swaying in the breeze below my feet. A bird I could not name gliding in a wide circle on a thermal, completely at ease with the air beneath its wings. I knew exactly how it felt.我慢慢地、仔细地打量着四周的一切。远处山丘上的薄雾。在脚下随风摇曳的树冠。一只我叫不出名字的鸟儿乘着热气流划出一个大大的弧形，从容游弋于翅膀下的气流之间。我完全懂得那种感觉。

🌿 What I've Learned About Hiking at 70🌿 七十岁登山，我学到的事

  1. Start early. The morning cool is your best friend. By midday the heat will win every argument — don't let it. Eight o'clock is perfect.早出发。清晨的凉爽是你最好的朋友。到了中午，酷热会赢得每一场争论——别让它得逞。八点出发正好。
  2. Slow is not stopping. Pace yourself without apology. There is no prize for finishing first; there is only the summit or not the summit.慢不等于停下。放慢步伐，无需道歉。没有第一名的奖励，只有到没到达山顶的区别。
  3. Use the handrails. Pride is not worth a twisted ankle. The rails are there — use them freely and gratefully.利用扶手。自尊心抵不上扭伤脚踝的代价。扶手就在那里——放心用，感恩用。
  4. Rest when you need to. Stop, look around, take in where you are. The pauses are part of the climb, not interruptions to it.需要休息就休息。停下来，环顾四周，感受你所在的地方。暂停是攀登的一部分，不是打断。
  5. Tell someone where you are going. A simple text to Joanne before I set off. It is common sense, not weakness.告诉别人你去哪里。出发前给 Joanne 发一条简短的短信。这是常识，不是软弱。
  6. The view at the top is better because of the steps that got you there. A summit you drove to is a nice view. A summit you climbed is something else entirely.山顶的风景之所以更美，是因为你一步步走上来的。开车到的山顶是一道美景。靠双脚爬上去的山顶，则是另一回事。

What It Means这意味着什么

Retired at 67, Climbing at 7067岁退休，70岁登顶

A reflection at the top of the world山顶的沉思

I retired at sixty-seven. Three years on, I am more active than I was at fifty-five. Retirement, for me, did not mean slowing down — it meant being freed to go faster in the directions that actually matter.我在六十七岁退休。三年后的今天，我比五十五岁时更加活跃。对我而言，退休不意味着慢下来——而是意味着被解放，可以在真正重要的方向上加速前行。

The jungle does not care how old you are. The steps do not get easier because you have a good excuse. But they also do not refuse you. They just sit there, waiting, for whoever is willing to climb them. Today I was willing. Today I was the person standing at the bottom at eight in the morning, looking up at a trail that disappeared into the green, and saying: yes.丛林不在乎你有多少岁。台阶不会因为你有充分的借口就变得容易。但它们也不会拒绝你。它们就在那里等着，等待任何愿意攀登的人。今天，我愿意。今天，我是那个在早上八点站在山脚，仰望那条消失在绿色中的山道，说出"好"的人。

If you are reading this and you have been putting something off — a hike, a trip, an adventure, a challenge that feels too big — let me be the voice that says: go. Go while you can. The steps are waiting. The summit is there.如果你正在读这篇文章，而你一直在推迟某件事——一次远足、一次旅行、一场冒险、一个感觉太难的挑战——就让我来说这句话：去吧。趁还可以的时候去。台阶在等你。山顶就在那里。

You just have to say yes.你只需要说一个"好"字。
