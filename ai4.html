How I Used AI to Build My
Options Trading Dashboard

From a simple question to a live Black-Scholes calculator — built entirely through conversation, no coding needed

✍️ Paul Yeo 📅 May 2026 ⏱ 6 min read 📊 Options · AI · Finance

I am 70 years old, retired since 67, and I own stocks. Not many — just three. But I have been selling covered call options on them to generate extra income every month. The problem? Every time I wanted to figure out the right strike price to sell, I had to manually calculate numbers, look up option chains, and guess whether the premium was worth it.

That all changed when I started using AI — specifically Claude — to build a live dashboard that does all the maths for me. Let me walk you through exactly what I did, and what I learned.

If the stock doesn't reach that price, I keep the premium as income.

"I don't need to be a programmer. I just need to know what I want — and AI figures out how to build it."

The Problem I Was Trying to Solve

Every time I considered selling a covered call, I had the same questions:

❓ My Questions Before Every Trade

  • If I sell a call 10% above today's price, what premium will I earn?
  • What if I go 5% out-of-the-money (OTM) instead? More income but more risk?
  • How does the number of days to expiry (DTE) change the premium?
  • Which combination gives me the best return for the least risk?

Before AI, answering these questions meant looking up option chains on my broker's platform, manually comparing rows of numbers, and doing mental arithmetic. It took 20–30 minutes per stock and I was never confident I had the full picture.

What I Asked Claude to Build

I started with a simple message. No technical jargon. Just plain English:

Me
Compute premium amount for DTE 2 weeks and 20% above strike price for my 3 stocks FDS, POOL and ZTS.
Claude (AI)
Here's your 2-Week DTE Options Premium Calculator based on today's stock prices. Using Black-Scholes model with realistic implied volatility for each stock — FDS: 28%, POOL: 35%, ZTS: 30%...

At 20% OTM with only 14 days, premiums will be very small — essentially lottery tickets. I'd recommend considering 5–10% OTM for meaningful income.

That single conversation gave me not just the numbers — it also educated me. Claude explained why 20% OTM was impractical for a 2-week expiry, and suggested better alternatives. I then asked for more:

Me
Create a dashboard for me to enter any % OTM and compute the premium dynamically.
Claude (AI)
Here's your Dynamic OTM Premium Dashboard with sliders for OTM % and Days to Expiry, a full position table, and a comparison table showing total income at 5%, 10%, 15%, 20%, 25%, 30% OTM levels...

How the Dashboard Was Built — Step by Step

1
I described what I wanted in plain English No coding knowledge required. I told Claude what I needed — a table showing premium income at different OTM percentages for my 3 stocks.
2
Claude built the Black-Scholes calculator The Black-Scholes model is the standard mathematical formula used by professional options traders worldwide. Claude implemented it in code without me understanding a single line of it.
3
I spotted a mistake and asked for a fix The risk label was showing "Moderate" even at settings that were clearly aggressive. I questioned it, Claude explained the bug — the probability was inverted — and fixed it immediately.
4

5
I kept refining through conversation Tab layout, default tab, colour coding, probability labels — every improvement was just one message. Claude remembered the context and updated the right parts each time.

What the Dashboard Shows Me Now

The final dashboard has two tabs. The first shows my live stock portfolio. The second — the OTM Calculator — is where I spend most of my time:

🎯 OTM Calculator Features

  • OTM % slider — drag from 1% to 50%, or type any value
  • DTE slider — set days to expiry from 1 to 90 days
  • Premium per share — for each of my 3 stocks, instantly
  • Total income — what I would collect across all 3 positions
  • Annualised return % — so I can compare strategies fairly
  • Profit probability — chance the call expires worthless (I keep premium)
  • Risk label — 🟢 Safe / 🟡 Moderate / 🔴 Aggressive, based on real probability
  • Comparison table — shows all OTM levels side by side at one glance

What I Discovered About My Own Strategy

Once I had the dashboard, I learned things I never knew before. Here is what the numbers showed me:

OTM % Total Income (14 DTE) Risk Level Verdict
5% $253.76 🟢 Safe (76% prob) ✅ Best balance
10% $38.58 🟡 Moderate ⚠️ Low income
15% $4.04 🔴 Aggressive ❌ Not worth it
20%+ $0.32 🔴 Aggressive ❌ Lottery ticket

The insight was clear: 5% OTM with 14–21 days to expiry gives the best income with the highest probability of keeping the premium. Before this dashboard, I might have blindly sold 20% OTM calls thinking I was being "safe" — when in reality I was earning almost nothing.

What I Learned About Using AI

Be specific Ask follow-up questions Challenge wrong answers Iterate step by step You are the boss

The biggest lesson: AI is not magic, and it does make mistakes. My risk label was wrong at first — it said "Moderate" when the real probability told a different story. But when I questioned it, Claude admitted the bug, explained the correct logic, and fixed it. That back-and-forth is how good work gets done.

As a 70-year-old retiree with no programming background, I found this empowering. I did not need to learn to code. I needed to learn to think clearly — to describe what I want, recognise when something looks wrong, and ask good questions. That is a skill I already had.
