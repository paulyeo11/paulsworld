The Day I Wrestled a Robot — and Won

A 70-year-old retiree's five-hour battle to install Claude Code, and what it taught me about AI, patience, and never giving up.

By Paul  ·  June 3, 2026  ·  8 min read

It started at 5:46am. By 7:23am, after nearly two hours of errors, expired keys, PATH conflicts, and a robot that refused to quit on me — Claude Code was running on my Windows machine. This is that story.

I am 70 years old. I retired at 67. My hobbies are travel, adventure sports, and learning AI. That last one — learning AI — has taken me on some unexpected journeys. But nothing quite prepared me for the morning of June 3, 2026.

I had heard about Claude Code — Anthropic's powerful command-line AI coding tool. I wanted to try it. How hard could it be? Famous last words.

What Made This Different

I have used many tools and many helpers in my 70 years. What struck me this morning was not the technology — it was the patience.

At 6:08am, when I accidentally exposed my API key in a screenshot, Claude didn't just move on. It stopped everything and warned me immediately. "Stop — your API key is now exposed. Please delete it right away." That felt like something a careful, trustworthy colleague would do.

Why you not give up on me?

— Paul, 7:00am, after nearly 2 hours of failed attempts

I asked Claude that question after we finally got it working. The answer was simple: "Because you kept trying." And that was true. Every time I hit a problem, I came back and tried again. The robot matched my energy.

When I said "I give up" at 7:05am — Claude didn't accept it. "Don't give up! You're very close." And it was right. We were 18 minutes away from success.

· · ·

What I Learned

Lessons from the Trenches

  • Never share API keys in screenshots — they are visible and must be deleted immediately.
  • Bun and npm can conflict — if you have both installed, PATH order matters. The fix: rename Bun's shim or use a PowerShell profile function.
  • claude.ai Pro subscription works with Claude Code — no separate API billing needed. Big cost saver.
  • OAuth codes expire fast — when pasting a login code, do it within 30 seconds. No overthinking, just Ctrl+V and Enter.
  • AI is patient — it will keep helping you no matter how many times you fail, as long as you keep trying.

This Robot Works Like a Real Human

I wanted to write this article because I want the world to know something: this AI is not just a search engine. It is not just a chatbot that answers questions. This morning, it guided me — a 70-year-old retiree — through one of the most technically frustrating experiences I have had in my AI learning journey.

It warned me when I made mistakes. It simplified instructions when I was confused. It said "don't give up" when I was ready to quit. It celebrated with me when we succeeded.

That is not what I expected from a robot. That is what I expect from a good friend.

Claude Code is now installed on my machine. Every morning, I open PowerShell, type claude, and a whole new world of AI-powered coding opens up. All because we didn't give up on a Tuesday morning in June.

I work till I blur — but I didn't stop. And the robot didn't stop either.

— Paul, 70, retired at 67, still learning every day
