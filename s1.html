S1: Lost in MacRitchie

The Day Police Rescued Me From the Forest

By Paul Yeo · Age 67 · Singapore

I have hiked in the Swiss Alps. I have navigated solo through Portugal. I have trekked across remote trails in Asia. But the most terrifying adventure I have ever had was right here in Singapore — just a few kilometres from my own house.

On April 10, 2023, my friend and I got completely lost inside MacRitchie Nature Trail and Reservoir Park. What followed was a rescue operation involving the Singapore Police Force, the Gurkha Contingent, the Aerial Response Team, and officers from NParks and PUB. We made the national news.

This is the full story — told honestly, with a little humour.

🥾 How It All Started — A Walk From Home

It started simply enough. My friend, aged 51, and I decided to go for a hike. We left my house at 8am feeling confident and well-prepared — or so we thought. MacRitchie is a well-known nature reserve in the heart of Singapore. Families go there. School children go there. It did not seem dangerous.

We did not bring enough water. We did not have offline maps. We did not tell anyone exactly which trail we were taking. Three classic mistakes — and we made all three before 9am.

⛩️ Stuck at the Japanese Temple

At 3pm, we reached the Ruins of Syonan Jinja — the old Japanese shrine deep inside the reserve. It was an interesting landmark — but we had a serious problem. We could not find the trail back out. Every path looked the same. The jungle was dense, the light was fading, and my phone battery was at 12%.

We tried three different paths. Each time, we ended up back at the same spot. We were going in circles. That was the moment I admitted to myself: we are genuinely lost.

🌧️ The Rain Comes — and So Does the Reality

Then it started to rain heavily. We were soaked, exhausted, and completely off the trail. My friend's battery was dead. Mine was barely alive. We called 999.

🚁

Within the hour: Singapore Police Force · Gurkha Contingent · Aerial Response Team · NParks · PUB were all deployed. It became a full search-and-rescue operation broadcast on national news.

🚔 The Rescue Boat Arrives

The Singapore Police Force deployed officers from multiple units. A rescue boat came through the reservoir. The Aerial Response Team swept overhead with drones. We were found at dusk — cold, embarrassed, but completely safe. The officers were professional and kind. They did not scold us.

"When the rescue team arrived, I was so happy — it felt better than winning Toto!"

📰 We Made the National News

📋 What I Learned — The Hard Way

MacRitchie taught me that overconfidence is the most dangerous thing you can bring into a forest. Here is what I now carry on every single hike — no exceptions:

🎒 Paul's Hike Checklist (Learned the Hard Way)

  • 📱 Offline maps downloaded before you leave (Maps.me or AllTrails)
  • 🔋 Fully charged power bank — always
  • 💧 At least 1.5 litres of water per person
  • 📍 Tell someone your exact trail and expected return time
  • 🌦️ Check the weather — Singapore rain is not gentle
  • 🆘 Know the emergency number: 999 (Singapore)

I am 67 years old. I still hike. I still explore. But now I do it smarter — with offline maps, a fully charged power bank, and a healthy respect for nature. The forest does not care how many countries you have visited. It will humble you every time.
