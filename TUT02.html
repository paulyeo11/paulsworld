TUT02: How to Build Your Own Claude Travel Agent — Step-by-Step Setup Guide

Turn Claude into a personal trip planner that researches hotels, food, and routes for you把Claude变成个人旅行规划师，为你研究酒店、美食和路线

Paul Yeo  ·  June 2026  ·  Tutorials Series

I built a travel agent called NOVA who helps me plan every trip — from Xinjiang self-drive routes to Tokyo hotel shortlists. She is not a real person. She is Claude, given a clear job, a clear style, and the right tools. This guide shows you exactly how to build your own version.

You do not need to know how to code. You do not need a developer. You need three things: a clear description of the job you want done, a way to give Claude live information (maps, search), and a habit of saving what works so you do not have to repeat yourself every time.

· · ·

Step 1 — Write the Job Description

Start by writing a short "persona" for your travel agent, the same way you would brief a real travel agent before a trip. Open a new chat with Claude and paste something like this:

You are my personal travel agent. Your job is to help me plan trips: researching destinations, comparing hotels, suggesting daily itineraries, finding local food, and warning me about visa or weather issues. Always ask about my budget, travel dates, and pace (relaxed vs packed) before suggesting an itinerary. Give me options in a table, not a wall of text.

— A starting persona prompt you can copy and edit

That single paragraph changes everything. Claude now knows the role, the output format you expect, and the questions to ask before answering. Save this prompt somewhere — a note, a Google Doc, a Notion page — so you can paste it at the start of every new travel-planning chat.

· · ·

Step 2 — Give It Your Travel Context

A good travel agent should not ask the same questions every trip. Add a short paragraph about your general preferences once, and reuse it:

  • Who is travelling (just you, a couple, family with kids)
  • Typical budget range per trip
  • Pace — do you want 2 cities a week, or one slow base?
  • Mobility or dietary needs
  • What you love (hiking, museums, street food, self-drive)

I keep mine in a single file: "Paul travels mostly self-drive, prefers mid-range hotels with good reviews, loves adventure sports and local food, and is planning trips to Xinjiang and Japan in 2026." One paste, and every plan Claude gives me already fits my life.

· · ·

Step 3 — Connect Live Tools

A travel agent that only knows what it learned during training is guessing about today's prices and opening hours. To make it genuinely useful, give it access to current information:

Ways to Connect Live Travel Data

1
Web search Turn on web search in your Claude app or use Claude Code with web search enabled — this lets it check current hotel prices, reviews, and travel advisories instead of guessing.
2
Maps & accommodation MCP tools If you use Claude Code, you can connect MCP servers for accommodation search and maps. These let Claude pull real listings and distances instead of generic suggestions.
3
Your own notes Paste in flight times, visa rules, or a packing list from a past trip. Claude reuses anything you give it, which saves you retyping every time.

If you are not using Claude Code and just have the regular Claude app, web search alone is enough to get most of the value — just make sure it is switched on before you ask for current prices or opening hours.

· · ·

Step 4 — Ask for Useful Output, Not Just Text

The biggest improvement I made was asking for structured output instead of paragraphs. Try prompts like:

  • "Give me a table comparing 5 hotels in Niseko: price, distance to ski lift, rating, breakfast included."
  • "Build me a day-by-day itinerary for 7 days in Xinjiang, self-drive, with driving times between stops."
  • "What should I pack for a 16-day road trip through Xinjiang in September? Group by category."
  • "Compare flying into Urumqi vs Kashgar for this route — pros and cons."

Tables and structured lists are easier to scan on your phone, easier to compare, and easier to save into a trip document later.

Step 5 — Save and Reuse What Works

Every time Claude produces a good itinerary or packing list, save it — in Notion, a Google Doc, or even just a pinned note. Next trip, you are not starting from zero; you are editing a template that already fits your travel style.

The first trip you plan this way takes effort. By the third trip, you are just editing dates and destinations into a system that already knows how you like to travel.

Usage Tips

  • 1Turn on web search before asking about prices, opening hours, or anything time-sensitive.
  • 2Always state your dates and budget upfront — vague requests get vague answers.
  • 3Ask for tables when comparing options — much easier to read on a phone.
  • 4Double-check anything critical — visa rules, vaccination requirements, and border crossing details should be verified on an official source before you travel.
  • 5Keep one master travel-preferences note and paste it into every new trip-planning chat.
· · ·

Q&A

Q: Do I need Claude Code, or does the regular app work?
A: The regular Claude app with web search switched on covers most travel planning — destination research, itineraries, packing lists. Claude Code with MCP tools adds live accommodation search, but it is optional.
Q: Can it actually book flights or hotels for me?
A: No — Claude researches and compares, but you still complete the booking yourself on the airline or hotel website. Think of it as the research stage, not the checkout stage.
Q: How accurate are the prices and details it gives me?
A: With web search turned on, it is usually close to current, but prices change fast. Always confirm the final price on the booking site before you pay.
Q: Is this safe — do I need to share my passport or payment details?
A: Never share passport numbers, payment details, or passwords with Claude. It does not need them to research and plan a trip.
· · ·

Final Thoughts

You do not need a real travel agency to get a personalised trip plan anymore. You need ten minutes to write a clear job description, a paragraph about how you like to travel, and the habit of asking for tables instead of paragraphs. The rest builds itself, trip after trip.

Your Starting Point

Open a new Claude chat right now and paste in the persona prompt from Step 1. Then describe your next trip in one sentence. That is your travel agent, born.
