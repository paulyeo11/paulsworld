Why You Should Retire at 65.

A personal message from someone who waited until 70 — and learned a few things along the way.

"I retired at 70. I wish I'd done it at 65."

Five extra years of working felt responsible at the time. But looking back, those were five years I could have spent travelling with Joanne, climbing new peaks, and learning AI while my mind was even sharper. If you're reading this and you're around 65 — please, don't wait.

🌅 Five Reasons to Retire at 65

1. Your health is at its peak window

At 65 you still have the energy for real adventure — hiking, diving, long-haul flights. Wait until 70 or 75 and the body simply does less, no matter how willing the mind is. Use the good years for the bold things.

2. Time with the people you love is finite

My wife Joanne and I have shared 38+ countries together. Every trip is precious, and we don't know how many more we'll get. Retiring earlier means more sunsets, more meals, more memories with your spouse and family.

3. Freedom to chase passions

I've taken up AI learning, adventure sports, and writing — none of which I had time for during my working years. Retirement isn't an ending. It's the start of the most interesting chapter, if you let it be.

4. Money is rarely the real problem

Most people work past 65 out of habit or fear, not need. Sit down with your numbers honestly. You probably have enough. What you don't have an endless supply of is time.

5. The world changes fast — go see it now

Places I visited 10 years ago are unrecognisable today. Travel while destinations, your knees, and the planet are still as you remember them.

💛 A Message to Friends Over 65

If you're still working and you don't have to be — this is your sign. The next ten years can be the richest of your life, but only if you claim them. Retire. Travel. Learn something wild. Call your old friends. Take that trip you keep postponing. I promise you won't regret it.

— Paul Yeo, retired at 70, age 70 and still going 🚀
