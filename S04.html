S04: 10 Unforgettable Days in Iceland

A June 2025 adventure through the Land of Fire and Ice — by a 70-year-old traveler who believes the best adventures are still ahead

In June 2025, my friends and I embarked on a 10-day self-guided journey across Iceland — a country that had been on my bucket list for years. At 70 years young, I can honestly say this trip reaffirmed my belief that travel and adventure have no age limit. From snow-capped mountains to roaring waterfalls, from black sand beaches to steaming geothermal lagoons, Iceland delivered beyond every expectation. Here is my story, and why I strongly recommend everyone visit this magical island at least once in their lifetime.

The Land of Fire and Ice

Iceland is unlike anywhere else on Earth. One moment you are driving through lush green pastures with isolated farmhouses tucked beneath snow-covered peaks, and the next you are standing in a moonscape of black volcanic rock. The contrasts are simply breathtaking.

One of our earliest stops gave us our first taste of the iconic Icelandic countryside — emerald-green meadows dotted with wildflowers, rustic red-roofed farmhouses, and a wall of snowy mountains rising behind them like a painted backdrop. Even in June, snow still clung to the high peaks.

Bruarfoss — The Bluest Waterfall

One of the highlights of our southern Iceland journey was the magnificent Bruarfoss waterfall, famous for its incredibly vivid turquoise-blue glacial waters. The way the milky-blue water cascades through layers of black volcanic rock is mesmerizing. We stood there for a long time, simply watching the water flow — it felt almost unreal, like a painting come to life.

Whale Watching off Akureyri

One of the most thrilling experiences of the trip was a whale watching tour off the coast of Akureyri, Iceland's northern capital. Dressed in bright red thermal suits, our group headed out into the cold Arctic waters — and we were rewarded with sightings of humpback whales. Standing on the deck with snow-capped mountains behind us, it was a moment none of us will ever forget.

Goðafoss — The Waterfall of the Gods

The name says it all. Goðafoss — the Waterfall of the Gods — is one of Iceland's most spectacular and historically significant waterfalls. According to legend, when Iceland converted to Christianity in 1000 AD, the lawspeaker threw his Norse pagan idols into these very falls. Standing at the edge, watching the glacial water thunder over the horseshoe-shaped cliff, you feel a deep sense of awe.

Ásbyrgi and the Power of Geology

Travelling north, we explored the legendary Ásbyrgi Canyon and surrounding areas, where towering basalt rock formations rise dramatically from the earth. Standing at the base of these ancient volcanic giants, you feel incredibly small — and incredibly humbled. According to Norse legend, Ásbyrgi was formed by the hoof of Odin's eight-legged horse, Sleipnir.

We even spotted some of Iceland's beautiful native birdlife — small red-necked phalaropes resting on rocks in crystal-clear pools, completely unbothered by our presence.

Stuðlagil Canyon — Basalt Like You've Never Seen

One of the most jaw-dropping geological wonders we visited was Stuðlagil Canyon — a gorge lined with perfectly symmetrical curved basalt columns that seem almost too beautiful to be natural. The columns fan out in sweeping arcs, like a frozen waterfall of stone. It is one of Iceland's newest tourist attractions, only exposed after a river was diverted.

The Westfjords — Iceland's Hidden Gem

The Westfjords were perhaps the most atmospheric part of our journey. Mist rolled in from the sea, partially veiling the steep cliffs as they plunged into still, silvery fjords. It felt mysterious, quiet, and deeply peaceful — a side of Iceland that fewer tourists ever see.

When the fog lifted slightly, we were treated to spectacular views of mountains capped with rolling clouds, fields of purple lupines stretching to the water's edge, and small fishing boats gliding silently across the fjord.

"At 70, I felt as alive as I did at 30. Adventure and travel keep the body young and the spirit even younger."

Reykjavík — City of Culture and Character

Our base at the start and end of the trip was Reykjavík, the world's most northerly capital. Despite its small size, the city has a huge personality — colourful houses, great restaurants, world-class museums, and the iconic Hallgrímskirkja church towering over everything like a concrete rocket ship.

Reynisfjara — The Black Sand Beach

No visit to Iceland is complete without standing on the legendary black sand beach of Reynisfjara near Vík. The dramatic basalt column cliffs, formed by ancient lava cooling into hexagonal shapes, look like something built by giants. The contrast of jet-black sand, blue Atlantic waves, and the green hillside above is unforgettable.

A word of caution: the "sneaker waves" here can be dangerous, so always keep a safe distance from the water.

Hiking the Volcanic Trails

One of my proudest moments was hiking the volcanic trails on the Reykjanes Peninsula, near the site of recent eruptions. Walking through fields of fresh lava rock, with the sun shining and a cool breeze on my face, was exhilarating. The freshly cooled black lava, still showing reddish veins of iron, stretched as far as the eye could see.

The Food — A Delicious Surprise

Iceland surprised me not only with its scenery but also with its incredible food. Fresh seafood was a daily highlight — Arctic char, langoustine, salted cod, and the famous Icelandic lamb soup (kjötsúpa) warmed us after cold, windy days. The skyr (Icelandic yogurt) was creamy and delicious, and the rye bread baked using geothermal heat was unforgettable.

Every meal felt fresh, hearty, and made with love. The food alone is worth the trip.

Final Thoughts — Go While You Can

Ten days in Iceland passed far too quickly. We drove, hiked, soaked, ate, and marveled at one of the most extraordinary countries on this planet. The scenery is endlessly beautiful, the food is wonderful, and the people are warm and welcoming.

To anyone reading this — young or old — I strongly recommend you visit Iceland at least once in your lifetime. Don't wait. Adventure is not just for the young. It is for the curious, the brave, and the grateful. At 70, I can promise you: it is absolutely worth every step.

Iceland, takk fyrir mig — thank you. Until we meet again.

Trip taken June 2025 with dear friends. Memories will last forever.
