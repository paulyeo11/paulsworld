At 2,073 m elevation, this brilliant blue alpine lake is the highest and largest alpine lake in Xinjiang. The water is so intensely blue it looks artificially dyed. The surrounding meadows are dotted with Kazakh yurts. A drive-around or cycle of the lake takes 2вҖ“3 hours.

Many Kazakh families around the lake offer yurt stays and traditional meals вҖ” fresh bread (naan), lamb skewers, and kumiss (fermented mare's milk). A genuine encounter with nomadic culture on the shores of a mountain lake.
If staying overnight at the lake, ask for a lakeside yurt вҖ” worth the extra ВҘ50вҖ“100. Yurt temperature drops sharply after sunset.

Hashilagen Pass (3,600 m)
е“ҲеёҢеӢ’ж №иҫҫеқӮ
The highest point of the Duku Highway вҖ” a dramatic high-altitude pass at 3,600 m where snow can appear even in September. The road winds through towering peaks, glacial valleys, and sheer cliff faces. This is the centrepiece of the entire highway.
Check the highway permit and weather status the night before. The pass can be closed by snow or road works. Drive slow вҖ” altitude affects both drivers and engines.

Near Kuqa, this stunning red sandstone canyon has towering walls of orange and crimson. Walk the canyon floor path (2вҖ“3 km loop) between walls that rise 100+ metres. The colours glow intensely in afternoon light.

Nalati means "first place to see the sun" in Mongolian вҖ” and the view from this elevated grassland plateau at 1,800 m justifies the name. Rolling hills covered with lush alpine meadow, herds of horses and sheep, and snow peaks as a backdrop. The scenic area has cable cars and horse-riding options.
Entry ВҘ135. The cable car gives stunning aerial views. Hire a horse for ВҘ150вҖ“300/hour to ride into the meadows. Best in morning light.

A UNESCO-listed wetland and China's only natural habitat of whooper swans. The broad valley floor holds a maze of meandering rivers and wetland pools. Sunset here вҖ” called the "Nine Bends and Eighteen Curves" вҖ” is one of the most photographed scenes in Xinjiang.

Kuqa Grand Mosque
еә“иҪҰеӨ§жё…зңҹеҜә
One of the largest mosques in Xinjiang, with a striking yellow-domed architecture blending Central Asian and Islamic styles. Built in 1923, it can hold 2,000 worshippers. The Friday bazaar outside the mosque gates is one of the most authentic in southern Xinjiang.
Modest dress required. Non-Muslim visitors welcome outside prayer times. The Friday market (Fridays 10 AMвҖ“2 PM) is the most vibrant time to visit.

Kizil Thousand Buddha Caves
е…Ӣеӯңе°”еҚғдҪӣжҙһ
One of the oldest Buddhist cave temple complexes in China (3rdвҖ“8th century AD), predating Dunhuang. Over 350 caves carved into red cliffs above the Muzart River, with stunning Buddhist murals. A UNESCO World Heritage Site on the ancient Silk Road.
Guided tour required (included in ВҘ75 entry). About 65 km from Kuqa вҖ” allow half a day. Photography inside caves is restricted.

Kuqa Old City Bazaar
еә“иҪҰеҸӨеҹҺе·ҙжүҺ
A labyrinth of narrow lanes packed with Uyghur bakers, blacksmiths, dried-fruit sellers, and spice merchants. The smell of freshly baked naan (ВҘ1вҖ“2 each!) and lamb kebabs is everywhere. This is living Silk Road culture.
Try samsa (lamb-filled pastry) fresh from the tandir oven. The best naan in Xinjiang is sold at the stalls near the mosque gate in the morning.

Jiaohe Ancient Ruins
дәӨжІіж•…еҹҺ
The world's largest, oldest, and best-preserved earthen city вҖ” a 2,000-year-old cliff city carved entirely from solid earth (not built with bricks). Once a major Silk Road city-state, now a haunting UNESCO World Heritage ghost town rising from the desert floor.
Entry ВҘ75. Visit early morning before it heats up вҖ” Turpan is China's hottest city (often 40В°C+ in summer, but cooler in September). Wear a hat and bring water.

Flaming Mountains
зҒ«з„°еұұ
The scorched red sandstone ridges made famous in the classic Chinese novel "Journey to the West." At peak summer the surface temperature exceeds 70В°C, but in September it's dramatic without being lethal. The scenic area has a giant thermometer and Tang Monk statue.
Roadside views are free. Entry to the main scenic area ВҘ40. The red rock glows most vividly in late afternoon light.

Turpan Grape Valley
еҗҗйІҒз•Әи‘Ўиҗ„жІҹ
A lush oasis valley cutting through the desert вҖ” kilometres of grapevine trellises heavy with fruit in September. Turpan grapes are famous across China. Walk through the vine corridors, taste fresh grapes and raisins, and see the ancient karez irrigation channels.
Entry ВҘ60. September is harvest season вҖ” the best time to visit. Buy dried raisins and apricots direct from farmers at much lower prices than tourist shops.

Terracotta Warriors Museum
е…өй©¬дҝ‘еҚҡзү©йҰҶ
One of the greatest archaeological discoveries of the 20th century вҖ” over 8,000 life-sized terracotta soldiers, horses, and chariots buried with China's first emperor Qin Shi Huang in 210 BC. Pit 1 alone contains over 6,000 figures. Allow 3вҖ“4 hours minimum to do it justice.
Book tickets in advance online (quota system вҖ” walk-in tickets often sold out). Hire an English audio guide (ВҘ40). Avoid weekends. Go to Pit 3 first вҖ” it's least crowded and most intimate.

Xi'an Muslim Quarter & Food Street
еӣһж°‘иЎ—
A vibrant maze of food stalls, mosques, and craft shops in the heart of Xi'an's Hui Muslim community. Must-eats: roujiamo (Chinese burger), lamb skewers, biangbiang noodles (the noodle with the most complex Chinese character), and sweet persimmon cakes.
Go hungry. Evening is best вҖ” stalls light up and the energy is incredible. Expect crowds but it's worth it. The Great Mosque inside the quarter is also worth a look.
