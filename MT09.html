🛖 Hemu Village 禾木村
⭐ Must See · Day 4
▼
Hemu Village Panorama View
禾木全景观景台
One of China's most photogenic villages — wooden Tuvan houses wrapped in birch and larch forest. Morning mist rolling over the valley at sunrise is unforgettable. The hilltop viewpoint gives a sweeping panorama over the entire valley.
Climb the viewpoint hill at sunrise (around 6:30 AM). Bring warm layers — it drops below 10°C in early September.
📸 Best at sunrise
Tuvan Village Life & Horse Riding
图瓦人村落与骑马
Hemu is home to the Tuvan ethnic minority. Walk the village lanes past log-cabin homes with smoke rising from chimneys. Horse riding through birch forests along the Hemu River is a bucket-list experience.
Horse rides cost ¥100–200/hour from local guides near the bridge. Negotiate directly with herders, not tour operators.
🐎 Local experience
5
🏞️ Kanas Lake 喀纳斯湖
⭐ Must See · Day 5
▼
Kanas Lake — Jewel of Xinjiang
喀纳斯湖景区
A glacial lake of shifting colours — emerald, turquoise, and deep blue depending on the light. Surrounded by ancient Siberian forest of pine, birch, and larch turning golden in early September. Home to the legendary "Kanas Lake Monster" (actually giant Hucho taimen fish).
Take the scenic bus to Fish Viewing Pavilion (观鱼台) at the top — best panoramic view of the lake. Entry ¥245 per person. Start early to beat tour groups.
🌊 Unmissable
Fish Viewing Pavilion Hike
观鱼台徒步
A 2 km uphill hike (or cable car option) leads to the 1,374 m hilltop observation deck overlooking the entire lake and valley. On clear days the view extends into Russia and Kazakhstan.
Cable car costs extra ~¥80. The hike takes 40–50 minutes each way but the views en route are excellent. Wear good shoes.
🥾 Hike or cable car
8
💎 Sayram Lake 赛里木湖
⭐ Must See · Day 8
▼
Sayram Lake — The Last Tear of the Atlantic
赛里木湖 — 大西洋最后一滴眼泪
At 2,073 m elevation, this brilliant blue alpine lake is the highest and largest alpine lake in Xinjiang. The water is so intensely blue it looks artificially dyed. The surrounding meadows are dotted with Kazakh yurts. A drive-around or cycle of the lake takes 2–3 hours.
Stop at the north shore rest area for the best photo angles. Avoid the main entrance area which is crowded. Morning light hits the west shore perfectly.
💙 Stunning blue
Kazakh Yurt Experience
哈萨克毡房体验
Many Kazakh families around the lake offer yurt stays and traditional meals — fresh bread (naan), lamb skewers, and kumiss (fermented mare's milk). A genuine encounter with nomadic culture on the shores of a mountain lake.
If staying overnight at the lake, ask for a lakeside yurt — worth the extra ¥50–100. Yurt temperature drops sharply after sunset.
🏕️ Authentic nomad culture

🏔️ Duku Highway 独库公路
⭐ Must See · Day 10 — Dushanzi → Kuqa
▼
Hashilagen Pass (3,600 m)
哈希勒根达坂
The highest point of the Duku Highway — a dramatic high-altitude pass at 3,600 m where snow can appear even in September. The road winds through towering peaks, glacial valleys, and sheer cliff faces. This is the centrepiece of the entire highway.
Check the highway permit and weather status the night before. The pass can be closed by snow or road works. Drive slow — altitude affects both drivers and engines.
🏔️ Highest point of route
Tianshan Grand Canyon
天山大峡谷（克孜利亚峡谷）
Near Kuqa, this stunning red sandstone canyon has towering walls of orange and crimson. Walk the canyon floor path (2–3 km loop) between walls that rise 100+ metres. The colours glow intensely in afternoon light.
Visit 2–4 PM for the best red-wall lighting. Entry ¥75. The canyon can get crowded midday — early morning is quieter.
🟥 Red canyon walls
Nalati Grassland (Bayanbulak to Kuqa section)
那拉提草原
The Duku Highway passes through some of Xinjiang's finest highland meadows — vast rolling grasslands where Kazakh herders graze their flocks. The contrast between meadow green, mountain snow, and blue sky is extraordinary.
Stop at any wide shoulder with a valley view — these unplanned stops often produce the best photos. Don't rush this 500 km road — allow 8–10 hours including stops.
🌿 Drive-through marvel
10
🌿 Nalati Grassland 那拉提草原
⭐ Must See · Day 10–11
▼
Nalati Sky Grassland
那拉提空中草原
Nalati means "first place to see the sun" in Mongolian — and the view from this elevated grassland plateau at 1,800 m justifies the name. Rolling hills covered with lush alpine meadow, herds of horses and sheep, and snow peaks as a backdrop. The scenic area has cable cars and horse-riding options.
Entry ¥135. The cable car gives stunning aerial views. Hire a horse for ¥150–300/hour to ride into the meadows. Best in morning light.
🌄 Sky meadow
Bayanbulak Swan Lake
巴音布鲁克天鹅湖
A UNESCO-listed wetland and China's only natural habitat of whooper swans. The broad valley floor holds a maze of meandering rivers and wetland pools. Sunset here — called the "Nine Bends and Eighteen Curves" — is one of the most photographed scenes in Xinjiang.
Sunset viewing platform opens from about 6 PM. Entry ¥90. The "9 bends" sunset is spectacular — plan a 1-hour stop at minimum.
🦢 Sunset classic
12
🕌 Kuqa City 库车市
⭐ Must See · Day 12–13
▼
Kuqa Grand Mosque
库车大清真寺
One of the largest mosques in Xinjiang, with a striking yellow-domed architecture blending Central Asian and Islamic styles. Built in 1923, it can hold 2,000 worshippers. The Friday bazaar outside the mosque gates is one of the most authentic in southern Xinjiang.
Modest dress required. Non-Muslim visitors welcome outside prayer times. The Friday market (Fridays 10 AM–2 PM) is the most vibrant time to visit.
🕌 Silk Road heritage
Kizil Thousand Buddha Caves
克孜尔千佛洞
One of the oldest Buddhist cave temple complexes in China (3rd–8th century AD), predating Dunhuang. Over 350 caves carved into red cliffs above the Muzart River, with stunning Buddhist murals. A UNESCO World Heritage Site on the ancient Silk Road.
Guided tour required (included in ¥75 entry). About 65 km from Kuqa — allow half a day. Photography inside caves is restricted.
🏺 UNESCO heritage
Kuqa Old City Bazaar
库车古城巴扎
A labyrinth of narrow lanes packed with Uyghur bakers, blacksmiths, dried-fruit sellers, and spice merchants. The smell of freshly baked naan (¥1–2 each!) and lamb kebabs is everywhere. This is living Silk Road culture.
Try samsa (lamb-filled pastry) fresh from the tandir oven. The best naan in Xinjiang is sold at the stalls near the mosque gate in the morning.
🫓 Best naan in XJ
D15
🏜️ Turpan Basin 吐鲁番
⭐ Must See · Day 14–15 (Korla → Urumqi route)
▼
Jiaohe Ancient Ruins
交河故城
The world's largest, oldest, and best-preserved earthen city — a 2,000-year-old cliff city carved entirely from solid earth (not built with bricks). Once a major Silk Road city-state, now a haunting UNESCO World Heritage ghost town rising from the desert floor.
Entry ¥75. Visit early morning before it heats up — Turpan is China's hottest city (often 40°C+ in summer, but cooler in September). Wear a hat and bring water.
🏛️ UNESCO ancient city
Flaming Mountains
火焰山
The scorched red sandstone ridges made famous in the classic Chinese novel "Journey to the West." At peak summer the surface temperature exceeds 70°C, but in September it's dramatic without being lethal. The scenic area has a giant thermometer and Tang Monk statue.
Roadside views are free. Entry to the main scenic area ¥40. The red rock glows most vividly in late afternoon light.
🔥 Journey to the West
Turpan Grape Valley
吐鲁番葡萄沟
A lush oasis valley cutting through the desert — kilometres of grapevine trellises heavy with fruit in September. Turpan grapes are famous across China. Walk through the vine corridors, taste fresh grapes and raisins, and see the ancient karez irrigation channels.
Entry ¥60. September is harvest season — the best time to visit. Buy dried raisins and apricots direct from farmers at much lower prices than tourist shops.
🍇 Harvest season

🏺 Xi'an Bonus 西安
Pre-trip · 29–31 Aug (transit days)
▼
Terracotta Warriors Museum
兵马俑博物馆
One of the greatest archaeological discoveries of the 20th century — over 8,000 life-sized terracotta soldiers, horses, and chariots buried with China's first emperor Qin Shi Huang in 210 BC. Pit 1 alone contains over 6,000 figures. Allow 3–4 hours minimum to do it justice.
Book tickets in advance online (quota system — walk-in tickets often sold out). Hire an English audio guide (¥40). Avoid weekends. Go to Pit 3 first — it's least crowded and most intimate.
🏺 World Wonder
Xi'an Muslim Quarter & Food Street
回民街
A vibrant maze of food stalls, mosques, and craft shops in the heart of Xi'an's Hui Muslim community. Must-eats: roujiamo (Chinese burger), lamb skewers, biangbiang noodles (the noodle with the most complex Chinese character), and sweet persimmon cakes.
Go hungry. Evening is best — stalls light up and the energy is incredible. Expect crowds but it's worth it. The Great Mosque inside the quarter is also worth a look.
🍢 Street food heaven
