AC01: How Toastmasters Changed My Life at 70 — 50 Speeches, 2 Pathways, and Counting

At 67, I walked into my first Toastmasters meeting not knowing how to draft a speech. Five years later, I have delivered over 50 speeches and completed 2 full pathways.

By Paul Yeo  ·  Singapore 🇸🇬  ·  2021–2026

In 2021, I made a decision that changed my life. I joined Toastmasters — not because I was confident, but because I was not. I wanted to learn how to speak in public, improve my Chinese, and challenge myself mentally. What I got was all of that — and so much more.

Five years later, I have delivered over 50 speeches, competed in 3 speech competitions, completed 2 full pathways, and made friendships that keep me young at heart.

Why I Joined Toastmasters

I have always believed that learning should never stop — regardless of age. After retirement, I realised that one area I had never truly developed was public speaking, especially in Chinese. I wanted to be able to stand in front of a crowd, speak confidently, and connect with people in my own language.

In 2021, I joined Queenstown Toastmasters Club. Then I expanded to 2 more clubs, giving me exposure to different audiences, different speaking styles, and a much wider network of friends.

"I did not join Toastmasters because I was confident. I joined because I wanted to become confident. There is a big difference."

The Hardest Part — The Beginning

The first few months were genuinely difficult. I did not know how to structure a speech. There were Chinese words I did not know how to pronounce correctly. Standing in front of an audience — even a small, supportive Toastmasters audience — was nerve-wracking at first.

But I kept showing up. Every single meeting. Every speech got a little easier. Every evaluation helped me improve. The Toastmasters environment is one of the most supportive and encouraging communities I have ever been part of.

My Most Memorable Speech — Bangkok 2004

Of all the speeches I have delivered, one stands out above the rest. I shared the story of my posting to Bangkok in 2004 — a chapter of my life full of unexpected adventures, cultural surprises, and moments that I had never shared publicly before.

The audience response was something I will never forget. There was laughter. There was attention. People came up to me afterwards to share their own stories. That speech taught me the power of personal storytelling — and gave me the confidence to keep going.

"When the audience laughed and leaned forward — that was the moment I knew I had found my voice."

— PAUL YEO, QUEENSTOWN TOASTMASTERS CLUB

What Toastmasters Gave Me Beyond Speaking

Yes, Toastmasters improved my public speaking. But what surprised me most was everything else it gave me — friendships, confidence, mental sharpness, and a reason to keep growing.

Every meeting is a workout for the mind. You listen, you evaluate, you think on your feet during Table Topics. At 70 years old, I leave every Toastmasters meeting feeling more energised than when I arrived.

My Message to People My Age

If you are retired and thinking that your learning days are over — I want to challenge that belief. Toastmasters is not just for young professionals. It is for anyone who wants to grow, connect, and stay mentally active.

We should be socialising. We should be making new friends. We should be putting ourselves in rooms where we are challenged and encouraged. This is what keeps us young at heart — not just the body, but the mind and the spirit.

I started Toastmasters at 67. You can start at any age. The only requirement is the willingness to show up and try.

"Socialise. Make new friends. Keep learning. This is what keeps us young at heart — age is just a number."

— PAUL YEO, 70, SINGAPORE 🇸🇬
