A 70-year-old retiree's story of building a professional data dashboard in just one hour using AI.

I am 70 years old, retired, and I had never written a single line of code in my life. Yet in just one hour, I built a fully interactive business intelligence dashboard with charts, filters, and real data insights — all using Claude AI.

This is the story of how I did it, and why I believe anyone can do the same.

The YouTube Video That Started It All

Everything started when I watched a YouTube tutorial on data dashboards. That video inspired me to try building one myself — but using AI instead of traditional tools.

After watching this video, I thought — "Can I build something like this using Claude AI, without any coding knowledge?" The answer turned out to be yes!

Why I Wanted to Build a Dashboard

I had always been curious about data analysis. In my working days, I relied on others to produce reports and charts. After retirement, I decided to learn how to do it myself — but traditional tools like Power BI felt overwhelming for a beginner.

Then I discovered Claude AI. I decided to try an experiment — could I describe what I wanted in plain English and have AI build it for me?

"I told Claude exactly what I wanted — in plain English — and it wrote all the code. No programming knowledge required."

The Data I Used

I used the classic SuperStore sample dataset — a fictional retail company's sales data covering years 2018 to 2021 across the United States. It contains nearly 10,000 sales records across categories like Technology, Furniture, and Office Supplies.

This is a well-known dataset used by data professionals to practice analytics. Perfect for a beginner like me to learn with.

How I Did It — Step by Step

The hardest part was not the technology — it was learning how to write good prompts. Here is exactly how I approached it:

1
Describe what I wanted. I told Claude: "Build me a business dashboard for retail sales data. I want to see total revenue, profit, monthly trends, and regional performance."
2
Review the output. Claude produced a complete HTML file with all the charts and data embedded. I opened it in my browser and checked if it looked right.
3
Refine with more prompts. If something was not right, I described the problem to Claude and asked it to fix it. No technical knowledge needed — just clear descriptions.
4
Add filters and interactivity. I asked Claude to add dropdown filters for Year, Region, Segment, and Category so users could explore the data themselves.
5
Publish online. I uploaded the final file to GitHub and deployed it on Vercel — both free — and shared the link with friends.

What the Dashboard Contains

After one hour of working with Claude, here is what I ended up with:

💰 Total Revenue — $2.84M across 9,994 line items
📈 Monthly Sales & Profit Trend — dual-axis line chart
🗺️ Regional Sales Performance — horizontal bar chart
📊 Profit Margin by Sub-Category — colour-coded bars
🔥 Monthly Orders Heatmap — colour intensity by volume
🧠 Automated AI Insights — category leaders and alerts
🎛️ Interactive Filters — Year, Region, Segment, Category

The Biggest Challenge — Writing Good Prompts

The hardest part of this project was not technical at all. It was learning how to communicate clearly with Claude. I learned that the more specific and detailed my instructions were, the better the results.

For example, instead of saying "make a chart", I learned to say "create a dual-axis line chart showing monthly sales on the left axis and monthly profit on the right axis, with sales as a solid blue line and profit as a dashed green line."

"The skill is not coding. The skill is communication. If you can describe what you want clearly, AI can build it for you."

What I Learned From This Experience

This project completely changed how I think about technology. At 70 years old, I proved to myself — and hopefully to you — that age is no barrier to learning new skills.

AI tools like Claude have made it possible for anyone, regardless of technical background, to build professional tools that would have required a team of developers just a few years ago.

I am now working on more AI projects — including AI-generated videos, automated scheduling, and more dashboards. The journey has just begun!
