The Quarry Walk
Singapore's Hidden Turquoise Lake

采石场漫步
新加坡隐秘的碧绿湖泊

By Paul Yeo  ·  27 June 2026  ·  Dairy Farm Nature Park, Singapore 郑保罗 著  ·  2026年6月27日  ·  新加坡乳农园自然公园

I set off this morning looking for a different route to Bukit Timah Summit. I ended up at a flooded quarry instead — turquoise water in the middle of the forest, still and deep and completely unexpected. Best change of plan I've made in a long time.

今天早上我出发时，原本想找一条通往武吉知马山顶的不同路线。结果却来到了一个被水淹没的采石场——森林深处的碧绿湖水，静谧而深邃，完全出乎意料。这是我很久以来做过的最棒的临时改变。

The Plan That Changed改变的计划

I have been trying different routes around Bukit Timah — four weeks ago I climbed the summit via the Dairy Farm Trail, and I wanted to find another way in. This morning I walked into Dairy Farm Nature Park from the Petir Road entrance, with a rough idea of heading toward the summit from the north side.

过去几周，我一直在尝试武吉知马周边的不同路线——四周前我经由乳农园步道登上了山顶，今天我想找另一条进山的路。今天早上，我从白沙路入口走进乳农园自然公园，大概打算从北侧朝山顶方向前进。

But the park had other ideas. The NParks trail map at the entrance showed something I had not noticed before: the Singapore Quarry, sitting in the southeastern corner of the park, marked on the map with a small patch of blue. I had walked past that map board many times on my way to the summit. Today, for the first time, I actually stopped and read it.

但公园有它自己的安排。入口处的公园步道地图上显示了一样我以前没有注意到的东西：新加坡采石场，位于公园的东南角，地图上用一小块蓝色标注。我在去山顶的路上经过那块地图牌子好多次了。今天，是第一次，我真正停下来仔细看了它。

I changed direction. Instead of the summit, I would go to the quarry.

我改变了方向。不去山顶了，去采石场。

The Outdoor Class户外健身课

A few minutes into the walk I came across something that made me smile: an outdoor fitness class in full swing at one of the park shelters. Eight or ten people, yoga mats rolled out on the concrete, doing push-ups and stretches under the shelter roof while the forest rose up green and enormous behind them. A trainer calling out instructions. Everyone working hard in the morning humidity.

走了几分钟后，我遇到了一幅让我忍不住微笑的景象：公园的一个有盖亭子里，一堂户外健身课正热火朝天地进行着。八九个人，瑜伽垫铺在水泥地上，在亭顶下做俯卧撑和拉伸，身后是绿意盎然、高大茂密的森林。教练大声喊着指令，每个人都在晨间湿热中努力地锻炼。

I am 70 years old. I watched them from the path and thought: this is exactly right. People of all ages, in a park, on a Saturday morning, choosing to move their bodies rather than sit at home. Singapore does this well. The parks are full of people using them — runners, cyclists, families, fitness classes, old men like me walking slowly and taking photographs.

我七十岁了。我站在小路上看着他们，心想：这真的很对。各个年龄层的人，在一个周六早晨，在公园里，选择活动身体而不是待在家里。新加坡在这一点上做得很好。公园里到处都是使用它的人——跑步者、骑行者、家庭，健身班，还有像我这样慢慢走着、不时拍照的老头。

"A park is only as good as the people who use it. Dairy Farm was full this morning — and every one of them had chosen movement over stillness. That is not nothing."

"一座公园的价值，取决于使用它的人。今天早上，乳农园里人头攒动——每一个人都选择了活动而非静止。这并不是微不足道的事。"

Through the Forest穿越森林

Past the fitness class, the trail narrows and the forest closes in. This is secondary forest — trees that grew back after the quarrying stopped, filling in what was once cleared land with decades of patient growth. The canopy is thick here, the light green and diffuse. You can hear the forest before you can see deep into it — birds, insects, the distant sound of something moving through leaves.

经过健身课的场地之后，步道变窄了，森林也随之靠拢。这是次生林——采石场停工后重新生长的树木，几十年来耐心地填满了曾被开垦的土地。这里树冠浓密，光线绿柔而漫散。你先听到森林，才看到它的深处——鸟鸣、虫声，还有某种生灵穿越树叶的遥远声响。

I followed the Dairy Farm Loop southward, past junctions and trail markers, the path rising and falling gently. Singapore's forests are not dramatic — no great cliffs, no towering peaks. But they are alive in a way that never stops surprising me. Even on a well-worn trail, every walk is different. Different light, different sounds, different things sitting still long enough to be noticed.

我沿着乳农园环线向南走，经过一个个路口和步道标志，小路缓缓起伏。新加坡的森林并不壮观——没有巨大的悬崖，没有高耸的山峰。但它充满生机，这一点从未停止让我惊喜。即便是走过多次的步道，每一次都不同。不同的光线、不同的声音，不同的事物静静等待着被发现。

The Quarry采石场

And then — the trees thinned, and I saw water.

然后——树木稀疏了，我看见了水。

Singapore Quarry. An old granite quarry, abandoned long ago, now completely filled with groundwater. The colour stopped me. Turquoise — vivid, clear, deep turquoise, the kind of colour you expect from a mountain lake in New Zealand or a glacial pool in Iceland. Not in the middle of a Singapore forest, twenty minutes' walk from a housing estate.

新加坡采石场。一座很久以前废弃的花岗岩采石场，如今完全被地下水填满。那颜色让我驻足。碧绿——鲜艳、清澈、深沉的碧绿，那种你在新西兰高山湖或冰岛冰川池才会期待看到的色彩。不是在新加坡森林的中央，步行二十分钟就能到达的地方。

The walls of the quarry drop straight down — sheer faces of pale granite, cut clean by workers and blasting decades ago, now softened by moss and fern wherever a seed has found a crack to root in. The water is completely still. No current, no wind ripple. Just flat, turquoise glass reflecting the sky and the trees above.

采石场的岩壁笔直向下延伸——几十年前工人和爆破留下的平整花岗岩面，如今凡是种子能扎根的裂缝，都已被苔藓和蕨类植物覆盖，变得柔和了。水面完全静止。没有水流，没有风吹起的涟漪。只有平坦的碧绿玻璃，映照着天空和上方的树木。

I stood at the edge and looked down for a long time. Not thinking about summits. Not thinking about routes or distances or how many steps I had done. Just looking at the water.

我站在边缘，低头凝视了很久。没有想山顶的事，没有想路线、距离，或者我走了多少步。只是看着水。

"I have stood at Annapurna Base Camp at 4,130 metres. I have reached the summit of Bukit Timah. Today I stood at the edge of an old quarry filled with turquoise water — and I felt the same quiet that those summits gave me."

"我曾站在4130米的安纳普尔纳大本营，也登上过武吉知马山顶。今天，我站在一个盛满碧绿湖水的旧采石场边缘——感受到了与那些山顶同样的宁静。"

What an Old Quarry Teaches You旧采石场教你的事

This quarry once supplied granite for building Singapore. Workers drilled and blasted here — the noise, the dust, the industry of a young country constructing itself from the ground up. Now it is the quietest place I have been in months. Nature moved back in slowly, without announcement, and turned a wound in the earth into something beautiful.

这座采石场曾为建设新加坡提供花岗岩。工人们在这里钻孔爆破——噪音、尘土、一个年轻国家从地底建设自己的劳作。如今，这里是我几个月来去过的最安静的地方。大自然悄悄地回归，没有任何宣告，将大地上的伤痕变成了美丽的存在。

There is a lesson in that. Things that are used hard and then abandoned do not always stay broken. Sometimes they fill up. Sometimes the moss grows back. Sometimes turquoise water finds its way in, and what was a scar becomes a lake.

这其中有一个道理。被用尽、被遗弃的东西，并不总是保持破碎的模样。有时它们会被填满。有时苔藓会重新生长。有时碧绿的水会悄然而来，曾经的伤疤变成了一片湖泊。

I am 70 years old, retired, and I walked to a quarry this morning instead of a summit. I stood at the water's edge for longer than I planned. I walked back slowly, through the forest, past the fitness class still going strong, back to the trail map at the entrance that started all of this.

我七十岁了，已退休。今天早上，我去的是采石场，而不是山顶。我在水边站的时间，比计划的要长。我慢慢走回来，穿越森林，经过仍在坚持上课的健身班，回到一切起点——入口处的那块步道地图牌。

📍 Zheng Xing Desserts, Beauty World 🗺️ Google 百度地图 高德地图

And then I walked to Beauty World Food Centre and had a bowl of Tau Suan at Zheng Xing Desserts. Warm sweet mung beans in ginger broth, fried dough on top, eaten standing in sweaty hiking shoes while the Saturday morning crowd moved around me. The quarry still in my head. The Tau Suan warm in my hands.

然后我走到美世界熟食中心，在正兴甜品吃了一碗豆爽。温热的绿豆瓣配着姜味甜汤，上面铺着炸面饼，我穿着汗湿的登山鞋站着吃，周围是周六早晨熙攘的人群。采石场的景象还留在脑海里，豆爽的温热还在手心里。

It was a good morning. Not because I achieved anything. Because I stopped, read a map, changed my plan, followed the water — and ended with Tau Suan.

那是一个美好的早晨。不是因为我完成了什么，而是因为我停了下来，看了一张地图，改变了计划，跟着水走——然后以一碗豆爽收尾。
