The Hidden Quarry
Hindhede's Turquoise Gem

隐秘采石场
兴德德那碧绿宝石

By Paul Yeo  ·  27 June 2026  ·  Hindhede Nature Park, Singapore郑保罗 著  ·  2026年6月27日  ·  新加坡兴德德那自然公园

I came looking for a different route to Bukit Timah Summit. What I found instead stopped me completely — a flooded granite quarry, still as glass, turquoise and deep, hidden inside the forest.

我原本是想找一条通往武吉知马山顶的不同路线。然而我所发现的，却让我驻足许久——一个被水淹没的花岗岩采石场，水面平静如镜，碧绿而深邃，隐匿在森林深处。

A Change of Plan计划有变

This morning I set off from Hindhede Drive with no fixed plan. I had been thinking about the summit — about taking a different route from the one I walked in June when I climbed Bukit Timah via the Dairy Farm Trail. But the forest had a different idea for me today.

今天早上，我从兴德路出发，没有固定计划。我一直在想着山顶——想着走一条与六月爬武吉知马山时不同的路线。但今天，森林为我安排了另一番际遇。

The trail into Hindhede Nature Park begins quietly. A narrow path between trees, the morning light filtering in sideways, the air heavy and warm. Birds I could hear but not see. The usual Singapore forest sounds — the kind that make you feel, for a moment, that the city around you does not exist.

进入兴德德那自然公园的小径静悄悄的。一条穿梭于树木间的窄路，晨光从侧面斜斜透入，空气闷热潮湿。鸟鸣声四起，却看不到鸟的踪影。那是新加坡森林特有的声响——让你短暂忘却四周城市喧嚣的声音。

The Quarry Appears采石场出现了

And then, around a gentle bend in the trail, the trees opened up — and I saw it.

然后，在小径的一个弯道处，树木突然向两侧展开——我看见了它。

Hindhede Quarry. An old granite quarry, abandoned decades ago, now filled entirely with groundwater. The walls drop steeply on all sides — sheer grey rock faces descending into water that is turquoise and impossibly clear. From the viewing platform at the edge, you can look straight down into the depths, and the colour of the water shifts as your eyes move — from pale green near the surface to deep blue-grey far below.

兴德德那采石场。一座废弃了几十年的花岗岩采石场，如今完全被地下水填满。四周岩壁陡峭垂直——灰色的石面笔直延伸入水中，水色碧绿透彻，清澈得令人难以置信。从边缘的观景台往下望，可以直视水底深处，随着视线移动，水色也随之变幻——近表面处是淡绿，深处则渐转蓝灰。

I stood there for a long time. Not photographing. Not moving. Just standing.

我在那里站了很久。没有拍照。没有走动。只是站着。

"I came looking for a summit. I found still water instead. And somehow — that was exactly right."

"我来寻找山顶，却找到了静水。而不知为何——这正好是我所需要的。"

What Granite Becomes花岗岩的归宿

This quarry supplied granite for building Singapore — for roads, for buildings, for sea walls. Workers came here every day to drill and blast and haul the stone out of the earth. The noise must have been enormous. The machines, the dust, the constant industry of a young nation building itself from the ground up.

这座采石场曾为建设新加坡提供花岗岩——用于道路、建筑、海堤。工人们每天来到这里钻孔、爆破、将石料运出地面。那时的噪音一定震耳欲聋。机器声、尘土飞扬，一个年轻国家正从地下开采着自己的基石。

Now it is silence. The quarry closed, the machinery left, the water crept in slowly over the years — and nature reclaimed what had been taken from it. Rainforest grew back along the edges. Birds nested in the rock faces. The water turned green and still and beautiful.

如今，是寂静。采石场关闭了，机器离去，多年来水慢慢渗入——大自然重新夺回了曾被带走的一切。热带雨林沿着边缘重新生长，鸟儿在岩壁上筑巢，水变得碧绿、静谧而美丽。

There is something deeply hopeful about this. A wound in the earth, healed by rain and time. A place of industry, returned to stillness. Singapore built itself with granite like this. And now the quarry rests, full of water, calm under the morning sky.

这其中有一种深刻的希望。大地上的伤痕，被雨水和时间愈合。一处喧嚣之地，重归宁静。新加坡以这样的花岗岩建造了自己，而今采石场静静休憩，满蓄着水，在晨空下安然沉睡。

Different from a Summit与山顶不同

A summit gives you a view outward — you stand high and look far, at a horizon that stretches in every direction. The feeling is one of conquest, of having earned something by climbing.

山顶给你向外的视野——你站在高处，向远处眺望，地平线向四面八方延展。那种感觉是征服，是通过攀爬赢得的某种东西。

A quarry gives you something different. You stand at the edge and look inward — down into the earth, into still water, into depth. The feeling is not conquest. It is contemplation.

采石场给你的是不同的东西。你站在边缘，向内凝视——往下，进入大地，进入静水，进入深处。那种感觉不是征服，而是沉思。

I have climbed summits on five continents. I have stood at Annapurna Base Camp, at the top of Bukit Timah, at viewpoints in Iceland and Japan and the Philippines. Those moments are real and they changed me. But this morning, at seventy years old, standing at the edge of an old quarry filled with green water, I felt something different — a kind of peace that does not require height to reach.

我已在五大洲登顶过山峰。我曾站上安纳普尔纳大本营，登上武吉知马之巅，站在冰岛、日本、菲律宾的观景台上。那些时刻都是真实的，它们改变了我。但今天早上，七十岁的我站在一个被绿水盈满的旧采石场边缘，感受到的却是另一种东西——一种不需要高度就能抵达的平静。

"Not every beautiful place requires you to climb. Some of the most striking things are found by looking down."

"并非每一处美景都需要你攀登。有些最动人的东西，是低头俯视才能发现的。"

Walking Back走回去

I stayed at the quarry viewpoint for a long while — longer than I planned. The morning was warming up, the humidity rising, and other visitors were beginning to arrive. Families with children. Joggers who slowed to look, then ran on. A couple with cameras, spending a long time at the railing.

我在采石场观景台待了很久——比原计划更久。早晨渐渐热了起来，湿度在上升，其他游客也开始陆续到来。带着孩子的家庭，慢下脚步看了看又继续跑的慢跑者，一对带着相机在护栏旁久久驻足的情侣。

I walked back through the forest the way I had come. The same path, but different now — my mind slower, my eyes quieter. On the way in I had been thinking about summits. On the way out I thought about water, about granite, about how a country builds itself and what it leaves behind.

我沿原路穿越森林走了回去。同一条小径，却感觉不同了——思绪更慢，目光更平静。来时，我满脑子想的是山顶。回去时，我想的是水，是花岗岩，是一个国家如何建设自己，又留下了什么。

I am 70 years old. I retired at 67. I have more free mornings than I can count, and I try to spend each one doing something that will matter when the day is over. Today: a quarry, a change of plan, and still water under a grey sky. That is enough. That is more than enough.

我七十岁了，六十七岁退休。我拥有数不清的自由早晨，我尽量把每一个早晨都用来做一些在一天结束后仍有意义的事。今天：一座采石场，一次计划改变，以及灰色天空下的静水。这就足够了。更是绰绰有余。
