They Said This Bridge Is for Animals.
I Climbed It Anyway. 他们说这座桥是为动物而建——
我还是爬上去了

By Paul Yeo & Mr Po  ·  21 May 2022  ·  Eco-Link@BKE, Singapore Paul Yeo 与 Po先生  ·  2022年5月21日  ·  新加坡
📍  See the Map查看地图 📖  Read the Story阅读故事

Singapore has a bridge officially built for animals to cross the BKE expressway. Most people have never heard of it. I not only walked across it — I found the secret entrance and climbed to the top. What I found up there changed the story completely. 新加坡有一座桥，官方说法是为动物穿越BKE快速公路而建。大多数人从未听说过它。我不仅走过了它——我还找到了秘密入口，爬上了桥顶。我在上面发现的，彻底改变了这个故事。

How It Started — A Wednesday Morning in May故事的开头——五月的一个星期三早晨

It was 21 May 2022. My friend Mr Po called me up. He had heard about a trail near Rifle Range Road — one that most people do not know about. He said: "Paul, you want to go and explore?" I said yes. That is how it starts. Two retired men with nowhere they have to be, heading into the jungle on a weekday morning.

那是2022年5月21日。我的朋友Po先生打电话给我。他听说靶场路附近有一条小径——一条大多数人不知道的小径。他说："Paul，要不要去探探？"我说好。就是这么开始的。两个退休男人，没有任何非去不可的地方，在工作日的早晨走进了丛林。

We did not know exactly what we would find. I had heard vaguely about the Eco-Link@BKE — the bridge that Singapore built for animals to cross the expressway. But I had never been. Neither had Mr Po. So we went to find it.

我们不知道会发现什么。我隐约听说过生态廊道@BKE——新加坡为动物穿越快速公路而建的桥。但我从未去过，Po先生也没有。于是我们就去找了。

The Secret Entrance — Remember This Spot秘密入口——记住这个地方

Most people who visit the Eco-Link@BKE walk across it from ground level. I found a way up to the top — but it took some exploring to find the right entry point. Look for the yellow circle marker on the ground. That is where you go in.

大多数到访生态廊道@BKE的人只是从地面走过。我找到了一条通往顶部的路——但要找到正确的入口需要一些探索。找地上那个黄色圆圈标记。那就是入口。

Starting the Trail开始徒步

The trail begins modestly — a metal-grated walkway cutting through shade, surrounded by jungle. No signs. No crowds. Most people driving past on Rifle Range Road have no idea this path is here. That is exactly why it is worth walking.

小径以朴素的方式开始——一条穿越阴凉的金属栅格步道，四周被丛林环绕。没有指示牌，没有人群。大多数驾车经过靶场路的人，根本不知道这条路的存在。这正是它值得一走的原因。

The trail entrance — quiet, shaded, and unknown to most. This is where the hike begins. 小径入口——安静、阴凉、鲜为人知。徒步就从这里开始。

The Military Zone军事禁区边缘

Within minutes you realise this is no ordinary nature trail. Long rows of yellow-and-black bollards mark the boundary of Rifle Range Road Camp 1. Then the old military hardware: rusty circular rings arranged in a line beside the path, overgrown with moss. Nobody moved them. The jungle is slowly claiming them back.

没走几分钟你就会意识到，这不是一条普通的自然步道。一排排黄黑相间的警示柱标示着靶场路一号营地的边界。然后是旧军事装备：锈迹斑斑的圆形铁环沿小径排列，苔藓丛生。没人搬走它们。丛林正慢慢将它们收回。

Rusty military rings in a row — forgotten remnants锈迹斑斑的军事铁环——被遗忘的遗物
Yellow-black bollards — army camp boundary黄黑警示柱——军营边界
The jungle opens into a wide green field beside the camp — perfectly maintained. Only the military keeps land this tidy inside dense jungle. 丛林豁然开朗，营地旁是一片宽阔的绿色草地，维护整洁。只有军队才能在茂密丛林中保持这样的整洁。
🔒 Rifle Range Road Camp 1 — What They Don't Tell You靶场路一号营地——他们没有告诉你的

A contractor who worked inside this camp told me: the garden inside is beautiful — manicured, green, well-kept. You would never guess from the plain gate. And according to those who know: somewhere beneath the camp lies an underground ammunition depot, designed to be undetectable from satellite imagery. The jungle canopy hides what Singapore does not advertise.

一位曾在营地施工的承包商告诉我：里面的花园很漂亮——修剪整洁、绿意盎然。从外面那扇普通铁门根本猜不到。据内情人士透露：营地某处地下设有弹药库，专门设计成无法被卫星侦测。丛林树冠遮住了新加坡不对外公布的一切。

The WWII Bunker & the Stone Cairn二战碉堡与石堆

Tucked beside the trail — easy to miss — is a solid British concrete bunker from World War II. Thick walls, a sealed metal hatch on top. Built in 1941-42 as part of Singapore's defensive line against Japanese advance. On top of it, hikers over many years have stacked flat stones into a cairn. In Buddhist tradition, each stone is a meditation: a symbol of balance, a wish for good luck.

小径旁隐藏着一座英国人建造的二战混凝土碉堡。厚实的墙壁，顶部封着铁盖，建于1941至42年，是新加坡抵御日军南下的防线一部分。碉堡顶上，多年来徒步者们叠起石堆。在佛教传统中，每块石头都是一次冥想：象征平衡，祈求好运。

A WWII British bunker, hidden in the jungle — with a Buddhist stone cairn on top built by hikers over many years. War gives way to peace, one stone at a time. 丛林中隐藏的二战英国碉堡——顶上是多年来徒步者堆砌的佛教石堆。战争一块石头一块石头地化为平和。

"War gives way to peace — one stone at a time.""战争一块石头一块石头地化为平和。"

The Fallen Tree — The Last Test倒塌的大树——最后的考验

Just before the bridge, the jungle throws up one last obstacle. A massive tree had fallen across the path — trunk wide as a car, roots ripped from the earth, branches still bearing green leaves. You have to duck underneath and push through. The forest tests you before it rewards you.

就在抵达桥前，丛林抛出最后一个障碍。一棵巨大的树横倒在小径上——树干宽如汽车，根部被连根拔起，枝桠上仍挂着绿叶。你必须弯腰钻过去。丛林在奖励你之前先考验你。

A massive fallen tree blocks the path just before the bridge. Duck under and keep going. 就在桥前，一棵巨树横倒在路上。弯腰钻过，继续前行。

The Bridge — Two Worlds, One Span那座桥——两个世界，一道跨越

Proof I was there — standing right under the Eco-Link@BKE sign with the expressway behind me. 21 May 2022. 证明我到过那里——站在生态廊道@BKE标志正下方，身后是快速公路。2022年5月21日。

And then you reach it. The Eco-Link@BKE — an elegant span covered in greenery, stretching across six lanes of expressway. Step on it and the world changes. The city noise falls away. Leaves rustle. You are walking through a living corridor of forest, suspended above the BKE. Look through the railings and you see it: cars rushing below at highway speed. Completely unaware of you standing silently above them.

然后你到了。生态廊道@BKE——一道覆盖着植被的优雅桥梁，横跨六线快速公路。踏上它，世界变了。城市的噪音消退。树叶沙沙作响。你正穿行于一条悬浮在BKE上空的活森林走廊。透过栏杆俯视：车辆在脚下以公路速度疾驰。完全不知道你正静静站在他们头顶。

Arriving at the Eco-Link@BKE — officially a wildlife crossing. Officially. 抵达生态廊道@BKE——官方说法是野生动物过桥。官方说法。
Standing on the bridge, looking straight down. The BKE in full flow — above: silence. Below: the city in full motion. 站在桥上径直俯视。BKE车流涌动——上方：寂静。下方：城市全速运转。
Me and Mr Po on the Eco-Link@BKE, 21 May 2022. Big smiles. We made it across. This is what retirement looks like. 我和Po先生在生态廊道@BKE桥上，2022年5月21日。笑得灿烂。我们成功穿越了。这就是退休生活的样子。

"Above: silence, leaves, the smell of forest. Below: the BKE at full throttle. Two worlds, one thin bridge between them.""上方：寂静、树叶、森林的气息。下方：全速行驶的BKE。两个世界，中间只隔一座薄薄的桥。"

I Climbed to the Top — And Now I Have Questions我爬上了桥顶——现在我有了疑问

Most people only see the Eco-Link from the road or from the pedestrian walkway underneath. I found the secret entrance and climbed up onto the bridge surface itself. Up there, it is covered in leaf litter, bare soil, trees, and metal mesh fencing on the sides.

大多数人只从公路或下方步行道看过这座桥。我找到了秘密入口，爬上了桥面本身。上面覆盖着落叶、裸土、树木，两侧是金属网围栏。

And then I noticed something: a fan. There is mechanical equipment running on the bridge. And I started wondering — if there is a fan running up here, creating noise and vibration and air movement, how comfortable can any animal really be while crossing? Is the Eco-Link@BKE truly the peaceful wildlife sanctuary it claims to be? Or does it serve a second purpose that Singapore does not publicise?

然后我注意到了什么：一台风扇。桥上有机械设备在运转。我开始想——如果这里有风扇在运转，制造噪音、震动和气流，动物在穿越时能感到舒适吗？生态廊道@BKE真的是它所声称的那片宁静野生动物廊道吗？还是它还承担着新加坡不公开宣传的第二个用途？

The top of the Eco-Link@BKE bridge — leaf litter, mesh fencing, trees. And a fan running up here. Wildlife sanctuary? You decide. 生态廊道@BKE桥顶——落叶、网围栏、树木。还有一台运转中的风扇。野生动物廊道？你来判断。
❓ The Question Nobody Is Asking没人在问的问题

The Eco-Link@BKE cost millions to build. It has a beautiful official story about reconnecting wildlife habitats. But if there is mechanical equipment — fans, ventilation, infrastructure — running on the bridge itself, this raises a real question: what is this bridge actually for? Wildlife? Military monitoring? Something else? Singapore is very good at building things with more than one purpose. And very good at not telling you about the second one.

生态廊道@BKE耗资数百万修建。它有一个关于重新连接野生动物栖息地的美丽官方故事。但如果桥上有机械设备——风扇、通风装置、基础设施——在运转，这就引出了一个真实的问题：这座桥究竟是为了什么？野生动物？军事监测？还是别的什么？新加坡非常擅长建造具有多重用途的设施。也非常擅长对第二个用途保持沉默。

Wildlife on the Other Side桥的另一端：野生动物

Whatever the bridge's full purpose, this much is true: we crossed to the west side of the BKE and immediately found a freshwater terrapin in a shallow sandy stream. Dark shell, head raised just above the water, completely unbothered. Something is using this corridor. Animals are moving through. Whatever else this bridge is for — it is working for them too.

无论这座桥有什么其他用途，有一点是真的：我们越过BKE西侧，立刻在浅浅的沙质溪流里发现了一只淡水龟。深色龟壳，头微微抬出水面，毫不在意。有东西在使用这条走廊。动物在穿越。无论这座桥还有什么用途——它对它们也在发挥作用。

Freshwater terrapin found in the stream right after crossing the bridge. Proof that at least some wildlife is making it across. 越桥后立刻在溪流中发现的淡水龟。证明至少有些野生动物确实在穿越。

Three Layers of History三个历史层次

Walk this trail and you walk through 80 years of Singapore compressed into one morning. Layer 1: WWII British bunkers built in 1941-42 to defend against Japanese advance — some still standing in the undergrowth. Layer 2: SAF takes over. Rifle Range Road Camp 1 established, bollards planted, an underground ammunition depot (so the story goes) built beneath the jungle. Layer 3: Eco-Link@BKE opens in 2013 — officially for wildlife, with questions remaining about what else it serves. War. Army. Nature. Three eras, layered like sediment, in the same stretch of Singapore jungle.

走这条小径，你在一个上午穿越了新加坡80年的历史。第一层：1941至42年英国人建造的二战碉堡，用以抵御日军南下——有些至今仍矗立于丛林中。第二层：新加坡武装部队接管。靶场路一号营地建立，警示柱竖起，据说地下弹药库也建于丛林之下。第三层：2013年生态廊道@BKE开通——官方说是为野生动物，但问题依然存在：它还承担着什么？战争、军队、自然。三个时代，层叠沉积在同一片新加坡丛林之中。

Rifle Range Road Camp 1 — plain gate, no fuss. But beneath it? It is said: underground ammunition storage, invisible to satellite. The jungle hides everything. 靶场路一号营地——普通铁门，毫不显眼。但地下呢？据说：无法被卫星侦测的地下弹药库。丛林隐藏了一切。

Walking Back — What I Carry With Me走回去——我带走了什么

We turned around and walked back the way we came — past the terrapin stream, back over the bridge, under the fallen tree, through the bollards, past the bunker, out to Rifle Range Road. The same jungle, but it felt different going the other way. Quieter. We did not talk much.

我们原路返回——经过龟类溪流，再次走过桥，钻过倒树，穿过警示柱，走过碉堡，回到靶场路。同样的丛林，但回程感觉不一样了。更安静。我们话不多。

I think about this hike every time I drive past that stretch of the BKE now. From the road, all you see is a green bridge overhead with some trees on it. You drive under it in two seconds. You don't think about it. But I know what is up there — the leaf litter, the mesh fence, the fan running, the military zone, the old bunker, the terrapin in the stream. Two retired men who went to find out for themselves.

现在每次开车经过BKE那一段，我都会想起这次徒步。从路上看，你只会看到头顶一座长着树的绿色桥。两秒钟就开过去了。你不会多想。但我知道上面有什么——落叶、网围栏、运转的风扇、军事禁区、旧碉堡、溪里的龟。两个退休男人，自己去探个究竟。

Mr Po, if you are reading this — thank you for the call that morning. Good adventure.

Po先生，如果你在读这篇文章——谢谢你那天早上的那通电话。好一趟探险。

"From the road you drive past in two seconds. But up there, I found 80 years of Singapore's secrets.""从公路开车两秒就过去了。但在上面，我发现了新加坡80年的秘密。"
