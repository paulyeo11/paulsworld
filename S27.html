The Bridge That Hides Singapore's Secrets 那座桥背后的秘密

By Paul Yeo & Mr Po  ·  21 May 2022  ·  Eco-Link@BKE, Singapore 作者：Paul Yeo 与 Po先生  ·  2022年5月21日  ·  新加坡生态廊道@BKE

Most Singaporeans have driven over the BKE a thousand times. On 21 May 2022, my friend Mr Po and I didn't drive over it — we walked across it. And what we found in the jungle on either side is the kind of Singapore story that never makes the news. 大多数新加坡人已驾车穿越武吉知马快速公路（BKE）无数次。但2022年5月21日，我和好友Po先生没有开车——我们徒步走过了那座桥。就在公路旁的丛林深处，我们发现了一段被遗忘的历史。

Why This Bridge?为什么是这座桥？

The Eco-Link@BKE opened in 2013 as Singapore's first wildlife crossing bridge. Its official purpose: to let animals move safely between Bukit Timah Nature Reserve and the Central Catchment Nature Reserve, which the BKE expressway had cut in two. A noble idea. But there is more to this bridge than meets the eye.

生态廊道@BKE于2013年开放，是新加坡首座野生动物过桥。官方用途：让动物安全穿越将武吉知马自然保护区和中央集水区自然保护区一分为二的BKE快速公路。这是个崇高的理念。但这座桥的故事，远不止于此。

Mr Po knew about the trail leading to it. Not many people do. You approach from the jungle side, not from the road — and what you walk through on the way tells you immediately that this corridor has been watched over for a very long time.

Po先生知道通往那里的小径。很少人了解这条路。你从丛林侧进入，而不是从公路——沿途所见，立刻告诉你这条走廊已被守卫了很久很久。

The Military Zone军事禁区边缘

The first sign that something is different: a long row of yellow-and-black bollards stretching through the undergrowth. Then the old rusty hardware — circular rings arranged in a line beside the path, overgrown with moss, heavy with age. These are remnants of military activity along this corridor. The path runs right alongside the boundary of Rifle Range Road Camp 1.

第一个不寻常的迹象：一排黄黑色警示柱延伸至丛林深处。然后是锈迹斑斑的旧装备——一排圆形铁环沿小径排列，苔藓丛生，年代久远。这些都是这条走廊军事活动的遗迹。小径紧邻靶场路一号营地的边界。

The open field beside the camp is well-kept and quiet. A sign stands in the distance. No one is around. The camp is there — you just cannot see inside. What you sense is the silence of a place that takes its privacy seriously.

营地旁的空旷草地保养整洁，一片寂静。远处竖着一块告示牌。四周无人。营地就在那里——只是你看不到里面。你感受到的，是一个极度重视隐私的地方所特有的那种沉默。

🔒 Rifle Range Road Camp 1🔒 靶场路一号营地

A contractor who has worked inside this camp once told me: the garden inside is beautiful. Manicured, green, well-kept — you would never guess from the plain gate outside. And according to those who know such things, somewhere beneath the camp lies an underground ammunition storage facility, designed to be undetectable from the air. The jungle canopy does the work the concrete cannot — it hides what Singapore does not advertise.

一位曾在营地内施工的承包商告诉我：里面的花园非常漂亮。修剪整齐、绿意盎然——从外面那扇普通的铁门，你根本猜不到。据内情人士透露，营地某处地下设有弹药储存设施，专门设计成无法从空中探测的结构。丛林的树冠完成了混凝土无法做到的事——隐藏了新加坡不对外公布的一切。

The WWII Bunker & the Stone Cairn二战碉堡与石堆

Tucked beside the trail — easy to miss if you are not looking — is a solid concrete structure. Thick walls, a metal hatch sealed on top. Old. Very old. This is not Singapore Armed Forces infrastructure. This is British. A World War II observation post or defensive bunker, built when this part of the island was a military line against Japanese advance in 1942.

小径旁隐藏着一处混凝土建筑——若不留意很容易错过。厚实的墙壁，顶部封着铁盖。古老，非常古老。这不是新加坡武装部队的设施。这是英国人留下的。一座二战时期的观测站或防御工事，建于1942年日军推进时，用于守卫这片土地。

On top of the bunker, someone had stacked stones — flat rocks carefully balanced into a cairn. Not one person's work. This is the accumulation of many hikers, over many visits, each one adding a stone. In Buddhist tradition, stacking stones is a meditative act: a symbol of balance, a wish for good luck, a quiet prayer left in the landscape.

碉堡顶上，有人堆叠了石块——一块块扁平的石头仔细叠成石堆。这不是一个人的杰作。这是许多徒步者在无数次到访中积累的，每人添上一块石头。在佛教传统中，叠石是一种冥想行为：象征平衡，祈求好运，一个静静留在山野间的祈愿。

"A WWII bunker. A Buddhist stone cairn on top. War giving way to peace — one stone at a time." "二战碉堡。顶上的佛教石堆。战争让位于平和——一块石头，一块石头。"

The Fallen Tree倒塌的大树

Just before the bridge, the forest throws up one more obstacle. A massive tree had fallen across the path — trunk wide as a car, roots ripped clean from the earth, branches still bearing green leaves. It lay there blocking everything, unapologetic. You have to duck underneath and push through. The jungle does not make anything easy. It is testing you before the reward.

就在抵达桥前，丛林抛出最后一个障碍。一棵巨大的树横倒在小径上——树干宽如汽车，根部被连根拔起，枝桠上仍挂着绿叶。它就那样横亘着，毫不妥协。你必须弯腰钻过去。丛林不会让任何事情轻易实现。它在考验你，然后才给你奖励。

The Bridge — Two Worlds, One Span那座桥——两个世界，一道跨越

And then — you see it. The Eco-Link@BKE. An elegant hourglass-shaped span, covered in greenery, stretching across the six-lane expressway below. You step onto it and the world changes completely. The noise of the city falls away. Leaves rustle. The air smells of earth and moss. You are walking through a living corridor of forest, suspended above the BKE.

然后——你看到了它。生态廊道@BKE。一道优雅的沙漏形桥梁，覆盖着植被，横跨下方的六线快速公路。踏上它，世界完全变了。城市的喧嚣消退。树叶沙沙作响。空气中弥漫着泥土和苔藓的气息。你正穿行于一条悬浮在BKE上空的活森林走廊。

Look through the railings and you see it: the BKE below. Cars rushing. Trucks grinding through gears. Motorcycles weaving. The whole machinery of a modern city moving at full speed — completely unaware of the two men standing silently above them on a wildlife bridge, looking down.

透过栏杆往下看：BKE就在脚下。车辆疾驰，卡车换档轰鸣，摩托车穿梭其间。一座现代城市的全部机器以最高速度运转——完全不知道，两个男人正静静站在他们头顶的野生动物桥上，俯视着这一切。

"Above: silence, leaves, the smell of forest. Below: the BKE at full throttle. Two worlds, separated by nothing but the bridge beneath our feet." "上方：寂静、树叶、森林的气息。下方：全速行驶的BKE。两个世界，只被我们脚下这座桥隔开。"

Wildlife on the Other Side桥的另一端：野生动物

Cross to the west side of the BKE and the first thing we found was a freshwater terrapin in a shallow sandy stream. Just sitting there, dark shell half-submerged, head raised above the water, completely unbothered by us. It was a small thing. But it told us something important: the bridge is working. Animals are crossing. The two halves of the forest are connected again.

越过BKE西侧，我们发现的第一样东西，是一只淡水龟在浅浅的沙质溪流中。就那样待着，深色龟壳半露出水面，头抬着，对我们毫不在意。这是件小事。但它告诉我们一件重要的事：这座桥正在发挥作用。动物在穿越。两片丛林重新连接了。

Three Layers of History三个历史层次

Walk this trail and you walk through 80 years of Singapore compressed into one morning:

走这条小径，你在一个上午穿越了新加坡80年的历史：

Layer 1 — World War II: British soldiers built concrete bunkers and observation posts through this jungle corridor in 1941–42, preparing a defence line against the Japanese advance down the peninsula. Some of those bunkers are still standing today, half-hidden in the undergrowth, waiting to be found by curious retirees.

第一层——二战：1941至42年间，英国士兵在这条丛林走廊修建了混凝土碉堡和观测站，准备抵御日军南下的攻势。这些碉堡有些至今仍屹立，半隐于丛林中，等待好奇的退休人士来发现。

Layer 2 — Singapore Armed Forces: After independence, the SAF took over this corridor. Rifle Range Road Camp 1 was established. The land was fenced, the bollards were planted, and — if the stories are true — an underground ammunition depot was built beneath the jungle, invisible from above.

第二层——新加坡武装部队：独立后，新加坡武装部队接管了这条走廊。靶场路一号营地建立起来。土地被围起来，警示柱竖了起来，而且——如果传闻属实——地下弹药库被建于丛林之下，从空中无法发现。

Layer 3 — Nature returns: In 2013, the Eco-Link@BKE bridge was opened. Wildlife began to cross again. The terrapin we found on the other side is proof that something is healing. War, army, nature — three eras layered like sediment in the same stretch of Singapore jungle.

第三层——自然回归：2013年，生态廊道@BKE开通。野生动物开始重新穿越。我们在对岸发现的那只龟，证明了愈合正在发生。战争、军队、自然——三个时代，层叠沉积在新加坡同一片丛林之中。

What This Hike Taught Me这次徒步教会了我什么

Mr Po and I crossed the bridge at about 10 AM. Standing there above the BKE, I don't remember what we talked about. I just remember the feeling — looking down at all that speed and noise and hurry, and feeling very, very still inside.

Po先生和我大约上午10点走过了这座桥。站在BKE上方，我不记得我们聊了什么。我只记得那种感觉——俯视着下方的速度、喧嚣和匆忙，内心却无比平静。

Some trails take you away from the city. This one takes you above it. And somewhere between the WWII bunker, the stone cairn, and the freshwater terrapin — you find something unexpected: proof that even in the most built-up city-state on earth, nature finds a way back.

有些小径带你远离城市。这条小径带你到城市之上。在二战碉堡、石堆和淡水龟之间——你发现了意想不到的事：即使在地球上城市化程度最高的国家，自然也能找到回归的方法。

"Some secrets are worth walking to find." "有些秘密，值得你徒步前去探寻。"
