The Day I Don't Have Cash to Take Bus to DisneySea 那天我身无分文 — 没钱搭巴士去迪士尼海洋

A stalled IC card, a stranger's kindness, and a 70-year-old's first animated map, built line by line with Claude 一张失效的交通卡、一位陌生人的善意，以及一位70岁老人与Claude一起，逐行搭建的第一张动态地图

By Paul Yeo  ·  Tokyo, Japan  ·  July 2026 杨汉忠 著  ·  日本东京  ·  2026年7月

Some mornings start rough and turn into the best stories. This one started with an empty transport card, a debit card with no PIN, and an ATM that simply refused to help — and it ended with me, sitting in a Tokyo hotel room that evening, teaching an AI to draw the exact route I'd taken, train by train, minute by minute. 有些早晨开局不顺，却会变成最好的故事。这一次的开局是：一张余额见底的交通卡、一张还没设PIN码的借记卡，还有一台完全不肯配合的ATM机——而结局，是当天晚上我坐在东京酒店房间里，教一个AI逐站逐分钟，画出我当天走过的确切路线。

A $10 Problem一个十元的难题

We were up early for Tokyo DisneySea — a 6:50 AM bus from Keikyu-Kamata Station, planned the night before down to the minute. Then, at the gate, my IC card came up short. Not a big deal, I thought — I'd just tap my DBS Visa or draw cash from the ATM. 我们一大早就出发前往东京迪士尼海洋——前一晚就精确计划好，早上6点50分从京急蒲田站搭巴士。可到了闸口，我的交通卡余额不够。我心想没关系，刷一下DBS Visa卡，或者去ATM取点现金就行了。

Except the Visa card's PIN had never been set up. And the ATM, for reasons known only to itself, would not release a single yen. My backup card was sitting safely back home — which did me no good at all in Kamata. I needed exactly ten Singapore dollars to get on a bus, and for a moment, none of my cards could produce it. 可这张Visa卡从来没有设置过PIN码。而那台ATM机，出于只有它自己知道的原因，一分钱也不肯吐出来。备用卡安安稳稳地留在家里——但这对身处蒲田的我毫无帮助。我只差新币十元就能上车，而那一刻，我手上的卡一张都派不上用场。

🙏 The Stranger Who Said Yes那位说好的陌生人

I don't know his name. I probably never will. But somewhere in that queue, a man saw a 70-year-old fumbling with three dead cards and simply handed over the fare — no hesitation, no fuss, like it was the most ordinary thing in the world. 我不知道他的名字，大概也永远不会知道。但在那条队伍里，一位陌生人看见一个70岁的老人手忙脚乱地摆弄着三张失效的卡，二话不说就替我付了车费——毫不犹豫，毫不张扬，仿佛这是世界上再平常不过的事。

It's a strange thing, needing help for something so small. Sixty-eight years old and I've crossed mountain passes, dived with sharks, paraglided off cliffs — and here I was, stuck by ten dollars and a queue of strangers. But that's the thing about travel: it has a way of humbling you exactly when you think you've got it all figured out. And it has an equally sure way of reminding you that most people, most of the time, are kind. 因为一件这么小的事需要求助，感觉很奇妙。我曾翻越高山垭口，与鲨鱼同游，从悬崖边一跃而下滑翔——而此刻，我却被十块钱和一队陌生人困住了。但旅行就是这样：总能在你以为自己已经什么都搞定的时候，给你上一课，让你谦卑下来。而它同样确定无疑地提醒你——大多数人，在大多数时候，都是善良的。

— Paul Yeo, 4 July 2026, somewhere in the queue for Tokyo DisneySea杨汉忠，2026年7月4日，写于东京迪士尼海洋的排队人群中

From Near-Miss to a Map That Moves从有惊无险到会动的地图

Two days later, on our travel day from Tokyo to Sapporo, I found myself staring at another Google Maps transit screen — this time a proper journey: Kamata to Shinagawa, Shinagawa to Sengakuji, Sengakuji all the way out to Oshiage by Tokyo Skytree, then the long haul to Narita Airport. One hour, fifty-four minutes, five names, one screenshot. 两天后，在我们从东京前往札幌的交通日，我又一次盯着谷歌地图上的换乘方案——这次是一段正经的旅程：从蒲田到品川，品川到泉岳寺，泉岳寺一路转到东京晴空塔附近的押上站，再一路奔向成田机场。一小时五十四分钟，五个地名，一张截图。

A screenshot is fine. But I wanted to see it move — to actually watch the journey unfold on a map, the way I imagine it unfolding in real life. So I described the whole route to Claude, gave it the real departure and arrival times from my transit app, and asked for an animated map: a little train icon travelling the exact path, at roughly the right pace, looping so I could watch it as many times as I liked. 一张截图当然够用。但我想看它动起来——真正在地图上看着这段旅程展开，就像我想象中它在现实中展开的样子。于是我把整条路线描述给Claude听，把交通App里真实的出发和到达时间都给了它，然后请它画一张动态地图：一个小小的火车图标沿着确切的路径行进，速度大致贴近实际时间，并且循环播放，让我想看几遍就看几遍。

What came back, a few minutes later, genuinely surprised me. A dark, clean map. A little 🚆 icon gliding from a green dot at my hotel, through orange stops at Shinagawa and Sengakuji, past Skytree, and finally to a red dot at Narita — timed almost exactly to the real schedule. I asked for the title to say "Hotel to Airport." Done. I asked for it to auto-replay instead of needing a button press. Done, in under a minute. 几分钟后收到的成果，真的让我很惊讶。一张干净的深色地图，一个小小的🚆图标从我酒店所在的绿点出发，途经品川和泉岳寺的橙色站点，路过晴空塔，最终抵达成田的红点——时间几乎完全贴合真实的时刻表。我说标题要改成"Hotel to Airport"，做到了。我说要自动循环播放，不用按按钮，不到一分钟也做到了。

Then I asked for one more thing — this exact standalone version, as its own real page on my own website, not just something I could see in a chat window. And a little while later, there it was: MT10-day6-map.html, live on paulsworld.vercel.app, with my Home button and my view counter, built the same way every other page on this site gets built — by describing what I wanted, in plain English, to an AI that did the coding. 接着我又提了一个要求——把这个独立版本原封不动地做成我自己网站上真正的一个页面，而不只是聊天窗口里能看到的东西。没过多久，它就出现了：MT10-day6-map.html，实实在在地上线在paulsworld.vercel.app上，带着我的主页按钮和浏览次数计数器，用和这个网站上其他每一个页面一样的方式建成——用大白话把我想要的东西说给一个负责写代码的AI听。

What I Took Away From This Trip这趟旅程给我的启发

  • ①Set up your PINs before you fly. A card is useless if you can't actually use it — test everything at home, not at a foreign turnstile.出发前先设置好PIN码。一张用不了的卡毫无意义——一定要在家里就测试好，别等到国外的闸机前才发现。
  • ②Carry a real backup, on you, always. Not in a drawer at home — in your bag, every single day.身上永远带一张真正的备用卡。不是放在家里的抽屉里，而是每天都揣在自己的包里。
  • ③Let strangers be kind to you. Accepting help isn't weakness. It's what makes room for you to return the favour to someone else, someday.坦然接受陌生人的善意。接受帮助不是软弱，它只是为你日后回馈他人留出了空间。
  • ④You don't need to know how to code to build something. At 70, I can now describe an idea in plain words and watch it appear as a working, animated map on my own website. That still amazes me every time.不会写代码，也能亲手创造点什么。七十岁的我，如今只需用大白话描述一个想法，就能看着它变成自己网站上一张真实可用、会动的地图。每一次，我依然感到惊叹。

If you're heading through Tokyo yourself, keep your IC card topped up, your PINs set, and your eyes open for the kindness that tends to show up exactly when you need it most. 如果你也即将途经东京，记得给交通卡充值、设好PIN码，并留意那些总在你最需要的时候悄然出现的善意。
