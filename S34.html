The Day I Don't Have Cash to Take Bus to DisneySea 那天我身无分文 — 没钱搭巴士去迪士尼海洋

A stalled IC card, a stranger's kindness, and a 70-year-old's first animated map, built line by line with Claude 一张失效的交通卡、一位陌生人的善意，以及一位70岁老人与Claude一起，逐行搭建的第一张动态地图

By Paul Yeo  ·  Tokyo, Japan  ·  July 2026 杨汉忠 著  ·  日本东京  ·  2026年7月

✅ Link copied!链接已复制！

Some mornings start rough and turn into the best stories. On this Japan trip, my wife handles our daily transport money — the buses, the trains, and normally a small string pouch of cash for me too. That morning, heading to Tokyo DisneySea together, I forgot two things at once: my physical Trust card, left at the hotel, and that same pouch, still sitting on the dresser. 有些早晨开局不顺，却会变成最好的故事。这趟日本之旅，日常的交通费一直是妻子在打理——巴士、电车的钱，还有平时装在一个小布袋里给我的现金。那天早上，我们一起前往东京迪士尼海洋，我却一口气忘了两样东西：留在酒店的实体Trust卡，还有同样留在梳妆台上的那个小布袋。

The Bus That Wouldn't Take My Card那辆不肯收卡的巴士

I didn't think much of it at first — we'd already boarded the train and were halfway there. All that was left was one bus ride to actually reach DisneySea. Except my IC card didn't have enough balance for the fare, and without the Trust card in hand, the ATM wouldn't give me a single yen either. 一开始我并没太在意——我们已经上了电车，走了半程。剩下的，只是转乘一趟巴士就能抵达迪士尼海洋。可我的交通卡余额不够付车费，而手上没有Trust卡，ATM机也一分钱都不肯吐给我。

I was sure the bus took Visa, so I wasn't worried — until a lady standing at the bus stop told me, quite firmly, that this particular bus only accepted cash. No card, no exceptions. Suddenly I needed real yen in my hand, and I had none. 我一直以为这班巴士能刷Visa卡，所以并不担心——直到站在巴士站的一位女士很肯定地告诉我，这班车只收现金，没有例外。突然间我需要的是手里实实在在的日元，而我一分现金都没有。

🙏 The Man Who Tried to Help那位试着帮忙的人

So I ran — from the bus stop to the nearest 7-Eleven and back, hunting for any way to turn a card into cash. Along the way, a stranger noticed a 70-year-old going back and forth and simply topped up ¥1,000 onto my IC card, unasked. It was a genuinely kind thing to do — except even with his ¥1,000, my card still wasn't full enough for the fare. I was grateful, and still stuck. 于是我跑了起来——从巴士站跑到最近的7-11，又跑回来，想尽办法把卡变成现金。途中，一位陌生人注意到一个70岁的老人来回奔波，二话不说就往我的交通卡里充了1000日元。这份好意是真心的——只是就算加上这1000日元，我卡里的余额还是不够付车费。我心存感激，却依旧困在原地。

I tried a different approach — offering to pay a few people with my credit card if they'd hand me the equivalent in cash. It didn't work. Most people I approached couldn't understand what I was asking, and I couldn't explain it in Japanese. That's when it really sank in: relying on someone else to carry your money for you is fine, right up until the one day they're not standing next to you. 我换了个办法——想找几个人，用信用卡付款给他们，换取等值现金。没有成功。我找的几个人大多听不懂我在说什么，而我又没办法用日语解释清楚。就是在那一刻，我才真正体会到：依赖别人替你带钱，平时没问题，可一旦那天他们不在你身边，麻烦就来了。

— Paul Yeo, 4 July 2026, a bus stop somewhere on the way to Tokyo DisneySea杨汉忠，2026年7月4日，写于前往东京迪士尼海洋路上的一个巴士站

From Near-Miss to a Map That Moves从有惊无险到会动的地图

Two days later, on our travel day from Tokyo to Sapporo, I found myself staring at another Google Maps transit screen — this time a proper journey: Kamata to Shinagawa, Shinagawa to Sengakuji, Sengakuji all the way out to Oshiage by Tokyo Skytree, then the long haul to Narita Airport. One hour, fifty-four minutes, five names, one screenshot. 两天后，在我们从东京前往札幌的交通日，我又一次盯着谷歌地图上的换乘方案——这次是一段正经的旅程：从蒲田到品川，品川到泉岳寺，泉岳寺一路转到东京晴空塔附近的押上站，再一路奔向成田机场。一小时五十四分钟，五个地名，一张截图。

A screenshot is fine. But I wanted to see it move — to actually watch the journey unfold on a map, the way I imagine it unfolding in real life. So I described the whole route to Claude, gave it the real departure and arrival times from my transit app, and asked for an animated map: a little train icon travelling the exact path, at roughly the right pace, looping so I could watch it as many times as I liked. 一张截图当然够用。但我想看它动起来——真正在地图上看着这段旅程展开，就像我想象中它在现实中展开的样子。于是我把整条路线描述给Claude听，把交通App里真实的出发和到达时间都给了它，然后请它画一张动态地图：一个小小的火车图标沿着确切的路径行进，速度大致贴近实际时间，并且循环播放，让我想看几遍就看几遍。

What came back, a few minutes later, genuinely surprised me. A dark, clean map. A little 🚆 icon gliding from a green dot at my hotel, through orange stops at Shinagawa and Sengakuji, past Skytree, and finally to a red dot at Narita — timed almost exactly to the real schedule. I asked for the title to say "Hotel to Airport." Done. I asked for it to auto-replay instead of needing a button press. Done, in under a minute. 几分钟后收到的成果，真的让我很惊讶。一张干净的深色地图，一个小小的🚆图标从我酒店所在的绿点出发，途经品川和泉岳寺的橙色站点，路过晴空塔，最终抵达成田的红点——时间几乎完全贴合真实的时刻表。我说标题要改成"Hotel to Airport"，做到了。我说要自动循环播放，不用按按钮，不到一分钟也做到了。

Then I asked for one more thing — this exact standalone version, as its own real page on my own website, not just something I could see in a chat window. And a little while later, there it was: MT10-day6-map.html, live on paulsworld.netlify.app, with my Home button and my view counter, built the same way every other page on this site gets built — by describing what I wanted, in plain English, to an AI that did the coding. 接着我又提了一个要求——把这个独立版本原封不动地做成我自己网站上真正的一个页面，而不只是聊天窗口里能看到的东西。没过多久，它就出现了：MT10-day6-map.html，实实在在地上线在paulsworld.netlify.app上，带着我的主页按钮和浏览次数计数器，用和这个网站上其他每一个页面一样的方式建成——用大白话把我想要的东西说给一个负责写代码的AI听。

What I Took Away From This Trip这趟旅程给我的启发

  • ①Always carry your own cash. Even when your travel partner handles the daily money, keep some on your own person — the one day you're separated is the day you'll need it.身上永远要带自己的现金。就算平时都是同伴在管钱，自己身上也要留一点——万一哪天两人分开，那正是你最需要它的时候。
  • ②Bring your backup card, every day. A card sitting safely at the hotel does nothing for you at a bus stop across town.备用卡每天都要带在身上。安安稳稳留在酒店房间里的卡，在城市另一头的巴士站帮不了你。
  • ③Don't count on English, or on strangers, off the tourist track. Kindness can still find you — a stranger topped up my card without being asked — but the language gap means you can't rely on getting understood, let alone rescued.离开游客区，别指望英语，也别指望陌生人。善意依然会出现——一位陌生人二话不说就替我的卡充了值——但语言的隔阂意味着，你很难指望自己能被理解，更别说被"救"了。
  • ④You don't need to know how to code to build something. At 70, I can now describe an idea in plain words and watch it appear as a working, animated map on my own website. That still amazes me every time.不会写代码，也能亲手创造点什么。七十岁的我，如今只需用大白话描述一个想法，就能看着它变成自己网站上一张真实可用、会动的地图。每一次，我依然感到惊叹。

If you're heading through Tokyo yourself, keep your IC card topped up, your PINs set, and your eyes open for the kindness that tends to show up exactly when you need it most. 如果你也即将途经东京，记得给交通卡充值、设好PIN码，并留意那些总在你最需要的时候悄然出现的善意。
