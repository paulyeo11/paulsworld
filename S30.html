The Drongo Trail
Where Butterflies Dance and Fungi Grow

卷尾鸟小径
蝴蝶起舞、真菌生长的地方

Not every great hike is long. Sometimes 1.1 kilometres is enough — if you walk slowly, keep your eyes low, and let the forest show you what it wants to show.

并非每一次伟大的徒步都需要很长的距离。有时候，1.1公里就足够了——只要你走得慢一点，眼睛看得低一点，让森林向你展示它想展示的东西。

A Short Trail with Big Surprises短短小径，大大惊喜

The Drongo Trail is one of those trails that most people walk past. It is not famous. There are no dramatic viewpoints, no summit, no Instagram-worthy vista. It is 1.1 kilometres of secondary forest in Bukit Timah Nature Reserve — quiet, green, and full of life if you know how to look.

卷尾鸟小径是那种大多数人都会直接走过的步道。它并不出名——没有壮观的观景台，没有山顶，没有适合拍照发网络的美景。它只是武吉知马自然保护区里1.1公里的次生林——宁静、翠绿，只要你懂得观察，就会发现这里充满了生机。

I arrived at the trailhead this morning and was greeted immediately by the usual NParks signs: Hiking Only, No Biking, Caution — Wild Boars in the Area. That last one always makes me smile. Wild boars have been here since long before the park signs. We are the visitors. They are the residents.

今早我抵达步道入口，迎接我的是NParks一贯的标牌：仅限徒步、禁止骑车、注意——区内有野猪出没。最后那块牌子每次都让我忍不住微笑。野猪在这里生活的时间，远比那些标牌立起来的时间长得多。我们是访客，它们才是这里的居民。

The Wild Boar Warning野猪警告

The sign at the trailhead is detailed — it tells you exactly what to do if you meet a wild boar. Do not run. Do not approach. Stand still. Move away slowly. Back against a tree if you can. I have read it many times over the years. I have never actually met a wild boar on a trail, but knowing the protocol makes me feel prepared, not afraid.

入口处的标牌内容详尽——它清楚地告诉你，如果遇上野猪该怎么办。不要跑，不要靠近，站着不动，慢慢后退，如果可以就背靠着一棵树。多年来我读过这块牌子很多次。我在步道上从未真正遇见过野猪，但了解应对方法让我感到的是从容，而不是恐惧。

What I do meet on trails — almost every time — is something quieter, smaller, and just as wonderful: butterflies.

而我在步道上几乎每次都会遇到的，是某种更安静、更微小、却同样美好的存在——蝴蝶。

Three Butterflies and a Moth三只蝴蝶和一只蛾

I saw my first butterfly even before fully entering the trail. Near the trailhead, a patch of orange cosmos flowers had attracted a vivid orange visitor — the Tawny Coster (Acraea terpsicore), wings spread wide and burning bright against the warm colours of the blooms. It fed without urgency, moving from flower to flower with a kind of deliberate slowness that I found myself matching, slowing my own steps to watch.

甚至还没完全进入步道，我就看见了第一只蝴蝶。在入口附近，一丛橙色波斯菊吸引了一位鲜艳的橙色访客——豹斑蝶（Acraea terpsicore），翅膀展开，在花朵的暖色中燃烧般耀眼。它不急不忙地觅食，从一朵花飞到另一朵花，那种从容的慢让我不由自主地放慢了自己的脚步，静静观看。

Further along, where purple Stachytarpheta flowers cascaded down a mossy tree trunk, two more butterflies were feeding. A larger brown-and-grey species — probably a Chocolate Pansy or a Grey Pansy — flickered from bloom to bloom. And then, on a lower leaf nearby, something smaller and more dramatic: a tiny moth with dark wings marked in bold orange patches, sitting perfectly still in the dappled light.

再往前走，在一棵长满青苔的树干上，紫色马鞭草层叠而下，又有两只蝴蝶在那里觅食。一只较大的棕灰色蝴蝶——可能是巧克力翠蛱蝶或灰翠蛱蝶——在花丛间飞舞。然后，在附近一片较低的叶子上，我发现了更小却更惹眼的存在：一只小蛾，深色翅膀上有醒目的橙色斑纹，在斑驳的光影中静静停着，一动不动。

Four species. One trail. One morning. This is why I walk slowly.

四个物种。一条步道。一个早晨。这就是我为什么总走得慢的原因。

"Most people walk a 1.1km trail in twenty minutes. I was there for over an hour — and I still felt I had not seen enough."

"大多数人走完1.1公里的步道只需二十分钟。我在那里待了一个多小时——却仍然觉得没看够。"

The Fallen Log and its Tenants倒木与它的住户

Deeper inside the trail, where the canopy thickened and the light turned green and soft, I found one of my favourite kinds of forest discoveries: a fallen log completely colonised by fungi. White cup-shaped brackets, dozens of them, arranged in neat rows across the rotting bark — some as small as a fingernail, some as wide as a palm. Each one a perfect little cup, the kind you might expect to find in a fairy tale.

步道深处，树冠越来越密，光线变得柔和而带着绿意，我发现了自己最喜欢的那种森林景象：一根倒下的木头，整个被真菌占据了。白色的杯状菌盖，密密麻麻，整齐地排列在腐烂的树皮上——有的小如指甲，有的宽如手掌。每一个都是一只完美的小杯，像是在童话故事里才会出现的模样。

Fungi like these are the forest's recyclers. This log — which might have been a living tree just a few years ago — is now home to dozens of organisms, slowly being broken down and returned to the soil that once fed its roots. The forest does not waste anything. Everything that falls becomes something else.

这样的真菌是森林的回收者。这根倒木——也许几年前还是一棵活树——如今已成为数十种生物的家园，正在被慢慢分解，重新归还给当年滋养它根系的土壤。森林从不浪费任何东西。所有倒下的，都会变成另一种存在。

I crouched down and photographed the fungi from below, looking up at the rows of cups against the light filtering through the canopy above. It is one of those moments that you cannot plan — you just have to show up, and look, and be willing to get your knees dirty for the shot.

我蹲下身来，从下方仰拍这些真菌——透过上方树冠筛落的光线，看着那一排排小杯子。这种时刻是无法计划的，你只需要出现在那里，仔细看，并愿意为了那张照片弄脏自己的膝盖。

What Short Trails Teach You短途步道教会你的事

I used to think that a worthwhile hike needed to be long. Annapurna Base Camp. Torres del Paine. Eight-hour summit days with a full pack. Those experiences are real and I treasure them. But I have learned — especially in retirement, especially in Singapore — that the short trails teach you something different.

我曾经以为，值得一走的徒步必须很长。安纳普尔纳大本营、托雷斯德尔潘内、背着全套装备的八小时登顶。那些经历是真实的，我也珍视它们。但我学会了——尤其是退休之后，尤其是在新加坡——短途步道教给你的是不同的东西。

A long trail tests endurance. A short trail tests attention. When you only have 1.1 kilometres, every tree matters. Every insect. Every patch of fungi on every fallen log. You cannot walk fast — there is nothing gained by being first to the end. The whole point is the middle.

长途步道考验的是耐力。短途步道考验的是专注力。当你只有1.1公里，每一棵树都重要。每一只昆虫。每一根倒木上的每一片真菌。你不能走快——最先到达终点没有任何意义。重点恰恰是中间的过程。

"The Drongo Trail does not reward speed. It rewards slowness. And slowness, I have found, is one of retirement's greatest gifts."

"卷尾鸟小径不奖励速度，它奖励慢。而慢，我发现，是退休生活最珍贵的礼物之一。"

I am 70 years old, retired, and this morning I spent over an hour on 1.1 kilometres of forest trail in Singapore. I crouched down to photograph fungi. I stood still for ten minutes watching a moth. I walked home with dirty knees and a full heart.

我七十岁，已退休。今天早上，我在新加坡1.1公里的森林步道上走了一个多小时。我蹲下来拍摄真菌。我站着一动不动，看了一只蛾整整十分钟。我带着脏了的膝盖和满满的心意走回家。

That is a good morning. That is exactly the morning I wanted.

这是一个美好的早晨。正是我想要的那种早晨。
